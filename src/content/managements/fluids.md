# Fluids

## Types of fluids

### Crystalloid

- **0.9% normal saline**
  - Risk of hyperchloremic metabolic acidosis with normal anion gap
- **Hartmann's solution / Lactated Ringer's solution**
  - Hypotonic
  - Lactate requires liver metabolism
  - Avoid in
    - Hyperkalemia
    - Brain injury
    - Blood transfusion
- **Plasmalyte**
  - Avoid in
    - Hyperkalemia
    - Brain injury
  - Compatible with blood products
- **5% Dextrose**
  - Hypotonic
  - Provides 170 kcal/L
- **Half-half**
  - Hypotonic
  - Less glucose fluctuations than alternating dextrose/normal saline

### Colloid

- **Albumin / Gelofusin**
  - Risk of anaphylaxis
  - Risk of bleeding from reduced platelet aggregation

## Fluid replacement

| Nutrient  | Daily requirement |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Water     | 30-40 ml/kg       |
| Sodium    | 2 mmol/kg         |
| Potassium | 1 mmol/kg         |
| Calcium   | 0.1-0.2 mmol/kg   |
| Magnesium | 0.1-0.3 mmol/kg   |
| Phosphate | 0.4 mmol/kg       |

| % deficit | Sensorium | Mucus membrane | Skin turgor  | Eyes   | BP     | Pulse  | Urine     |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|--------------|--------|--------|--------|-----------|
| 3-5%      | Normal    | Dry            | Normal       | Normal | Normal | Normal | Normal    |
| 6-10%     | Lethargic | Dry            | Decreased    | Sunken | Normal | High   | Decreased |
| 10-15%    | Semicoma  | Parched        | Decreased    | Sunken | Low    | High   | Decreased |
| >15%      | Coma      | Parched        | Cold and dry | Sunken | Low    | Weak   | Absent    |

### Resuscitation

- Replace fluid deficit
  - `kg weight * % deficit * 10` in ml

### Rehydration

1. Determine total daily fluid volume
   - Fluid maintenance
     - 4-2-1 rule

     | Weight   | Rate         |
     |----------|--------------|
     | 0-10 kg  | 4 ml/kg/hour |
     | 10-20 kg | 2 ml/kg/hour |
     | >20 kg   | 1 ml/kg/hour |

     - 100-50-20 rule

     | Weight   | Rate          |
     |----------|---------------|
     | 0-10 kg  | 100 ml/kg/day |
     | 10-20 kg | 50 ml/kg/day  |
     | >20 kg   | 20 ml/kg/day  |

   - Replace deficit
     - `kg weight * % deficit * 10` in ml
   - Cover ongoing loss
2. Prescribe normal saline to cover daily sodium requirement
   - Usually 1L normal saline
3. Fulfil remaining requirement with 5% dextrose
4. Add potassium as needed
   - Not for short fasting <48 hours
   - Replace if ongoing loss or serum potassium on low side
   - Replace with 10-20 mmol KCl per 500 ml fluids
   - **Do not administer KCl bolus**
5. Replace 50% of total volume in first 8 hours
   - Replace remaining 50% in subsequent 16 hours

## Specific indications

### Cardiogenic shock

- 100 ml crystalloid bolus

> Avoid fluids in pulmonary edema

### Hypovolemic shock

- Small volume blood loss (<1 L)
  - Colloid replacement 1-1.5 times the volume lost
    - 5% albumin
    - Gelatin
    - Hetastarch
    - **Side effects** - renal dysfunction, coagulopathy
  - Crystalloid replacement 1.5-2 times the volume lost
    - Lactated Ringer's solution
    - Plasma-Lyte A
- Large volume blood loss
  - Consider blood transfusion

### Septic shock

- 30-by-3 crystalloid - 30ml/kg crystalloid over 3 hours
  - Assess response with mental state and urine output
  - Consider 30-by-6 for patients with congestive heart failure
  - **Disregard fluid in-out balance in the first 24 hours**
