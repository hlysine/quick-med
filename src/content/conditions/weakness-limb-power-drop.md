# Weakness / Limb Power Drop

## Causes
* **Brain: HI, CVA (ischaemic/haemorrhagic), SOL (haematoma/abscess/tumour)**, encephalitis, demyelination, seizure/post-ictal (Todd's paresis)
* **Spinal cord: trauma, CVA, myelopathy** (haematoma/abscess/tumour/spondylosis/OPLL), transverse myelitis (demyelination, autoimmune diseases, infection, ADEM, paraneoplastic), B12 deficiency
* Anterior horn cell: MND, SMA
* Nerve root: cauda equina, radiculopathy (PID, tumour etc.)
* Neuropathy: GBS, CIDP, **DM, alcoholic**, autoimmune, **paraneoplastic**, MM, lymphoma, HIV, Lyme disease, lead/mercury/organophosphate poisoning, porphyria, CMT, nerve entrapment/trauma
* NMJ: **MG**, LEMS, Botulism, organophosphate poisoning
* Myopathy: statin, Cushing, hypothyroid, autoimmune myositis, dystrophies, periodic paralysis

> New onset weakness is stroke/cord compression until proven otherwise!
> Subacute/chronic limb weakness is not urgent and can leave for MO round

## Assessment
* **Vitals, GCS**
* **Onset (need exact last seen well time for stroke!)**, trigger (fatigue, exercise, meal)
* **Pattern:** myotomal (nerve root) vs nerve distribution (neuropathy) vs single limb vs hemiparesis vs paraparesis vs tetraparesis; proximal (NMJ, myopathy) vs distal (neuropathy) vs pyramidal (UMNL)
* **Associated symptoms:** headache/**slurring/facial weakness** (brain), sensory loss (UMNL/neuropathy), sphincter disturbance (brain/cord/nerve root), fatiguability (NMJ), myalgia (myopathy)
* **Full neuro exam:** CN (pupils, EOM, CN7, dysarthria), 4 limbs **power/reflex/sensation**, cerebellar signs
    * *Affecting bulbar/respiratory muscles: may need swallowing assessment/ventilatory support*
    * *Hyperreflexia/clonus/hypertonia suggests UMNL*
* If suspect **cord pathology:** spinal tenderness, sensory level, **PR** for tone, grip, perianal sensation
* If **no weakness** on testing: consider MG (test for **fatiguability**), fibromyalgia, sarcopenia

## Management
* New/acutely worsened weakness: neuro obs Q1H x4 then Q4H if stable
* If **respiratory fatigue:** bedside spirometry Q4H, early ventilation if FVC <15ml/kg
* If **bulbar S/S (dysarthria/dysphagia):** NPOEM, consult speech therapist x swallowing assessment
* Acute weakness localised to **brain: urgent CT brain, activate stroke call / urgent consult NS**
* Acute weakness localised to **spinal cord: urgent MRI C/T/LS spine** (according to level of clinical localisation) **+ whole spine sagittal screening, urgent consult NS**
* Consult neuro if no known cause (may need further testing e.g. MRI brain/spine, NCS, RNS, Tensilon test, EMG, CK, LDH, aldolase, AST, ALT, autoimmune markers, nerve/muscle biopsy)

> **Pitfall:** in suspected stroke or neurosurgical emergency, it is important to **simultaneously** activate stroke call / urgent consult NS while waiting for CTB (rather than wait for CTB first), because:
> * Even urgent CTB can take over half a day to queue!! But activating stroke protocol speeds it up
> * Earlier informing allows earlier preparation for tPA / IR thrombectomy / OT – time is brain!
