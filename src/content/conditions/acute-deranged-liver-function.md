# Acute Deranged Liver Function

## Causes
* **Hepatitic pattern (increased ALT):** hepatitis A/E/B/C, alcohol, drug-induced/TCM, haemochromatosis, autoimmune, HCC
* **Cholestatic pattern (increased ALP):** cholangitis, biliary obstruction (stones/malignancy/stricture e.g. PSC, PBC)
* Ddx for increased ALT: myositis
* Ddx for increased ALP: bone conditions, pregnancy
* Ddx for increased bili: haemolysis

## Complications
* Synthetic dysfunction: coagulopathy, hypoalbuminaemia
* Metabolic dysfunction: hypoglycaemia, HE

## Assessment
* **Clinical history:** fever, TOCC, alcohol intake, drug intake including TCM, Hx of CLD
* **Physical examination**
    * Vitals (especially **fever**), H'stix and **GCS**
    * I/O (ensure BO if history of chronic liver disease)
    * Jaundice (including tea colour urine in BSB)
    * Stigmata of chronic liver disease (if acute on chronic liver failure)
    * **RUQ mass / hepatomegaly**
    * **Ascites, LN**

## Management
* **Bloods** x AST, GGT, heat stable ALP, conjugated bili, clotting, NH3, RG, HBsAg, anti-HCV, anti-HAV IgM, anti-HEV IgM, iron profile, serum toxicology, alcohol level, Panadol level (if indicated), AFP, CEA (if suspect malignancy)
    * **AST:ALT >2:1 and GGT increased suggests alcoholic liver disease**
    * Heat stable ALP showing non-bone origin, **GGT increased, conjugated bili increased suggest cholestasis**
* Urine toxicology (if indicated)
* **Off TB drugs, Panadol (or change to PRN or reduce dose), statins**
    * *Actually use of Panadol less than 2g/day is not associated with acute decompensation*
* **If fever/signs of sepsis + dLFT: blood culture, urgent USG hepatobiliary system; urgent consult SUR if +ve findings**
* **Thiamine** if alcoholic
* **Vit K** if clotting derangement
* Monitor LFT, clotting, NH3, RG
* Consult MED if unexplained and persistent
