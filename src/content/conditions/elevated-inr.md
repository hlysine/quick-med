## Approach to Abnormal INR

- Check if patient is on **warfarin**:
  - Target INR 2–3; or 2.5–3.5 if mechanical mitral valve
  - If **NOT** on warfarin and grossly deranged (>8.0): consider lab error/haemolysed sample — urgent recheck clotting profile
- Check **liver enzymes**: liver disease causes elevated INR
- Any recent **new drugs**: drug-drug interactions with warfarin (CYP inhibitors); antibiotics may prolong INR (e.g. Sulperazon)

### Common CYP Inhibitors (raise INR on warfarin)
Erythromycin, ketoconazole, cimetidine, amiodarone, clarithromycin, verapamil, diltiazem, grapefruit juice

---

## Assessment

- Vitals
- Look for possible bleeding source
- Repeat **CBC** (Hb drop) + clotting ± LFT/mixing study if unknown cause
- No IMI / arterial puncture (e.g. femoral blood)

---

## Management

- W/H antiplatelets and anticoagulants including warfarin (if beyond INR target)
- **Do NOT** stop DAPT for ACS without MO
- If **NOT on warfarin**, no bleeding: monitor INR; consider PO/IV vitamin K1
- If INR >1.5, **NOT on warfarin**, active bleeding: transfuse **4 units FFP full rate**

### If on Warfarin — Reversal Indications

| Situation | Action |
|---|---|
| INR <4.5, no bleeding | Reduce dose or omit next few doses; daily INR monitoring |
| INR 4.5–8, no bleeding, no risk factors | Omit next few doses (daily INR) |
| INR 4.5–8, no bleeding, with risk factors | Low dose oral vitamin K **1.0–2.5 mg** |
| INR ≥8, no bleeding, no risk factors | **3.0–3.5 mg** oral vitamin K |
| INR ≥8, no bleeding, with risk factors | **PCC 15–30 IU/kg** |
| INR ≥2.0 with significant bleeding | Vitamin K **5–10 mg IV** or PCC **35–50 IU/kg** (plasma 15 ml/kg if PCC unavailable) |
| Any INR with **life-threatening bleeding** | FFP 2 units IV rapid infusion → check INR 15 min after → if INR ≥1.5 give another 2 units, repeat until INR ≤1.5; vitamin K **10 mg IV** over 10–20 min; withhold warfarin |

---

## Key Principles

- Avoid large dose vitamin K (long half-life, difficult to re-titrate warfarin; give **1 mg by 1 mg** if IV)
- **PCC preferred over FFP** when rapid reversal needed or risk of fluid overload (e.g. renal failure)
- **Beriplex** = 4-factor PCC (factors II, VII, IX, X)
