# Haematuria

> **Gross (red/pink urine) or microscopic (urine m'stix RBC +ve)**

## Causes
* Recent instrumentation (e.g. foley insertion/ FC)
* UTI
* Urinary stones
* Urinary tract malignancy
* Bleeding BPH
* Nephritic syndrome (microscopic haematuria + proteinuria)
* Systemic: antiplatelets/anticoagulants, coagulopathy

> **Light red / pink** = light haematuria
> **Dark red** may be "old" haematuria
> **Bright red with clots** is most worrying

## False positives
* Food dye (beetroot) or drug staining (pyridium, nitrofurantoin, rifampicin, phenytoin)
* Menstruation
* Myoglobinuria (rhabdomyolysis)

## Complications
* Clot retention (AROU / blocked foley)
* Anaemia

## Assessment
* Severity of bleeding (vitals/ Hb drop/ presence of **blood clots**)
* **Fever and vitals**
* Urinary symptoms: especially **symptoms of stone** (loin pain/ renal colic/ passage of stone) or **clot retention** (LUTS, AROU)
* **Abdominal exam, costovertebral tenderness**
* **PR exam** for size and contour of prostate – median sulcus intact? any nodules?
* Background history: any previous urological disease/workup, antiplatelet/anticoagulant

## Management for Gross Haematuria
* If it's only mild haematuria after foley insertion, can keep obs and only workup if persistent!
* Chart I/O, haematuria
* **Bld x CBC d/c, RFT, clotting;** transfuse as needed
* **MSU/CSU x m'stix, c/st, cytology**
* **KUB x1**
* **PVRU x1**
* **If PVRU >350ml or blood clots +:**
    * **3-way haematuria catheter (i.e. 3-way 24Fr foley) to BSB**
    * Blocked washout +/- bladder irrigation full rate (after washout)
* **W/H antiplatelets** (consult if DAPT for ACS), **W/H +/- reverse anticoagulation**
* Consult URO (may need FC + CTU/USG for workup if no known cause)

> **Pitfall:** DO NOT prescribe Transamin for haematuria!!! It will precipitate clot retention!

> **Tips for bladder washout:**
> * Insert a **3-way haematuria catheter (i.e. 3-way 24Fr foley)**
> * Flush with sterile NS using bladder syringe (50ml) – **flush must be rapid to dislodge clots**
> * **Ensure outflow volume >= instilled volume**
> * Rotate the catheter tip so you wash the entire bladder rather than just the anterior wall
> * Wash till light haematuria only or clear; if not clear, then order bladder irrigation

## Management for Microscopic Haematuria
* Chart I/O, haematuria
* Bld x CBC d/c, RFT, clotting
* MSU/CSU x m'stix, c/st, microscopy (for dysmorphic RBC, casts)
* Consider consult/refer MED if not explained by UTI
