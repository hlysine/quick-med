## ECG Changes

- Peaked T waves
- Widened QRS
- Sine wave pattern (severe)

---

## General Measures (All Grades)

- Check for underlying cause
- **Recheck RFT** if haemolysed sample (haemolysis falsely elevates K by ~1 mmol/L)
- Withhold: **K supplements** (check IVF!), **ACEI**, **ARB**, **MRA**, **BB**, **NSAIDs**
- Look out for: tumour lysis syndrome, acute renal failure, adrenal insufficiency

---

## Management by K Level

### K 5.0–6.0 mmol/L
- **PO Resonium A/C 15 g Q4H x 1–3 doses**
  - Use Resonium **A** in hyponatraemic patients
  - Can give PR if NPO
  - **Avoid repeated PR Resonium** in GI surgery / NBO patients — risk of intestinal perforation
- Recheck RFT

### K 6.0–7.0 mmol/L
- **Cardiac monitoring** +/- ECG
- **IV Calcium gluconate 10 ml bolus** over 3–5 min under cardiac monitor
  - Stabilises myocardium; can infuse over 30 min if preferred
  - **Does NOT lower K** — only stabilises the myocardium
- **Dextrose-insulin (DI) drip**: 10 units Actrapid in 50 ml D50 over 30 min x1
  - Only causes **transient intracellular K shift**
- **Oral Resonium A/C x 3 doses** (the only treatment that lowers total body K)
- Recheck RFT

### K >7.0 mmol/L
- **Inform MO**; consider consult renal +/- ICU for haemodialysis support
- Give **IV Ca gluconate over 3 min immediately**
- Attempt to lower K as above

---

## Special Situations

| Situation | Action |
|---|---|
| Haemolysed sample | Order urgent recheck; if >6.5 give Ca gluconate first |
| Resonium contraindicated | Consider saline + Lasix, zirconium, or patiromer |

---

## Key Reminders

- **Ca gluconate** → stabilises myocardium only; does not reduce K
- **DI drip** → transient shift only; must also give Resonium
- **Resonium** → the only agent that removes K from the body
