## Causes

| Location | Examples |
|---|---|
| Oesophageal | Mallory-Weiss/Boerhaave's tear, varices, oesophagitis, ulcer, CA, vascular lesions |
| Gastric | Gastritis, ulcer, CA, varices, vascular lesions |
| Duodenal | Duodenitis, ulcer, CA, vascular lesions |
| Systemic | Coagulopathy, antiplatelet/anticoagulant use |

## Assessment

- **Vitals** (tachycardia = unstable)
- ± Hemocue
- Amount; inspect vomitus/stool
- **Abdomen:** tenderness, peritoneal signs, mass, stigmata of CLD
- **PR exam**
- Background: CLD, previous OGD findings and treatment

## Management

### Resuscitation

- Massive bleeding: consider **direct blood transfusion**, intubation for airway protection
- **Strict NPO**, W/H DM meds, maintenance IVF
- Obs Q1H ×4, then Q4H if stable
- Chart haematemesis

### Investigations

- Urgent CBC + L/RFT + clotting ± T&S
- **Transfuse to keep Plt >50, INR <1.5**
- Urgent AXR + erect CXR

### Medications

- Off NSAIDs; W/H antiplatelets (consult if DAPT); W/H ± reverse anticoagulation
- **IV Pantoloc 40mg Q24H + stat**
  - Severe: IV Nexium 80mg bolus then infusion 80mg in 100ml NS at 10ml/hr until 72hrs after OGD
  - Or if Hx peptic ulcer: IV Pantoloc 40mg Q12H

### OGD (Consult SUR or MED GI)

- **Immediately** if ongoing active bleeding/unresponsive to resuscitation
- Within **24hrs** for non-variceal
- Within **12hrs** for variceal
- If OGD fails: CT angiogram / catheter angiogram + embolisation

### Variceal Bleed (Hx CLD/Known Varices)

- **Vasoactive agent:**
  - IV Terlipressin 2mg Q4H ×2 days ± 1mg Q4H ×3 more days (C/I in organ ischaemia)
  - OR IV Octreotide 50mcg stat then 50mcg/hr ×2–5 days
- **Antibiotic:** IV Rocephin 1g daily (or ciprofloxacin 500mg Q12H) ×5–7 days
- IV Vitamin K1 10mg daily + stat; IV thiamine
- **OGD within 12hrs**
- If massive haematemesis: **Sengstaken-Blakemore tube** until OGD
- Long term: OGD surveillance + banding, non-selective beta blocker (propranolol/nadolol)
