# Acute Retention of Urine (AROU)

> **Difficult painful voiding, with post-void residual urine (PVRU) > 300-350ml**

## Causes
* Bladder outflow obstruction: **constipation, blood clot, bladder stone**, urethral stricture
    * Male: **BPH, CA prostate/bladder neck**, phimosis/paraphimosis
    * Female: pelvic mass (e.g. CA cervix), pelvic organ prolapse
* Neurogenic bladder: **cord/conus/cauda compression**, autonomic neuropathy (e.g. DM, GBS)
* Drugs: GA/EA, anticholinergic, opioid, decongestant

> **Pitfall:** do not routinely order PSA for AROU case, since AROU can cause PSA rise up to 20! But can order PSA if suspected bone met (high ALP, pancytopenia, symptoms) – PSA >100 is 99% specific to CA prostate

## Complications
* **UTI**
* **Obstructive uropathy** (post-renal AKI)
* **Post-obstructive diuresis**

## Assessment
* **Urinary symptoms:** haematuria, dysuria, loin pain, fever
* Reversible causes: post-op, **pain, constipation**
* **LL power (r/o neurological compression!)**, neuro symptoms
* **PR exam** for 1) size and contour of prostate – median sulcus intact? any nodules? 2) anal tone, grip, perianal sensation, 3) faecal loaded?
* Background history: baseline LUTS, previous DRE/PSA/prostate sizing/biopsy, neuro disease

## Management
* **Foley to BSB, chart U/O Q4H** (watch out for post-obstructive diuresis)
    * Some departments prefer cath once first, then repeat PVRU x1 and foley to BSB if >350ml
    * If likely reversible: can write "TWOC on xx/xx/xx (24-48hrs later), PVRU x1, reinsert if >350ml"
* **Monitor CBC d/c, RFT**
* **CSU x m'stix, c/st**
* +/- KUB (for bladder stone)
* **Off offending drugs** (anticholinergic, opioid, decongestant)
* **Laxatives**, ensure BO daily
* Analgesics, ensure good pain control
* If BPH not on alpha blocker: **start Xatral XL 10mg nocte** if BP tolerates (C/I in postural hypotension, severe renal/hepatic impairment)
    * If refractory retention on alpha blocker -> indicated for BPH surgery
* **Consult URO** if failed TWOC or recurrent AROU after correction of reversible causes

*If gross haematuria with clot retention – refer to haematuria section!*
