## Causes

| Vomit Type | Causes |
|---|---|
| Undigested fluid/food | **GI:** GE, GOO, pancreatitis, cholecystitis, hepatitis, mesenteric ischaemia, Crohn's; **Neuro:** raised ICP, brainstem infarct, ICH, SOL, vertigo, migraine, demyelinating disease; **Metabolic:** pregnancy, drugs, uraemia, DKA, Addisonian crisis, hyper-/hypoCa |
| Bile-stained | IO, ileus |
| Haematemesis/coffee ground | UGIB — see Coffee Ground Vomiting section |

## Assessment

- When did N/V start, still nauseous?
- Content of vomitus
- **Abdomen:** painful, distended, peritoneal signs, masses, succussion splash
- **Neurological:** headache/dizziness/visual disturbance, GCS, pupils, EOM, nystagmus, facial asymmetry, limb power

## Management

### If Significant Vomiting (Aspiration Risk)

- **NPOEM**, W/H DM meds
- IVF; R/T to BSB, aspirate Q4H
- Chart I/O
- Monitor RFT + VBG + Ca/PO4 + Mg ± CBC d/c

### Antiemetics

- **IV Maxolon 10mg TDS** regular or prn

### Further Workup (If Persistent)

- AXR
- CT brain
- Treat underlying cause

### GOO

- IV Pantoloc 40mg Q24H + stat
- Workup for cause (PUD vs CA)

### CINV

- **Dexamethasone**
- **5-HT3 antagonist** (Zofran, Kytril)
- **NK-1 antagonist** (Emend)
- **Akynzeo** = 5-HT3 + NK-1 antagonist (combined)
