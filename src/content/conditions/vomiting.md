# Vomiting

## Causes
* **Vomiting of undigested fluid/food:**
    * GI: **GE, GOO**, pancreatitis, cholecystitis, hepatitis, mesenteric ischemia, Crohn etc.
    * Neuro: **↑ ICP**, brainstem infarct/ICH/**SOL**, vertigo, migraine, demyelinating disease etc.
    * Metabolic: pregnancy, **drugs** (e.g. opioids, chemo), uraemia, **DKA**, Addisonian crisis, **hyper/hypoCa** etc.
* **Bile-stained vomiting: IO**, ileus
* **Haematemesis / coffee ground vomiting: upper GI bleed** (refer to next section!)

> Sometimes it is not actual vomiting but "嘔痰" (expectoration of sputum)!

## Assessment
* When did N/V start? Still nauseous now?
* Content of vomitus – Undigested fluid/food? Bile-stained? Haematemesis / coffee ground?
* **Abdomen – Painful? Distended?** Examine for peritoneal signs, masses, succussion splash
* **Neuro – Any headache/dizziness/visual disturbance?** Check GCS, pupils, EOM, nystagmus, facial asymmetry, limb power

## Management
* **If significant vomiting (risk of aspiration): NPOEM, W/H DM meds, IVF, R/T to BSB, asp Q4H**
* **Chart I/O; monitor RFT, VBG, Ca/PO4, Mg, +/- CBC d/c**
* **IV Maxolon 10mg TDS regular or prn**
* **Workup if persistent: AXR, CT brain**
* **Treat underlying cause if known**
    * GOO: + IV Pantoloc 40mg Q24H + stat, further workup for cause (PUD vs CA)

> **Chemotherapy-induced N/V (CINV) can be treated with 3 drug classes:**
> * Dexamethasone
> * 5-HT3 antagonist (Zofran, Kytril)
> * NK-1 antagonist (Emend)
> * *Akynzeo = 5HT3 antagonist + NK-1 antagonist*
