- **Causes**:
  - Renal/ureteric stone, pyelonephritis, tumour, PCKD
  - Muscle strain, rib #, zoster, radiculopathy, retroperitoneal fibrosis, pneumonia, AAA

- **Assessment**: fever; urinary symptoms (renal colic radiating to suprapubic, dysuria, haematuria, turbid urine, passage of stone, LUTS); abdominal exam, costovertebral tenderness

- **Management**:
  - Blood x CBC d/c + RFT + Ca/PO4 + urate + clotting
  - MSU/CSU x m'stix + c/st (90% of patients with stone have microscopic haematuria)
  - PVRU ×1; KUB
  - **Urgent NCCT A+P stone protocol** (alternative: urgent USG urinary system)
  - If hydronephrosis/hydroureter + urosepsis/obstructive uropathy (AKI): **urgent consult URO** (may need urgent PCN or JJ stent)
  - Otherwise: routine consult/refer URO
  - Panadol prn; if Cr not markedly deranged (<120): PO Voltaren SR 100mg daily ×5/7 + PO Pantoloc 40mg daily ×5/7 for renal colic
  - If septic/UTI/elevated WCC: empirical antibiotics (per past urine c/st)
