# Loin Pain

## Causes
* **Renal/ureteric stone**, pyelonephritis, tumour, PCKD
* Muscle strain, rib #, zoster, radiculopathy, retroperitoneal fibrosis, pneumonia, AAA

## Assessment
* **Fever**
* Urinary symptoms: renal colic (radiate to suprapubic area), dysuria, haematuria, turbid urine, passage of stone, LUTS
* Abdominal exam, **costovertebral tenderness**

## Management
* Bld x CBC d/c, RFT, Ca/PO4, urate, clotting
* **MSU/CSU x m'stix, c/st** (90% of patients with stone have microscopic haematuria!)
* **PVRU x1**
* **KUB**
* **Urgent NCCT A+P stone protocol** (alternative: urgent USG urinary system)
    * **If hydronephrosis/hydroureter with urosepsis/obstructive uropathy (AKI): urgent consult URO** (may need urgent PCN or JJ stent)
    * Otherwise, routine consult / refer URO for positive findings
* Panadol prn
* If Cr not markedly deranged (e.g. <120): can add **PO Voltaren SR 100mg daily x 5/7 + PO Pantoloc 40mg daily x 5/7** for renal colic
    * Better give a fixed duration (e.g. 5-7 days) and not until 永遠
* If septic / UTI symptoms / ↑ WCC: empirical antibiotics (according to past urine c/st)

> **Tips for reading KUB:** trace **course of ureter** for stones (if not on the course: likely phlebolith)
> Originate from renal pelvis (may see renal shadow) around L1/2 level -> down along transverse spinous processes -> go slightly lateral along SI joint -> turn medially near ischial spine to join bladder
>
> **Reading NCCT:** try to trace the entire ureter for stones (or transition point with upstream dilation); sometimes it is difficult to trace, but **at least make sure no hydronephrosis/hydroureter**
> Don't forget to check any pneumoperitoneum, aorta/IVC, bowels, liver, gallbladder, biliary tree, spleen, pancreas, spine, psoas and abdominal wall muscles!
