- **Causes**:
  - Polyuria/diuresis: diuretics, osmotic/DM, UTI, polydipsia, cranial/nephrogenic DI, channelopathies
  - Urinary incontinence: multiparity, detrusor overactivity, neurogenic bladder, overflow incontinence

- **Management**:
  - **Polyuria/diuresis**: chart I/O; fluid replacement (post-obstructive diuresis: replace 1/2 to 2/3 of lost volume); monitor RFT + electrolytes + H'stix; FG + A1c; MSU/CSU x c/st; if persistent: paired serum/urine osmo and Na, refer MED
  - **Urinary incontinence**: r/o neurological cause; pelvic floor exercise; refer URO/GYN if needed
  - **Pitfall**: chronic retention may present as overflow incontinence (PVRU still >350ml); anticholinergics C/I in these cases
