## Precautions

- **Droplet precautions**; isolation not mandatory
- **NDORS reporting**: only required if desaturation requiring **>4 L O2**

## Antivirals

Indicated for **mild symptomatic high-risk patients** within **5 days of symptom onset**:

### Paxlovid (nirmatrelvir/ritonavir) — preferred

| eGFR | Dose |
|---|---|
| >60 | 300 mg / 100 mg PO Q12H × 5 days |
| 30–60 | 150 mg / 100 mg PO Q12H × 5 days |
| <30 | Contraindicated |

- **Drug-drug interactions +++** — common: statins (stop statin for duration of course)

### Molnupiravir — fewer interactions, less effective

- Use if Paxlovid contraindicated
- **PO 800 mg Q12H × 5 days**

## Management of Desaturation / New Infiltrates

Indicated if **SpO2 <94% on room air** or **new CXR infiltrates**:

### Remdesivir

- **Contraindicated** if ALT ≥5× ULN or eGFR <30
- **IV 200 mg STAT**, then **100 mg Q24H × 4–9 days** (total 5–10 days)

### Dexamethasone

- **IV 6 mg STAT**, then **Q24H × 9 days** (total 10 days)
- Alternatives:
  - Hydrocortisone 100 mg IV then 50 mg Q8H
  - Prednisolone 40 mg PO daily
- Check **HBsAg**; monitor **H'stix BD**; add **PO Pantoloc 40 mg daily** during steroid course

### Adjunctive Immunomodulator

- Baricitinib / tocilizumab
- **Consult MED / ID** if worsening / no improvement and **CRP >75**

## DVT Prophylaxis

For patients not already on anticoagulation:

- **Prophylactic LMWH**: Clexane 1 mg/kg Q24H
- Inpatient: therapeutic dose (1 mg/kg Q12H) may be superior
- TED stocking; early mobilisation
- Discontinue before discharge if no long-term indication
