# Anorectal Pain / Discharge

## Causes
* Internal/ external haemorrhoids
* Anal fissures
* Anal fistulas
* Ano-rectal malignancy
* Perianal abscess
* **NF**
* Pilonidal disease, STDs

## Assessment
* **PR + proctoscopy** for thrombosed haemorrhoid / fissure / fistula / cancer
* **Examine perianal area for fluctuance (perianal abscess) and crepitus/blisters (NF)!!**
* Perianal abscess: size, skin erythema, indurance, fluctuance, ruptured or not +/- discharge or not

## Management
* Consult SUR, plus:
* Anal fissure: xylocaine jelly LA PRN for pain relief, stool softeners
* Perianal abscess: IV antibiotics, **urgent consult SUR x I&D**, work-up for undiagnosed DM (FG, A1c)
* **NF: urgent consult SUR/ URO + broad spectrum IV antibiotics (e.g. Meronem) + stat**

## Haemorrhoid
* **Faktu/Anusol** rectal suppository/ointment – (dosage: 1 tablet e.g. daily/ BD/ TDS)
* **PO Daflon**
* **PO Metamucil** (3 x 5ml spoonfuls), advise high fibre and fluid intake
* Relieve constipation (e.g. lactulose/ senna/ Dulcolax)
* Refer SUR
