## Causes

| Level | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Brain** | Head injury, CVA, SOL, encephalitis, demyelination, seizure / post-ictal Todd's paresis |
| **Spinal cord** | Trauma, CVA, myelopathy, transverse myelitis, B12 deficiency |
| **Anterior horn cell** | Motor neuron disease (MND), spinal muscular atrophy (SMA) |
| **Nerve root** | Cauda equina syndrome, radiculopathy |
| **Neuropathy** | GBS, CIDP, DM, alcoholic, autoimmune, paraneoplastic, multiple myeloma, lymphoma, HIV, Lyme disease, heavy metal poisoning, porphyria, CMT, nerve entrapment/trauma |
| **Neuromuscular junction** | Myasthenia gravis (MG), Lambert-Eaton myasthenic syndrome (LEMS), botulism |
| **Myopathy** | Statin-induced, Cushing's, hypothyroid, autoimmune myositis, muscular dystrophies, periodic paralysis |

## Assessment

- **Vitals**, GCS
- **Onset**: exact last-seen-well time needed for stroke workup
- **Trigger**: fatigue, exercise, meal
- **Pattern**:
  - Myotomal vs peripheral nerve vs single limb vs hemiparesis vs paraparesis vs tetraparesis
  - Proximal vs distal vs pyramidal (UMNL)
- **Associated symptoms**: headache, dysarthria, facial weakness, sensory loss, sphincter disturbance, fatiguability, myalgia
- **Full neurological exam**: cranial nerves, 4-limb power/reflexes/sensation, cerebellar signs
- **Bulbar/respiratory muscle involvement**
- **UMNL signs**: hyperreflexia, clonus, hypertonia
- **If cord suspected**: spinal tenderness, sensory level, PR
- **If no objective weakness**: consider MG (test fatiguability), fibromyalgia, sarcopenia

## Management

- **New or acutely worsened weakness**: neuro obs Q1H × 4, then Q4H if stable
- **Respiratory fatigue**: bedside spirometry Q4H; early ventilation if FVC **<15 ml/kg**
- **Bulbar signs/symptoms**: NPOEM, consult speech therapist
- **Brain-origin weakness**: urgent CT brain, **activate stroke call** / urgent consult neurosurgery
- **Cord-origin weakness**: urgent MRI C/T/LS spine + whole-spine sagittal screening, **urgent consult neurosurgery**
- **Unknown cause**: consult neurology

> **New onset weakness is stroke / cord compression until proven otherwise.**
