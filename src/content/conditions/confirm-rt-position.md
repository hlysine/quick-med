# Confirm R/T Position

## Methods:
* Gurgling sound on air injection
* Gastric aspirate pH
* CXR

> **If R/T position is not correct:**
> * **DO NOT START FEEDING**
> * **Order to advance** (if not deep enough) **or withdraw** (if coil) **or reinsert then repeat CXR** for position
>
> Better safe than sorry (aspiration pneumonia)!

## Evaluation of Nasogastric Tube Position
* The chest X-ray view should be adequate - upper esophagus down to below the diaphragm
* The NG tube should remain in the midline down to the level of the diaphragm
* The NG tube should bisect the carina (T4)
* The tip of the NG tube should be clearly visible and below the diaphragm
* The tip of the NG tube should be 10 cm beyond the gastro-esophageal junction to be confident that it's within the stomach
