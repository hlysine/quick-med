Retrospective assessment after transient LOC. For sustained coma, refer to the Decreased GCS section.

## Causes

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Metabolic/other** | Hypotension, hypoxia/hyperventilation with hypocapnia, hypoglycaemia, intoxication, anaemia |
| **Reflex syncope** (most common) | Vasovagal, situational, carotid hypersensitivity |
| **Postural hypotension** (2nd most common) | Diuretics/vasodilators, hypovolaemia (e.g. haemorrhage), autonomic neuropathy |
| **Cardiac syncope** | Arrhythmia, ACS, structural (AS, HOCM, tamponade) |
| **Vascular** | PE, pulmonary HTN, SVCO, aortic dissection |
| **Neurological** | SAH, vertebrobasilar TIA, seizure, subclavian steal syndrome |

## Complications

- Fall, head injury (HI)

## Assessment

- Vitals, GCS
- If **fever + LOC**: manage as CNS infection → blood c/st + LP
- **History — circumstances**: trigger, position during episode, witness, fall/HI
- **Preceding symptoms**: pre-syncope/autonomic symptoms, dizziness, headache, visual disturbance, slurring/weakness, chest discomfort, SOB, palpitations
- **Seizure features**: twitching, up-rolling eyeballs, tongue biting, double incontinence, post-ictal drowsiness
- Current symptoms
- **Full neurological exam**: CN, 4-limb power, cerebellar signs, meningism
- **Cardiovascular exam**: JVP, carotid bruit, heart sounds

## Management

- Neuro obs, cardiac monitoring, H'stix monitoring
- Lie supine
- **Postural BP** (supine + erect 0, 1, 3 min) × 2
- **ECG LLII**: note tachy, brady/pause, AV block, pre-excitation, ST/T changes, QT prolongation
- Urgent CT brain
- **Bloods**: CBC d/c + L/RFT + Ca/PO4 + Mg
- IVF replacement
- Withhold diuretics/vasodilators
- If S/S of stroke/TIA: **activate stroke call**
- Treat underlying cause
- Consult MED (may need 24-hr Holter, echo, tilt-table test, carotid sinus massage)
