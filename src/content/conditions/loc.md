# Loss of Consciousness (LOC)

> **About retrospective assessment after transient LOC (if sustained coma, refer to section on ↓ GCS)**

## Causes
* Hypotension
* Hypoxia / hyperventilation with hypocapnia
* **Metabolic** e.g. **hypoglycaemia**, intoxication
* Anaemia
* Reflex syncope (most common): vasovagal/situational, carotid hypersensitivity
* **Postural hypotension** (2nd most common): diuretics/vasodilators, hypovolaemia (e.g. **haemorrhage**), autonomic neuropathy
* **Cardiac syncope: arrhythmia, ACS, structural** (AS, HOCM, tamponade)
* **Vascular: PE**, pulmonary HTN, **SVCO, aortic dissection**
* **Neurological: SAH, vertebrobasilar TIA, seizure**, subclavian steal syndrome

## Complications
* Fall, HI

## Assessment
* Vitals, GCS (*if fever + LOC, manage as CNS infection, need bld c/st & LP – refer to section on ↓ GCS*)
* **History:**
    * **Circumstances:** Trigger? **Standing/sitting/in exertion?** Any witness? Any **fall/HI?**
    * **Preceding symptoms:** pre-syncope (autonomic symptoms), dizziness, headache, visual disturbance, **slurring/weakness**, **chest discomfort, SOB**, palpitations
    * **Seizure features** during episode (reported by witness): limb twitching / up-rolling eyeballs / tongue biting / double incontinence / post-drowsiness
    * **Current symptoms**
* **Full neuro exam:** CN (pupils, EOM, CN7, dysarthria), 4 limbs power, cerebellar signs, **meningism**
* (CV exam: JVP, carotid bruit, heart sounds)

## Management
* Neuro obs, cardiac monitoring, H'stix monitoring
* Lie supine
* **Postural BP** (supine + erect 0,1,3 minutes) x2
* **ECG LLII** (note tachy, brady/pause, AV block, pre-excitation, ST/T changes, QT prolongation)
* **Urgent CT brain**
* Bld x CBC d/c, L/RFT, Ca/PO4, Mg
* IVF replacement
* W/H diuretics/vasodilators
* **If s/s of stoke/TIA (slurring, weakness, cerebellar signs): activate stroke call**
* Treat underlying cause if known
* Consult MED (may need further Ix e.g. 24hr Holter, echo, tilt-table test, carotid sinus massage)
