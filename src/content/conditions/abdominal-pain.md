## Causes

| Region | Emergency | Non-Emergency |
|---|---|---|
| Central / generalised | Perforated viscus, IO, ischaemic bowel, complicated diverticulitis/collections, ruptured AAA, SBP/CAPD peritonitis, DKA/Addisonian crisis | IBD, CA, diverticulosis, GE, IBS, constipation, hyperCa |
| Upper abdomen | PPU, pancreatitis, cholecystitis/cholangitis/liver abscess, ruptured HCC/spleen/AAA, ACS | GERD/gastritis/PUD/CA, functional dyspepsia, biliary colic, hepatitis |
| Lower abdomen | Appendicitis, diverticulitis, AROU/obstructive uropathy/urosepsis, testicular torsion, ruptured ectopic/ovarian cyst/ovarian torsion, PID | Simple cystitis, non-obstructive ureteric stone, dysmenorrhea/fibroid/endometriosis, CA |

## Assessment

- **Vitals**
- **SOCRATES** of pain
- Associated symptoms: N/V, bowel opening, GI bleed, jaundice, urinary symptoms, constitutional symptoms
- **Gynaecological history** (women)
- **Abdomen:** masses, ascites, peritoneal signs, Murphy sign, hernia, bowel sounds
- **PR (± PV and bimanual)** if relevant
- Background + previous imaging/endoscopy

## Management

### Mild / Non-Sinister

- Analgesics: Panadol, Tramadol
- **Buscopan/Holopon** for colicky pain (C/I in IO)
- If persistent: reassess + consider imaging

### Severe / Worrying

#### NPO and Supportive
- NPO, W/H DM meds, maintenance IVF

#### Bloods
- CBC d/c + L/RFT + Ca/PO4 + clotting
- VBG + lactate (ischaemic bowel)
- Epigastric: + amylase (pancreatitis) + TnI/CK + ECG (ACS)

#### Urine
- Pregnancy test for women of childbearing age
- MSU/CSU x m'stix + c/st

#### Stool
- Microbiological workup if diarrhoea

#### Imaging
- AXR
- Erect CXR (perforated viscus)
- Consider urgent **CT A+P with contrast** (+ arterial phase if active bleeding)
- Gynaecological causes: **TAS + TVS**

#### Medications
- IV Pantoloc 40mg Q24H + stat
- PO/PR Panadol 500mg Q6H prn
- IV Tramadol 50mg Q6–8H prn
- IV Maxolon 10mg Q8H prn
- Empirical antibiotics if infection signs (e.g. IV Augmentin)

#### Consults
- Urgent consult **SUR/GYN** for emergencies

### SBP (Spontaneous Bacterial Peritonitis)

- Criteria: ascitic fluid polymorphs >250 or WCC >500 AND >50% polymorphs or +ve culture
- IV cefotaxime / Rocephin / ciprofloxacin
- Limited paracentesis (<5L/day) under IV albumin
- Watch for HE/GIB
- **Secondary prophylaxis:** Septrin / ciprofloxacin

### Symptomatic Ascites

- Low salt diet; FR <1.2L/day
- Lasix / Aldactone
- Paracentesis; treat underlying cause
- **Large volume paracentesis (>5L): need IV albumin 8g/L**
- C/I in SBP / renal failure / dehydration

### Ischaemic Bowel

> Lacks abdominal signs unless perforated

- Clues: AF/heart/vascular disease, abd pain ± PR bleed, metabolic acidosis
- **Urgent CT angiogram + consult SUR**
