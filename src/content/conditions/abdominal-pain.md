# Abdominal Pain

## Causes

| | Emergency | Non-emergency |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Central/generalised** | Perforated viscus<br>IO<br>Ischaemic bowel<br>Complicated diverticulitis, collections<br>Ruptured AAA (radiates to back)<br>SBP, CAPD peritonitis<br>DKA, Addisonian crisis | IBD<br>CA<br>Diverticulosis<br>GE<br>IBS<br>Constipation<br>HyperCa |
| **Upper abdomen** | PPU<br>Pancreatitis (radiates to back)<br>Cholecystitis, cholangitis, liver abscess<br>Ruptured HCC/spleen/AAA<br>ACS | GERD, gastritis, peptic ulcer, CA<br>Functional dyspepsia<br>Biliary colic, symptomatic gallstone<br>Hepatitis |
| **Lower abdomen** | Appendicitis<br>Diverticulitis<br>AROU, obstructive uropathy, urosepsis<br>Testicular torsion<br>Ruptured ectopic/ovarian cyst, ovarian torsion, PID | Simple cystitis, non-obstructive ureteric stone<br>Dysmenorrhea, fibroid, endometriosis<br>CA |

> Abdominal pain may be trivial or life-threatening – fever, tachycardia, elevated WCC, and abdominal signs indicate significant pathology!

## Assessment
* **Vitals**
* **SOCRATES** of pain
* **Associated symptoms:** N/V, bowel opening, **GI bleed, jaundice**, urinary, constitutional symptoms
* **Gynaecological history:** PV bleed/discharge, menstrual, sexual
* **Abdomen:** masses, ascites, peritoneal signs, **Murphy sign, hernia**, bowel sounds
* **PR** (+/- PV and bimanual exam) if relevant
* Background history, previous abdominal imaging/endoscopy

## Management for Mild Non-sinister Cases of Abdominal Pain
* Analgesics e.g. Panadol, Tramadol
* Buscopan/Holopon for colicky abd pain (**C/I in IO**)
* If persistent: **reassess** and reconsider ddx, consider abdominal imaging/endoscopy
    * E.g. early/routine USG abdomen for non-specific abdominal pain

## Management for Severe / Worrying Cases of Abdominal Pain
* **NPO, W/H DM meds**, maintenance IVF
* **Bloods**
    * **CBC d/c, L/RFT, Ca/PO4, clotting** (for bleeding, infection, HBP/URO pathology)
    * **VBG, lactate** (for ischaemic bowel)
    * **[Epigastric pain] + amylase** (for pancreatitis), **+ TnI/CK + ECG** (for ACS)
* **Urine**
    * **Pregnancy test for all women of childbearing age**
    * MSU / CSU x m'stix, c/st, (+/- amylase if epigastric pain)
* Stool x microbiological workup if diarrhoea (see section on diarrhoea)
* **Imaging (ensure PT -ve before XR)**
    * AXR
    * **Erect CXR** (for perforated viscus)
    * **Consider urgent CT A+P with contrast** (inform MO!)
        * With arterial phase (i.e. CT angiogram) if suspect active bleeding
        * Alternative: USG abdomen, or just hepatobiliary/urinary system
    * Gynae causes: TAS + TVS
* **GEN SUR "四味"**
    * IV Pantoloc 40mg Q24H + stat
    * PO or PR Panadol 500mg Q6H prn (max daily dose 4g; lower dose if Cr ↑; avoid if ALT ↑)
    * IV Tramadol 50mg Q6-8H prn (max daily dose 300mg; lower dose if Cr ↑)
    * IV Maxolon 10mg Q8H prn
* **Empirical antibiotic** (e.g. IV Augmentin) if signs of infection
* Treat underlying cause; **urgent consult SUR/GYN** for respective emergencies (inform MO!)
    * If ascites +ve: **consider ruptured HCC/AAA/spleen, or SBP (or CAPD peritonitis)!**

> **Ischaemic bowel classically lacks abdominal signs** (unless perforated), so requires high degree of suspicion! Clues:
> * **Underlying AF/heart/vascular disease**
> * Abd pain +/- PR bleed
> * **Metabolic (lactic) acidosis**
>
> -> **urgent CT angiogram and consult SUR**

> **If eGFR <45** not on dialysis but need contrast CT:
> * Plain CT or USG if doesn't lower diagnostic yield; **proceed with contrast CT** if clinically indicated
> * **Volume expansion with NS** (alternatives: IV NaHCO3 or IV acetylcysteine but no evidence)

> **Symptomatic ascites:** low salt diet, FR <1.2L/day, Lasix/Aldactone, paracentesis, treat underlying cause (ruptured HCC/AAA/spleen, SBP, CAPD peritonitis, CHF, cirrhosis, CKD, nephrotic syndrome)
> **Large volume paracentesis (>5L): need IV albumin 8g/L**, and is **C/I in SBP/renal failure/dehydration**
> **SBP (ascitic fluid polymorph >250 / WCC >500 AND >50% polymorphs / +ve culture):** IV cefotaxime/ Rocephin/ ciprofloxacin, + **limited volume paracentesis (<5L/day)** under IV albumin cover, watch out for HE & GIB; Septrin/ciprofloxacin for secondary prophylaxis after acute episode
