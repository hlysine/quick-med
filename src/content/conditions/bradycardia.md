# Bradycardia

## Definition

- HR <60

## Causes

- Anti-arrhythmic drugs
- MI (especially inferior / posterior)
- Electrolyte disturbance: hyperK, hyperMg, hyperCa
- Hypothyroid
- Hypothermia
- Sepsis
- Pre-existing sinus node dysfunction / AV nodal block

## Assessment

- BP, HR level, alertness, dizziness, chest discomfort
- **Unstable bradycardia** = shock attributed to bradyarrhythmia / altered consciousness / angina / ADHF

## Management

### General Measures

- Cardiac monitor; bed rest
- 12-lead ECG with long lead II — look for ischaemic changes, sinus pause, AV block
- If new onset: CBC d/c, RFT, Ca, Mg, TFT, TnI/CK
- Withhold anti-arrhythmic drugs
- Consult Cardiology if indicated for permanent pacing

### Additional Measures (if HR <40, sinus pause >4 s, or unstable)

- Apply transcutaneous pacing pads
- Consult CCU for pacing (usually only if HR <30 and symptomatic)
- **Atropine 0.5 mg IV bolus** under cardiac monitoring; repeat Q3–5 min, max 3 mg total
  - C/I in glaucoma
  - **Effective for:** sinus bradycardia or intranodal AV block (PR prolonged, QRS narrow)
  - **Ineffective for:** infranodal block (PR normal, QRS wide)
- If atropine ineffective: **dopamine 200 mg in 100 ml NS at 10–30 ml/hr**
  - ± isoproterenol / adrenaline in ICU setting
