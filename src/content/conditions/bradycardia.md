# Bradycardia

> **Typically refer to when HR <60**

## Causes
* Anti-arrhythmic drugs
* Myocardial infarction (especially inferior or posterior)
* HyperK/Mg/Ca
* Hypothyroid
* Hypothermia
* Sepsis
* Pre-existing sinus node dysfunction / AV nodal block

> **Verbal order:** BPx1, ECG LLII, cardiac monitor

## How to determine the URGENCY?
* What is the **blood pressure**?
* How **low** is the HR? Is it **new** bradycardia?
* Is the patient **alert**? Any **dizziness/chest discomfort**?

> If HR >40 and no pause >4 seconds, patient should be asymptomatic **at rest**

> **Unstable bradycardia** = shock attributed to bradyarrhythmia / altered consciousness / angina / acute decompensated heart failure; in critically ill patients, **bradycardia can mean impending arrest!**

## General management
* **Cardiac monitor**, bed rest
* **12-lead ECG with long lead II** (look for *ischaemic changes* and *sinus pause/AV block*)
    * Consult cardiac if indicated for permanent pacing
* If new onset bradycardia: CBC d/c, RFT, Ca, Mg, TFT, TnI/CK
* **W/H antiarrhythmic drugs** (if recent GA, check anaesthetic record for HR and neostigmine use)

## Additional measures if HR<40 or sinus pause >4 seconds or unstable:
* **Monitor patient with transcutaneous pacing pads**
* **Consult CCU x transcutaneous/transvenous pacing** (usually only do if HR<30 and **symptomatic**)
* **Atropine 0.5mg IV bolus** under cardiac monitoring, repeat Q3-5min, max 3mg (**C/I in glaucoma**)
    * Effective for sinus bradycardia or intranodal AV block (PR prolonged, QRS narrow)
    * Ineffective for infranodal block (PR normal, QRS wide)
* Atropine ineffective: dopamine 200mg in 100ml NS 10-30ml/hr, +/- isoproterenol/adrenaline in ICU
