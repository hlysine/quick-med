## Causes

- Internal / external haemorrhoids
- Anal fissures
- Anal fistulas
- Ano-rectal malignancy
- Perianal abscess
- Necrotising fasciitis (NF)
- Pilonidal disease
- STDs

## Assessment

- **PR + proctoscopy:** thrombosed haemorrhoid, fissure, fistula, cancer
- **Examine perianal area:**
  - Fluctuance → perianal abscess
  - Crepitus / blisters → necrotising fasciitis
- Perianal abscess: size, erythema, induration, fluctuance, ruptured, discharge

## Management

Consult **SUR** for all cases, plus condition-specific management:

### Anal Fissure

- Xylocaine jelly (LA) prn for pain
- Stool softeners

### Perianal Abscess

- IV antibiotics
- **Urgent consult SUR x I&D**
- Workup for undiagnosed DM: fasting glucose, HbA1c

### Necrotising Fasciitis (NF)

- **Urgent consult SUR/URO**
- Broad spectrum IV antibiotics (e.g. Meronem) + stat

### Haemorrhoid

- **Faktu/Anusol** rectal suppository/ointment (1 tablet daily/BD/TDS)
- PO Daflon
- PO Metamucil (3×5 spoonfuls)
- Advise high fibre and fluid
- Relieve constipation
- Refer SUR
