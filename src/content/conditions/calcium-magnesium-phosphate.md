## Hypercalcaemia

- Normal Ca: **2.10–2.55 mmol/L**
- **Adjusted Ca = measured Ca + 0.02 × (40 − albumin)**

### Causes
| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Hyperparathyroidism | Primary (adenoma / hyperplasia / CA); tertiary (CKD) |
| Malignancy | Bone erosion, PTHrP, ectopic calcitriol |
| Drug-induced | Thiazide, vitamin D, Ca supplements, vitamin A |
| Other | TB, milk-alkali syndrome, hyperthyroidism, FHH |

### Assessment
- Symptoms, volume status (typically dehydrated), drug history
- Blood: ionised Ca + PO4 + RFT + PTH
- ECG: short QT, bizarre QRS
- +/- 24 hr urine Ca or fractional excretion of Ca (if FHH suspected)

### Management
- Off thiazide / Ca / vitamin D / vitamin A / antacids
- **Fluid replacement** (crisis: NS 200–500 ml/hr, aim UO 100 ml/hr)
- Monitor I/O + electrolytes + RFT + ECG
- **After adequate rehydration**: loop diuretic (Lasix) to enhance renal Ca excretion
- +/- **IV pamidronate** 30–90 mg in 250–500 ml NS over 4–6 hrs (bisphosphonate — **C/I in renal failure**)
- +/- **SC salmon calcitonin** 4 IU/kg Q12H (ICU only; onset 2–3 hrs)
- +/- **IV hydrocortisone** 5 mg/kg Q8H (useful in vitamin D intoxication / haematological malignancy)
- +/- Consult renal / ICU for HD if severe

## Hypocalcaemia

### Causes
- CKD (secondary hyperPTH)
- Hungry bone syndrome (post-parathyroidectomy — needs aggressive replacement)
- Vitamin D deficiency
- Hypomagnesaemia
- Sequestration: pancreatitis, rhabdomyolysis, tumour lysis

### Assessment (Complications)
- Chvostek / Trousseau signs
- Carpopedal spasm / tetany
- Seizure
- ECG: bradyarrhythmia, AV block, prolonged QT

### Management
- Check: ionised Ca + PO4 + Mg + RFT + PTH + vitamin D
- **Correct hypomagnesaemia first**
- If critically low or symptomatic: **IV 10% calcium gluconate 20 ml over 10–15 min**; add Ca to IVF
- **Oral Ca supplementation** 1–2 g elemental Ca daily:
  - Calcichew: 1 g CaCO3 + 800 IU vitamin D
  - Calcium carbonate: 40% elemental Ca (typically 500 mg/chew)
  - Caltrate: 600 mg Ca; Oscal: 500 mg Ca
  - Take with meals (acidic environment needed); if on acid-blocking therapy — use **calcium citrate**
- **Vitamin D analogue**: calcitriol 0.25–1 mcg daily, or alfacalcidol 0.5–3 mcg daily
- Monitor Ca and PO4
- **Space out Ca supplements from thyroxine**

## Hypomagnesaemia

### Causes
- Malnutrition / NPO / refeeding syndrome (monitor K and PO4 as well)
- GI loss: vomiting / diarrhoea
- Renal loss
- Drugs: diuretics, amphotericin B, aminoglycosides, PPIs

### Assessment (Complications)
- Hyperreflexia
- ECG: Torsades de pointes
- Hypokalaemia / hypocalcaemia

### Management
- Monitor K + Ca + PO4
- Review culprit drugs (cyclosporine / diuretics / aminoglycosides / amphotericin B / PPIs)
- **Mg supplement options**:
  - IV: 4 ml 50% MgSO4 (8 mmol) in 500 ml NS Q8H x1 day
  - If urgent: IV 4 ml 50% MgSO4 in 20 ml NS over 15 min
  - If **Torsades de pointes**: IV 1–2 g MgSO4 in 10 ml D5 bolus
  - PO: Mg L-lactate dihydrate 84–168 mg BD (slow release)
  - PO: Mg trisilicate mixture 10 ml TDS (poor oral bioavailability)
  - PO: MgSO4 5–10 ml TDS
- Amiloride can reduce urinary Mg loss

## Hyperphosphataemia

- Usually ESRD or iatrogenic (PO4 over-replacement / fleet enema)
- Beware: tumour lysis / rhabdomyolysis / haemolysis
- Check: RFT + Ca/PO4 + ALP
- Management:
  - Low phosphate diet
  - **Phosphate binder with meals** (Caltrate / sevelamer / Alusorb)
  - RRT if refractory

---

## Hypophosphataemia

### Causes
- Malabsorption / refeeding syndrome
- Vitamin D deficiency
- Primary hyperPTH
- Hungry bone syndrome (post-parathyroidectomy)
- Renal tubular defects

### Assessment
- Blood: RFT + Mg + Ca/PO4 + ALP + PTH +/- vitamin D
- Usually **no treatment if PO4 >0.5 mmol/L**

### Management by Severity

| Severity | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Mild (<0.5 mmol/L) | Phosphate Sandoz 1 tab BD/TDS/QID x3 days (16 mmol PO4, 20 mmol Na); or phosphate sodium 10 ml BD/TDS x3 days |
| Severe (<0.3 mmol/L with symptoms) | IV K diphosphate (K2HPO4) 6 ml in 500 ml D5 Q12H (14.5 mmol PO4 + 25 mmol K per 10 ml); or IV Na glycerophosphate (Glycophos) 20 ml in 100 ml NS Q4H (1 mmol PO4 + 2 mmol Na/ml) |

- **Rapid IV replacement** may cause symptomatic hypocalcaemia and CaPO4 precipitation
- If **hypoCa + hypoPO4 together**: prescribe vitamin D supplement (Calcichew) instead of IV PO4
