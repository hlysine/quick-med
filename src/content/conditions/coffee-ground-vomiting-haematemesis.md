# Coffee Ground Vomiting / Haematemesis / Tarry Stool

## Causes
* Oesophageal: tear (Mallory-Weiss/Boerhaave's), **varices**, esophagitis, ulcer, CA, vascular lesions
* Gastric: gastritis, **ulcer, CA, varices**, vascular lesions
* Duodenal: duodenitis, **ulcer, CA**, vascular lesions
* Systemic: coagulopathy, antiplatelet/anticoagulant

## Assessment
* Vitals (considered unstable if tachycardia)
* +/- Hemocue (POCT for Hb level)
* Amount of bleeding
* Inspect the vomitus / stool
* **Abdomen:** tenderness, peritoneal signs, mass, stigmata of chronic liver disease
* **PR exam**
* **Background history: any chronic liver disease, previous OGD findings and tx**

> **Pitfall:** iron-stain stool mistaken as tarry stool – check if patient is on FeSO4!
> Melaena has a distinctive pungent smell!
> **Fresh melaena = 捽開佢見到黑中帶紅 = active UGIB -> need urgent OGD!** (vs old melaena may wait till next day for OGD, but call MO/SUR if see melaena anyway!)

## Management
* Resuscitation (if massive bleeding: consider direct blood transfusion, intubation for airway protection)
* **Strict NPO, W/H DM meds** during fasting, maintenance IVF
* Obs Q1H x4 then Q4H if stable
* Chart haematemesis
* **Urgent CBC, L/RFT, clotting, +/- T&S;** transfuse to keep Plt > 50, INR < 1.5
* **Urgent AXR + erect CXR**
* **Off NSAIDs, W/H antiplatelets** (consult if DAPT for ACS), **W/H +/- reverse anticoagulation**
* **IV Pantoloc 40mg Q24H + stat** [If severe: can give IV Nexium 80mg bolus followed by infusion (80mg in 100ml NS, 10ml/hr until 72hrs after OGD) or if Hx of peptic ulcer, IV Pantoloc 40mg Q12H]
* Inform if deteriorate
* **Consult SUR** (or MED GI) **x OGD** (may be C/I if suspect perforation)
    * **Immediately if ongoing active bleeding (fresh melaena / massive haematemesis), or unresponsive to resuscitation**
    * Otherwise: within 24hrs for non-variceal bleed; within 12hrs for variceal bleed
* If OGD fails to identify source / stop bleeding: CT angiogram / catheter angiogram + embolisation

## Additional management for Variceal Bleed (Hx of chronic liver disease / known varices)
* **Vasoactive agent:** **IV Terlipressin 2mg Q4H x 2 days +/- 1mg Q4H x 3 more days** (C/I in organ ischemia) or **IV octreotide 50mcg stat then infusion 50mcg/hr x 2-5 days**
* **Antibiotic: IV Rocephin 1g daily** (or ciprofloxacin 500mg Q12H) x 5-7 days
* **IV vitamin K1 10mg daily + stat**, IV thiamine
* **OGD within 12hrs**
* If massive haematemesis: Sengstaken-blakemore tube until OGD
* (TIPSS as salvage therapy)
* Long term: OGD surveillance and banding, non-selective beta blocker (e.g. propranolol, nadolol)
