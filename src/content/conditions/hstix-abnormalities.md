# H'stix Abnormalities

## Definition

- Nurses inform when H'stix **<4** or **>12**

## Urgency

- **Hypoglycaemia**: treat immediately
- **Hyperglycaemia**: very common, not urgent unless unconscious or DKA/HHS symptoms

---

## Hyperglycaemia

### Information Needed

- NPO? Pre- or post-meal? Regular DM meds resumed?
- Symptoms of DKA/HHS: abdominal pain, N/V, Kussmaul breathing, dehydration, confusion

### Initial Management

- H'stix TDS + nocte (Q4H if >20 or symptomatic)
- SC Actrapid stat ± sliding scale
- Treat less aggressively if: before bedtime, NPO, renal failure, on regular mixed insulin
- If diet allowed: DM diet, resume DM meds (check Cr, renal dosing)
- **Do not resume SGLT2i during stress episode**

### Persistent Hyperglycaemia

- Fasting glucose + HbA1c
- Refer GOPC ± consult MED
- Consider metformin 500mg daily (max 2000mg; max 1000mg if eGFR 30–44; **C/I if eGFR <30, liver failure, metabolic acidosis, tissue hypoxia, alcoholism**)

---

## Hyperglycaemic Emergencies

### DKA vs HHS

| Feature | DKA | HHS |
|---|---|---|
| **Symptoms** | Abdominal pain, vomiting, dehydration, Kussmaul breathing, fruity odour | Confusion, altered GCS, dehydration |
| **H'stix/RG** | >14 | >30–33 |
| **Ketonaemia** | >3 / ketonuria >2+ | No/mild ketonuria |
| **pH** | <7.3 | >7.3 |
| **HCO3** | <15 | >15 |
| **Serum osmo** | Normal/raised | >320 |

### Management (Both DKA and HHS)

- H'stix Q1H; aim 6–11
- VBG + RG + anion gap + BHBA + RFT Q4–6H
- Urine m'stix + ketone
- **Rehydration IVF**: Q1H ×2 then maintenance Q6H
  - Use NS → switch to ½:½ or D5 when hyperNa or H'stix <14
  - + KCl into IVF as needed
- **Insulin pump**: Actrapid 50 units in 50ml NS/gelofusine, start 0.1 unit/kg/hr
- Urgent consult endocrine ± ICU

### Pitfall

- **Euglycaemic DKA on SGLT2i**: acidosis/urine ketone +ve should prompt DKA workup even with normal glucose

---

## Hypoglycaemia

### Immediate Treatment

- If can eat + borderline H'stix 3–4: apple juice / sugar granules
- If NPO or severe: **IV D50 40ml bolus**
- If alcoholic/poor intake: **IV thiamine 100mg**

### Severe Hypoglycaemia (H'stix <2 or Altered GCS)

- Withhold all DM meds including insulin
- Maintenance **IV D10 Q6–8H** (especially if on long-acting DM meds)
- Recheck H'stix in 15 min + Q1H ×4, then Q4H if stable

### Follow-up

- Down-titrate DM medications
- Recurrent hypoglycaemia: consult endocrine for drug titration/workup
