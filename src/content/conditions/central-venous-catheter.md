- **Types**: temporary HD catheters (jugular/femoral), tunnelled catheters (Hickmann), PICCs (arm vein), port-a-cath

- **Common indications**: blood taking (haematology patients), drug administration (ICU: inotropes/concentrated electrolytes/prolonged abx), TPN (surgical patients), HD (renal patients)

- **Tasks**: confirm CVC position; take line blood; off central line

- **CXR after central line insertion**: look for PTX; CVC tip at SVC/RA junction; femoral lines: tip in common femoral vein

- **Blood taking technique**:
  - Stop IVF for 3min/TPN for 5min before; aseptic technique per lumen
  - 4 syringes for aerobic/anaerobic/NS flush/heparin flush; 10ml NS, 5ml heparin
  - Rub hub 15s with chlorhexidine; discard first 5ml if not taking blood c/st
  - Flush with NS push-pause-push-pause (turbulent flow); then flush with heparin creating positive pressure
  - Close clamp; apply disinfecting cap

- **Paired blood c/st tips**: equal volume for all sets; clearly label which lumen

- **Off CVC (jugular or femoral) or arterial sheath**:
  - Head down position if jugular/lie flat if femoral (elevate bed for your back)
  - Remove anchoring stitches (usually 3)
  - Remove under Valsalva/end inspiration/end expiration; remove in one go, check tip intact
  - **IMMEDIATELY press on exit site AND more proximal site** where sheath enters vessel
  - Press 15-20 min for haemostasis; pressure dressing; document all; obs Q1H ×4
  - **Beware**: arterial sheath can cause rapid/spurting blood loss if pressure not properly applied
