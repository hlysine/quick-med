## Important Cut-offs

- **Hb <7.0 g/dL** = transfuse
- **Hb drop ≥1.0 g/dL within 1–2 days** = assess for bleeding

---

## Approach to Hb Drop

- Check history: major OT, charted bleeding episodes
- Abdominal exam + PR for GI bleeding
- Check drain output + wound dressing in post-op patients
- Repeat **CBC + clotting** mane
- If continued drop:
  - Transfuse to keep **Hb >7**, **Plt >50**, **INR <1.5**
  - W/H antiplatelets (urgent consult cardiac if on **DAPT**)
  - W/H ± reverse anticoagulation

---

## Transfusion Triggers

| Indication | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Routine | Hb <7.0 g/dL |
| Ongoing blood loss | Any Hb |
| Symptomatic anaemia | Any Hb |

---

## Transfusion Order

- **1 unit red cell Q4H per unit**
  - More if severe/ongoing loss
  - Faster rate Q1–2H if haemodynamically unstable
- **Post-transfusion:** CBC + RFT (transfusion can cause/exacerbate hyperK)
- **Post-transfusion Lasix** to prevent TACO:
  - IV Lasix 20–40mg; omit if SBP <110
  - 1 dose per pint if >1U transfused
  - If APO before transfusion: IV Lasix 40–60mg **pre-transfusion**
- **CAPD patients:** extra 4.25% PDF Q2H instead of Lasix
- **HD patients:** transfuse during next HD session

## Special Blood Products

### Leucodepleted
- Recurrent febrile non-haemolytic reactions (≥2 consecutive)
- Thalassaemia major
- Haematological disease
- Paediatric oncology
- CMV prevention (HIV patients, CMV sero-negative)

### CMV Negative
- Elective transfusion in pregnancy (**not** during labour/delivery/miscarriage/TOP)
- CMV negative allogeneic HSCT recipients
- Intrauterine transfusion
- Neonates BW <1.5 kg
- **Do not withhold lifesaving transfusion** if CMV negative unavailable; leucodepleted blood is an acceptable alternative

### Irradiated (to prevent transfusion-associated GVHD)
- Severe congenital cellular immunodeficiency
- Allogeneic HSCT
- Autologous HSCT (7 days before harvest to 3 months post-transplant)
- CAR-T therapy
- Purine analogs (fludarabine, cladribine, clofarabine, bendamustine) — until **12 months** after completion
- Anti-T cell treatment (anti-thymocyte globulin, alemtuzumab) — from initiation until **12 months** after
- Intrauterine transfusion
- Neonates who had prior intrauterine transfusion (until 6 months of age)

---

## Pitfalls

- **Correct hyperK first** before transfusing
- Transfusion **contraindicated** in hyperleukocytosis (WCC 3-digit) — consult MO first

---

## Key Facts

- Each unit of red cell raises Hb by **~1 g/dL**
- If Hb doesn't rise after transfusion: **reassess for ongoing bleeding**
