# Desaturation

> **No strict definition, but usually nurses will inform you when SpO2 <92%**

## Causes
* **Upper airways**
    * Airway obstruction
    * Sputum retention
    * Aspiration
* **Lungs**
    * PTX
    * Pleural effusion/collapse
    * Pneumonia
    * Exacerbation of underlying lung disease (asthma/COPD/ILD/bronchiectasis)
* **Cardiovascular**
    * PE
    * ACS
    * Tamponade
    * APO
* **Neurological** (hypoventilation/apnoea)

> **Verbal order:** vitals, up-titrate O2, sputum suction, **CXR**, (ECG)

## How to determine the URGENCY?
* **Desat is the next most urgent complaint after arrest**
* **Degree** of desat and **latest SpO2**
    * Ask for *exact SpO2* and O2 requirement (baseline and current): \_% on \_L -> \_% on \_L
    * 0 -> 2L is minor; 0 -> 6L or 6 -> 10L are major
    * Attend immediately if latest SpO2 is still <88% despite up-titrating O2!
* What is the BP and HR?
* Is the patient **alert**?

> Sometimes nurses **empirically** give excess O2 when seeing the patient in respiratory distress, so –  
> 1) there may not have been a genuine desat, 2) the degree of desat may be exaggerated.  
> -> you may try to down-titrate O2 if SpO2 >97% when you arrive.

## First step: Titrate O2 (*usually done by nurse, but check it's correctly given!*)
* SpO2 target: >92% is reasonable (*based on the Hb-O2 dissociation curve*)
    * Aim 88-92% if COPD (*lost hypercapnic drive so depend on hypoxic drive to ventilate*)
* **Nasal cannula:** 0-6L (*no FiO2 gain beyond 6L, and significant leak*) -> FiO2 0.24-0.4
* **Simple Hudson mask:** 5-10L (*rebreathe CO2 if <5L; no FiO2 gain beyond 10L*) -> FiO2 0.35-0.5
* **Non-rebreathing mask:** 10-15L (*high enough O2 flow to fully inflate reservoir bag*) -> FiO2 0.7-1.0
* Venturi mask: can directly set a near-fixed FiO2 (*but seldom used since aerosol generating*)
* **If still significant desat on 15L O2: give 100% O2** (turn knob beyond 15L) **via BVM (Ambubag)**
    * FiO2 1.0 only if tight seal with E-C clamp technique and reservoir bag fully inflated
    * **Provide breaths if hypoventilation/apneic:** RR 12-16, TV 300-500ml (1/4 bag)
    * If high resistance on ventilation: **suspect airway obstruction!**
    * **Avoid intubating on your own;** do airway maneuvers + BVM until Anes arrives!
* **If require >=10L: call MO** to discuss intubation/PAP/DNACPR with patient family
    * **Invasive ventilation**
        * Typical initial ventilator settings: SIMV mode; RR 12-16, TV **6-8ml/kg ideal body weight** (~400-500ml), FiO2 titrate down from 1.0 keep SpO2 >92%, PEEP 5; I:E 1:2 (1:3 in obstructive airway, 1:1.5 in poor compliance e.g. ARDS)
        * Typical sedation: Dormicum 45mg in 45ml NS start at 1ml/hr, W/H if SBP <90; titrate by +/- 1ml/hr
        * Repeat ABG 1hr after initiation to adjust settings
    * **BiPAP for T2RF with respiratory acidosis** (check C/I for NIV!)
        * Typical initial BiPAP settings: IPAP 14 EPAP 4 RR 14 FiO2 0.4
        * (RR is just for backup as patient on BiPAP should be conscious)
        * Repeat ABG 1hr after initiation to adjust settings
    * **CPAP for APO or OSA** (keep airways & alveoli open) (check C/I for NIV!)
    * **High-flow nasal cannula (HFNC) for T1RF**
        * If desat: flow rate start at 60L/min (equal to peak inspiratory flow)
        * Typical settings: FiO2 0.3-0.6, flow 30-60L/min, temp 34C

> **C/I for NIV (need intubation):**
> * **A:** fixed upper airway obstruction, excessive secretions
> * **B:** frank/impending respiratory arrest, undrained PTX
> * **C:** hypotension, arrhythmia, cardiac arrest
> * **D:** GCS < 8 (unable to protect airway)
> * **E:** facial trauma/burns

## Assessment

### Bedside
* Vitals, **GCS**
* Signs of **airway obstruction**: stridor/inability to speak, absent air flow, high resistance on ventilation
* **History of choking** (aspiration pneumonia), **chest discomfort**, cough/sputum, haemoptysis
* **Respiratory exam (note asymmetry):** trachea, chest rise, breath sounds +/- added sounds
* **Calves** (soft, non-tender?)
* **Fluid status**

### Patient records
* History of **cardiac** (CHF), **pulmonary** (CHF, asthma/COPD, ILD, bronchiectasis, CA) or **neuro** disease
* **Current medical problems** and work-up performed before – **blood tests, ECG, imaging (e.g. CXR)**

## Initial management
* **NPOEM** (and W/H DM meds during fasting) if significant desat or suspect aspiration
* Prop up (*improves V/Q matching*), sputum suction prn
* 0-\_L O2 prn, keep SpO2 >92% (88-92% in COPD) (*see above for O2 therapy*)
* **Monitoring**
    * BP/P/SpO2 Q1H (or obs Q1H x4 then Q4H if stable)
    * Continuous SpO2 monitor
* **Investigations**
    * **CXR (must order)**
    * ECG (for PE, ACS)
    * Bld x CBC d/c, L/RFT, TnI/CK, clotting
        * D dimer seldom taken as low specificity; usually MO orders if plan to book CTPA
        * **ABG** indicated if significant desat or T2RF suspected (**↓ GCS or hypoventilation or hx of asthma/COPD**)
    * Microbiological workup
        * Sputum x c/st (+/- AFB smear/PCR/c/st, cytology if suspicious e.g. haemoptysis)
        * NPS x resp viruses (e.g. flu, nCoV)
        * Urine x pneumococcal and legionella antigen (for CAPD)
    * Bedside spirometry for neurological causes (early ventilation if FVC <15ml/kg!)
    * Consider CT thorax with contrast (or CTPA) for respiratory failure with unknown cause

> When reviewing CXR for desat case: **look for PTX**, pleural effusion, collapse, consolidation, APO; **for desat unexplained by CXR, think PE!**

* **Treat underlying cause\*\***
    * **Anaphylaxis:** **IM adrenaline 1:1000 0.5ml stat** +/- repeated dose, remove allergen, IV hydrocortisone 100mg Q6-8H, IV Piriton 10mg Q8H, **label allergy and discontinue allergen**
        * **AVOID sedation!!** Need awake fibreoptic intubation / tracheostomy / cricothyroidotomy
    * **Aspiration:** consult speech therapist x swallowing assessment, sputum x c/st, antibiotics
        * If +dysarthria/dysphonia: **suspect bulbar/pseudobulbar palsy** (can be stroke!)
    * **Sputum plugging/atelectasis:** chest physiotherapy (sputum drainage; incentive spirometry)
    * **PTX:** chest drain (*BTS algorithm: indicated for all symptomatic PTX*) +/- pleurodesis
        * Ensure Plt >50, INR <1.5 before chest drain insertion
        * **C/I for ventilation before chest drain insertion**
    * **Massive pleural effusion** (with lung whiteout/mediastinal shift): pleural tap +/- pigtail
        * Ensure Plt >50, INR <1.5 before tapping/pigtail; post CXR to exclude iatrogenic PTX!
        * Send pleural fluid x cell count, TP, LDH, pH, glucose, ADA, AFB, TB culture, c/st, cytology (with paired bld x LFT, LDH for Light's criteria)
    * **APO: off IVF (even if NPO)**, FR <1.2L/day + low salt diet (if not NPO), IV Lasix, W/H BB/ACEI
        * IV Lasix 40mg stat + 20mg Q12H (W/H if SBP <100)
        * Can try 40mg Q12H if renal failure, 20mg Q8H if heart failure
        * On PD: extra 4.25% PDF Q2H x 1-2 doses (*no extra benefit from using Icodextrin or a rate quicker than Q2H*); + IP Actrapid 4-8U/bag if DM
        * On HD: call MO for need to arrange early/urgent HD
    * **Chest infection:** microbiological workup (as above), start/step up antibiotics
        * Check previous c/st results
        * Mild/moderate CAP: IV/PO Augmentin + PO doxycycline (PO levofloxacin for elderly)
        * HAP/severe CAP/bronchiectasis: IV Tazocin (fail Tazocin: to Meronem)
        * Quinolone (levo/cipro) if beta lactam allergy
        * Immunocompromised: consider TB, PCP, HIV, Candida
    * **Wheeze:** Ventolin 4 puffs Q4H prn, Atrovent 2 puffs Q4H prn (do not exceed 12 puffs/day)
    * **Asthma/COPD exacerbation**
        * Ventolin 4 puffs up to Q1H prn + stat via MDI with spacer
            * Monitor for **tachycardia and HTN**; RFT, VBG Q6H x4
        * Atrovent 2 puffs Q4H prn + stat (do not exceed 12 puffs/day)
        * IV hydrocortisone 100mg Q6H + stat or PO prednisolone 40mg daily + stat
            * H'stix BD, Pantoloc 40mg daily, entecavir if HBsAg +ve
        * Asthma: + IV MgSO4 1.2-2g over 20min
        * COPD: + IV/PO Augmentin if >= 2 symptoms (↑ SOB / sputum volume/purulence) or need ventilatory support
        * ABG; ventilatory support if respiratory acidosis
    * **ILD exacerbation:** discontinue offending drugs; IV pulse steroids for CTD-ILD
    * **PE:** SC enoxaparin 1mg/kg Q12H (Q24H if CrCl <30ml/min) + stat **if no C/I**
        * If in obstructive shock: refer to section on hypotension
    * **Haemoptysis**
        * Gentle suction prn; position affected side down
        * Chart haemoptysis
        * W/H antiplatelets (urgent consult cardiac if on DAPT), W/H +/- reverse anticoagulation
        * Transamin (**C/I in active thrombosis** e.g. PE/DVT/DIC /ACS/stroke)
        * Bld x CBC, clotting; transfuse to keep Plt > 50, INR < 1.5
        * Sputum x AFB, TB PCR, TB culture, cytology x3
        * ECG (for PE)
        * Massive haemoptysis:
            * Hemodynamic support, T&S and transfusion as needed
            * Urgent consult Anes x intubation with lung isolation
            * Urgent CTA +/- bronchial artery embolization
            * +/- urgent consult CTS x rigid bronchoscopy/lung resection

> **Desat due to benzodiazepine:** 1) stop BZD, 2) r/o anaphylaxis, 3) airway manoeuvres and bag with BVM, 4) +/- IV flumazenil 0.2mg Q1min (up to 4 times), obs Q20min x3 for re-sedation  
> *Note: flumazenil is **seldom given** for short-acting BZD like Dormicum (offset ~30min), as it may cause seizure (tricky to manage as you cannot give further BZD)*

> **Desat due to opioid** (may have **pinpoint pupil** if overdose): 1) stop opioid, 2) r/o anaphylaxis, 3) airway manoeuvres and bag with BVM, 4) IV/IM/SC/ET naloxone 0.1-0.2mg Q2-3min, obs Q1H for re-narcotisation (naloxone onset 1-2min, half-life 1-1.5hr)

## Special Situation 1: Airway Obstruction

### Causes:
* Functional: decreased consciousness (including OSA), bilateral VC palsy (neurological, recurrent laryngeal nerve injury)
* Mechanical: trauma, FB (vomit/blood), anaphylaxis, infection, tumour, goitre, stenosis

### Signs of airway obstruction: **stridor/inability to speak, absent air flow, high resistance on ventilation**
* *Stridor may be absent in complete airway obstruction; desat is late sign*

1. **Airway maneuvers:** head tilt chin lift (C/I in C spine injury), jaw thrust
    * If just due to decreased consciousness, place in recovery position
    * FB: if conscious, encourage cough, abdominal thrust; if unconscious, retrieve with Magill forceps
2. **Airway adjuncts:** oropharyngeal airway (OPA) or nasopharyngeal airway (NPA)
    * Unconscious (loss of gag reflex) -> OPA [size: corner of mouth to angle of mandible]
    * Semi-conscious -> NPA (C/I in skull base #) [size: nostril to angle of mandible]
3. **Advanced airway interventions**
    * Call Anes for intubation; avoid intubating on your own, focus on airway maneuvers and bagging with FiO2 1.0 instead
    * Needle cricothyroidotomy only for emergency subglottic obstruction (cannot ventilate and cannot intubate) where tracheostomy set or ENT is not available

## Special Situation 2: Tracheostomy Emergency

### 1. Call for help, give 100% O2
* Down-titrate to 4L (max 6L) as tolerated (*high flow stimulates secretions -> blocks tube*)

### 2. Identify temporary vs permanent – **bag at the correct site!**
* For **temporary** tracheostomy: cover tracheostomy, **ventilate via nose and mouth** (*if displaced/dislodged, ventilating via tracheostomy may induce subcutaneous emphysema and exacerbate airway obstruction!*)
* For **permanent** tracheostoma (i.e. post-laryngectomy): **ventilate via stoma** using tracheostomy mask / size 3 (paed) LMA (**DO NOT OCCLUDE THE STOMA as it is the only airway opening!**)

* **Tube dislodgement**
    * Check **date** of tracheostomy insertion
    * If tract well-formed (takes 3-5 days after surgical tracheostomy, 7-10 days after percutaneous tracheostomy): reinsert tracheostomy with aid of tracheal dilator
        * Check position: moisture, sputum, pass suction catheter without resistance, direct visualisation with flexible laryngoscope
    * If tract not well formed: call ENT to reinsert (*as false tract can be fatal!*), ventilate via nose and mouth for temporary, via stoma for permanent
* **Tube occlusion**
    * Do not bag through it (may cause distal dislodgement worsening airway)
    * If double lumen, remove inner tube; if not relieved, replace with non-fenestrated tube
    * **Pass suction catheter to check patency** (unable to advance beyond few cm means blocked) & attempt to remove obstruction
    * Prop up, encourage coughing
    * If persist/LOC: deflate cuff and ventilate via nose and mouth for temporary, via stoma for permanent; if needed, remove tracheostomy tube
* **Bleeding**
    * **Temporarily hyperinflate cuff** to prevent aspiration
    * Bleeding from stoma: direct pressure with absorbent pads
    * Bleeding from lung (aspirate blood from suction catheter): increase FiO2, gentle suction, urgent consult CTS

## Special Situation 3: Desat on Ventilator
* **Give 100% O2 boost** (no air mix if portable ventilator), find cause

### 1. Circuit problem (block/kink: high system pressure; leak: low system pressure)
* Check **ETT/TT position:** 5-point auscultation, symmetrical chest rise, CXR
    * ETT/TT malposition: call anes/ENT; do not shift on your own
* **ETT/TT blockage (e.g. sputum, biting):** resistance on passing suction catheter
    * Tx: sputum suction, device to prevent biting on tube
    * Persistent tube occlusion: deflate cuff and ventilate **via nose and mouth for ETT / temporary TT**, via stoma for permanent TT
    * Extubate if fail to ventilate
* Ask nurses to measure **ETT/TT cuff pressure** (low -> leakage/dislodgement)
* **Check connections of all tubing** from oxygen source to patient (dislodgement, leakage, kinking)
* If the cap on CO2 capnography port is unscrewed, air leak will occur

### 2. Patient problem
* Follow desat algorithm above
* Ensure symmetrical AE & chest rise: **exclude PTX, ETT/TT malposition**
* If due to **apnea** on pressure support mode: switch to controlled mode / SIMV
* **T1RF:** increase FiO2, PEEP (*preserves functional residual capacity to allow more alveoli to participate in gas exchange during end expiration*)
    * PEEP too high compromises systemic and cerebral venous return
* **T2RF:** increase MV (RR x TV)
    * RR too high may create iatrogenic air trapping; ensure expiratory phase of flow-time curve reaches baseline!
    * TV too high may cause barotrauma; keep plateau pressure <30mmHg!
* **PTX:** chest drain; reduce ventilation pressure (e.g. reduce TV)
* **High pressure alarm** (low lung compliance or high airway resistance): reduce TV, increase RR
* **Patient-ventilator dyssynchrony:** increase sedation

### 3. Ventilator problem
* Disconnect ventilator and manual ventilation via BVM transiently; if SpO2 recovers, means it is ventilator problem; change to another ventilator or change settings
