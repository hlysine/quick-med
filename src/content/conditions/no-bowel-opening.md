## Definition

- Nurses inform if **NBO >3 days**

## Causes

- Intestinal obstruction (IO)
- Ileus
- Constipation

## Assessment — Red Flags for IO

- NBO days; any flatus?
- Abdominal pain / N/V
- Eating well?
- Abdomen: soft, tenderness
- ± Hyperactive / tinkling bowel sounds

## Management of Constipation

> If suspicious of IO: **AXR first** to rule out IO before laxatives

### Oral Laxatives

| Drug | Type |
|---|---|
| Senna 7.5–15mg | Stimulant |
| Lactulose 10–15ml/dose | Osmotic |
| Docusate | Stool softener |
| PO Dulcolax | Stimulant |
| Metamucil 3×5 spoonfuls | Bulk forming |

### PR Laxatives

- **Dulcolax suppository 10mg** prn ± stat
- If above fails: increase laxative type/dose/frequency
- **Fleet enema** (osmotic)
- Digital rectal evacuation

### Treat Underlying Cause

- Mobilisation, fluid/fibre
- Treat electrolytes: hypoK, hypoMg, hyperCa, hypothyroidism
- Off antidiarrhoeal / anticholinergic / opioid / iron supplement

## Management of IO

### Presentation

- **Obstipation** (NBO + no flatus)
- Abdominal pain + distension ± vomiting
- Dilated bowels on imaging

### Management

- **Strict NPO**, W/H DM meds, maintenance IVF
- W/H all laxatives / opioids / anticholinergics
- **R/T to BSB**, aspirate Q4H
- Urgent AXR (+ erect CXR if suspect perforation)
- **Urgent CT A+P** for mechanical cause
- **Urgent consult SUR**

## AXR Dilated Bowel Criteria (3-6-9 Rule)

| Bowel Segment | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Small bowel | >3 cm |
| Large bowel | >6 cm |
| Caecum | >9 cm |

- Also look for: **rectal gas** and **Rigler's sign** (double wall sign) for perforation
