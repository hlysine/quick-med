# No Bowel Opening (NBO)

> **Nurses may inform you if NBO x >3 days, or as requested by the patients**

## Causes
IO, ileus, constipation

## Assessment (for red flags of IO)
* NBO x how many days?
* Any flatus?
* Any abd pain/N/V? Eat well?
* Abdomen soft? Any tenderness?
* +/- any hyperactive/tinkling bowel sounds?

> **3-6-9 criteria for dilated bowel on AXR:**
> * Small bowel diameter >3 cm
> * Large bowel diameter >6 cm
> * Cecum diameter >9 cm
>
> Also look for **rectal gas** and watch out for **Rigler's (double wall) sign** which indicates perforation!

> Dilated bowels != mechanical obstruction
> Possible **pseudo-IO** (esp bedbound patients!)
> **Check bowel sounds, and rectal gas on AXR!**

## Management of Constipation
* **If suspicious of IO: AXR x1 to rule out IO before prescribing any laxatives**
* **Oral laxatives**
    * **Senna** ("草餅" – stimulant) 7.5mg or 15mg
    * **Lactulose** ("乳果糖" – osmotic) 10-15ml per dose
    * Less often used (slow effect)
        * Docusate (stool softener)
        * PO Dulcolax (stimulant)
        * Metamucil (bulk forming): 3 x 5 spoonfuls
* **PR laxatives**
    * Dulcolax suppository (D/S – stimulant) prn +/- stat (if NBO >3/7) 10mg per dose
* **If above fails:**
    * ↑ laxative type/dose/frequency
    * Fleet enema (F/E – osmotic)
    * Digital rectal evacuation
* **Treat underlying cause:** encourage mobilisation, fluid and fibre intake; treat electrolytes e.g. hypoK/ hypoMg/ hyperCa/ hypothyroid, ↓ antidiarrhoeal/anticholinergic/opioid/Fe supplement

## Management of IO
> **IO presentation:** obstipation (i.e. NBO + no flatus), abd pain + distension, +/- vomiting, dilated bowels

* **Strict NPO, W/H DM meds** during fasting, maintenance IVF
* **W/H all laxatives, opioids, anticholinergics**
* **R/T to BSB, asp Q4H**
* **Urgent AXR** (+ erect CXR if suspect perforation)
* **Urgent CT A+P** for mechanical cause (inform MO!)
* **Urgent consult SUR** (inform MO!)
