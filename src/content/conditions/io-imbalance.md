---
title: I/O Imbalance
section: Ward Complaints
keywords:
  - fluid balance
  - input output
  - fluid overload
  - dehydration
---

- **Definition**: nurses inform if daily input-output difference >500ml

- **Causes**:
  - Input > output: oliguria, fluid resuscitation
  - Output > input: diuresis, diarrhoea, high drain/stoma output, poor oral intake

- **Complications**:
  - I>O: fluid overload, APO
  - O>I: dehydration, AKI

- **Assessment**: vitals, fluid status; I/O chart over past few days (persistent +ve/-ve balance?); RFT

- **Management**:
  - Unintended I>O: off IVF if excess; treat oliguria (e.g. AKI, AROU); monitor fluid status
  - Unintended O>I: encourage fluid intake; IVF; spigot ascitic drain after 5L/day; treat diarrhoea/diuresis/high drain/stoma output; monitor RFT

- **Note**: I/O charting only meaningful if daily difference >1L; do not balance out if I/O difference is part of therapeutic aim (e.g. IVF for shock, diuretics for APO)
