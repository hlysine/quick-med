# I/O Imbalance

> **Nurses may inform you if daily input-output difference >500ml**

## Causes
* **Input > output:** oliguria, fluid resus
* **Output > input:** diuresis, diarrhoea, high drain/stoma output, poor oral intake

> In reality, I/O charting is crude and often inaccurate, so only meaningful if daily I/O difference > 1L

> **Pitfall:** sometimes the I/O difference is **part of the therapeutic aim!**
> e.g. IVF to treat shock / tachycardia / dehydration / hyperNa
> e.g. diuretics/drainage to treat APO / fluid overload / effusion
> In such cases, **do not balance out the I/O** (will defeat the MO's effort)!

## Complications
* I > O: fluid overload, APO
* O > I: dehydration, AKI

## Assessment
* Vitals, **fluid status**
* I/O chart over past few days: is it persistent +ve/-ve balance?
* **RFT**

> If want closer I/O monitoring without catheterising, can consider **daily body weight**; in males, can use **Paul's tube!**

## Management
* **Unintended I > O:**
    * ↓ IVF
    * Treat oliguria (e.g. AKI, AROU)
    * Monitor fluid status
* **Unintended O > I:**
    * Encourage fluid intake
    * ↑ IVF
    * Spigot ascitic drain after 5L drained/day
    * Treat diarrhoea / diuresis / high drain/stoma output
    * Monitor RFT
