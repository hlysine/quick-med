## Causes

- **Infection**: bacteria, viruses, tinea, scabies
- **Drug reaction**: allergy, DRESS, SJS/TEN
- **Autoimmune**: connective tissue disease (CTD)

## Assessment

- **Vitals**
- Onset and progression
- Distribution; mucosal involvement
- **Morphology**: macular / papular / maculopapular / urticarial / petechial / vesicular / pustular / bullous
- Associated symptoms: fever, SOB, angioedema, pruritus, joint pain

## Management

### Anaphylaxis

- **IM adrenaline 1:1000 0.5 ml** — repeat as needed
- Remove allergen
- **IV hydrocortisone 100 mg Q6–8H**
- **IV Piriton (chlorphenamine) 10 mg Q8H**
- Label allergy
- **AVOID sedation**
- If persistent airway problem: awake fibreoptic intubation / tracheostomy / cricothyroidotomy

### Generalised Allergic Rash (no anaphylaxis)

- **IV hydrocortisone 100 mg Q8H** + stat dose
- **IV Piriton 10 mg Q8H** + stat dose

### Drug Reaction

- **Stop suspected offending drug(s)**
- **Suspected / confirmed drug allergy**: label in CMS alert; review new drugs given in past day
- **SJS/TEN**: any drug in recent **8 weeks** may be the culprit

### Cellulitis

- Mark erythema margins; antibiotics (e.g. Augmentin)
- **Drip-site cellulitis**: remove heparin lock; add antibiotics if severe

### Vesicular Rash

- Vesicular fluid / swab × HSV 1/2, VZV
- Antiviral: acyclovir / valacyclovir / ganciclovir

### Scabies

- Pruritic rash in skinfolds, periumbilical, genitalia, buttock
- Skin scraping × scabies; **permethrin cream**

### Tinea / Onychomycosis

- Ring-shaped rash / nail changes
- Azole cream: clotrimazole / isoconazole / itraconazole

### Perineal / Perianal / Skin Care

- Perineal/perianal rash: **zinc cream**
- Dry skin: Vaseline or aqueous cream
- Pruritus: **Eurax cream** + Piriton
- Bruise: **Hirudoid**

### Consult Dermatology

- Consult DERM for complex, persistent, or uncertain rashes
