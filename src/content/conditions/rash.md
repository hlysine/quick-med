# Rash

> Sorry, don't know DERM >< but:

## Causes
* Infection: bacteria, viruses, tinea, scabies etc.
* Drug reaction: allergy (e.g. anaphylaxis, urticaria), DRESS, SJS/TEN
* Autoimmune: connective tissue diseases

> **Suspected/confirmed drug allergy/reaction: label in CMS alert!**
> Suspected allergy: review any **NEW** drugs given in the past day
> Suspected SJS/TEN: any drug in **recent 8 weeks** can be culprit!

## Assessment
* Vitals
* Onset, progression
* Distribution, **mucosal involvement**
* Morphology: macular/ papular/ MP/ urticarial/ petechial/ vesicular/ pustular/ bullous
* Associated symptoms: **fever, SOB, angioedema**, pruritis, joint pain

## Management
* **Anaphylaxis: IM adrenaline 1:1000 0.5ml stat +/- repeated dose, remove allergen**, IV hydrocortisone 100mg Q6-8H, IV Piriton 10mg Q8H, **label allergy and discontinue allergen**
    * *AVOID sedation!!* If airway problem persist after IM adrenaline -> consult anes/ENT x awake fibreoptic intubation / tracheostomy / cricothyroidotomy
* **Allergy with generalised rash (but no anaphylaxis): IV hydrocortisone 100mg Q8H + stat (check HBsAg, monitor H'stix, + PO Pantoloc), IV Piriton 10mg Q8H + stat**
* **Stop suspected offending drugs**
* Cellulitis: **mark erythema or clinical photo** (since ddx is NF), antibiotics (e.g. Augmentin)
* Drip site cellulitis: remove the heparin lock (venous access), + abx if severe (e.g. large area, discharge)
* **Consult DERM**
* Vesicular rash: vesicular fluid/swab x HSV1/2, VZV; acyclovir/valacyclovir/ganciclovir
* Pruritic rash in skinfolds, periumbilical, genitalia, buttock: skin scraping x scabies, permethrin cream
* Ring-shaped rash/onychomycosis (tinea): azole cream (e.g. clotrimazole/isoconazole/itraconazole)
* Perineal/perianal rash: zinc cream
* Dry skin: Vaseline (paraffin soft white) or aqueous cream
* Pruritis: Eurax cream, Piriton
* Bruise: Hirudoid
