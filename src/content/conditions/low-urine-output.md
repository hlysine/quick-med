---
title: Low Urine Output
section: Ward Complaints
keywords:
  - oliguria
  - AKI
  - urinary retention
  - foley
  - blocked catheter
---

- **Definition**: oliguria = U/O <0.5ml/kg/hr; nurses inform if NPU ×2 (dry napkin for 8hrs)

- **Causes**: AROU, blocked foley/PCN, AKI, anuric ESRF, dehydration, poor renal perfusion (shock)

- **Assessment**: vitals, fluid status; urinary symptoms (AROU, UTI); foley tubing and BSB (kinking/blockage, debris/turbid/haematuria); Cr baseline and trend

- **Management**:
  - Chart I/O; blood x RFT; PVRU ×1 (if on foley: bladder scan + flush)
  - If not fluid overloaded: fluid challenge (NS Q4H ×1)
  - Treat underlying cause (refer to respective sections)

- **Blocked foley** (BS despite foley): ask nurse to flush foley; change foley if fail to flush; monitor for complications (UTI, obstructive uropathy, post-obstructive diuresis)

- If gross haematuria with clot retention: refer to haematuria section
