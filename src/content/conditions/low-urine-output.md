# Low Urine Output (U/O)

> **Oliguria is defined as U/O < 0.5 ml/kg/hr**
> **In practice, nurses may inform you if NPU x2 (兩轉冇尿 i.e. dry napkin for 8hrs)**

## Causes of Oliguria
* **AROU**
* **Blocked foley/PCN**
* **AKI**
* Anuric ESRF
* **Dehydration**
* Poor renal perfusion e.g. **shock**

> **Total urine output** = amount on napkin/foley + PCN output + ileal conduit output (if present)

> When informed of NPU, **order a bladder scan**; if nil output on foley, **order to flush foley**

## Assessment
* **Vitals, fluid status**
* Urinary symptoms: AROU, UTI
* Foley tubing and BSB: Visible kinking/blockage? Debris/turbid urine/haematuria?
* **Cr baseline and trend**

## Management
* Chart I/O
* **Bld x RFT**
* **PVRU x1 (if on foley: bladder scan + flush foley)**
* If not fluid overloaded: **fluid challenge e.g. NS Q4H x1**
* Treat underlying cause (refer to respective sections!)

## Blocked Foley (BS ↑↑ despite on Foley)
* Ask nurse to flush foley, and change foley if fail to flush
* Monitor for complications (UTI, obstructive uropathy, post-obstructive diuresis) as in AROU section

*If gross haematuria with clot retention – refer to haematuria section!*

> **Tips for difficult foley insertion:**
> * Ask for a **Tiemann foley** (curved tip to navigate the bulbourethral curve, & silicone so tougher)
> * After disinfection, use one hand with a gauze to hold the **penis in erect position**
> * **Squeeze the whole tube of xylocaine jelly into the urethra** (leave some to lubricate foley tip)
> * Use the other hand to insert the Tiemann, with the **curved tip pointing superiorly/ up**
> * Confirm tip is inside bladder by seeing urine outflow -> **only then inflate foley balloon with 10ml water for injection (NOT NS!)** (*don't force it; difficult foley insertion may cause false tracts!*)
> * If fail: try a bigger Fr if BPH, smaller Fr if stricture/CA, call MO/SUR/URO (may need cystoscopy guide)
