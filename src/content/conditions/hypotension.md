# Hypotension

> **No strict definition, but usually refer to when SBP < 90**

## Causes – 4 types of shock

| Type             | Causes                                                                              | Heart rate                                                                                            | JVP         | Peripheries |
|------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------|-------------|
| **Cardiogenic**  | Arrhythmia<br/>ACS<br/>Congestive&nbsp;heart&nbsp;failure<br/>Pericarditis/effusion | **Heart failure**<br>Moderate&nbsp;tachycardia<br>**Arrhythmia**<br>Severe bradycardia or tachycardia | High/normal | Cold        |
| **Hypovolemic**  | Hemorrhage<br>Renal<br/>Extra-renal<br/>Third-spacing                               | Moderate tachycardia                                                                                  | Low         | Cold        |
| **Distributive** | Sepsis<br>Anaphylaxis<br/>Addisonian&nbsp;crisis                                    | Moderate tachycardia                                                                                  | Low         | Warm        |
| **Obstructive**  | Massive PE<br>Cardiac tamponade<br>Tension&nbsp;pneumothorax                        | Moderate tachycardia                                                                                  | Very high   | Cold        |

## How to determine the URGENCY?

* How **low** is the BP? What is the **baseline** BP?
* Is there **tachycardia**? Is the patient **alert**? Any **symptoms**?
* Basic information about the patient – age, PMH, admission reason, DNACPR case?
  * DNACPR cases may specify **not for inotropes +/- not for fluid challenge** [respect their wishes]
* Is he on any antihypertensives? Were the medications given?

> **Before you arrive, you can verbal order over the phone:**
> 1. **ECG, CXR** (can verbal order in some hospitals only)
> 2. **IVF – how much?** Ask the nurse:
     >
* `<PMH>` 有無心底/腎底 (cardiac/renal disease)?
>    * `<Fluid status>` 腫唔腫? On O2?
>    * How much IVF already given?
     >
     >    -> if doubt over fluid tolerance: 250ml full rate/Q1H; otherwise: 500ml full rate

## Assessment

### Bedside

* **Vitals** (make the good habit to **recheck**, also pay attention to **HR & RR**)
* **GCS/alertness**
* **Chest pain/SOB** (suggest cardiogenic/obstructive shock)
* **JVP** (elevated JVP in cardiogenic/obstructive shock)
* **Peripheries** (warm peripheries in distributive e.g. septic shock)
* **Calves** (soft, non-tender?)
* **Fluid status** (air entry/basal crep, LL edema; vs decreased skin turgor, dry mucous membranes)
* **Any ongoing fluid or blood loss** (e.g. abd exam & PR, **drain output**)

### Patient Records

* History of **cardiac** and **renal** diseases (latest Cr and LVEF)
* Any **antihypertensives** / steroids use (Addisonian crisis)
* **Current medical problems** and work-up performed before – **blood tests, ECG, imaging (e.g. CXR)**

## Initial management

* **Monitoring**
  * BP/P/SpO2 Q1H (+ cardiac monitoring if suspect cardiogenic)
    * If mild: obs Q1H x2 or 4 then Q4H if stable (*otherwise nurses will hate you!*)
  * Foley to BSB, chart U/O Q1H or Q4H (oliguria can be an early sign of shock!)
* **Investigations** (as indicated; may not be necessary for mild transient hypotension)
  * ECG (for arrhythmia/ACS/PE/tamponade; needed especially if chest pain/SOB)
  * CXR (if suspect fluid overload)
  * Bloods (Blds)
    * CBC (monitor Hb trend/ WCC)
    * L/RFT (to look for any end-organ failure)
    * Clotting (if suspect bleeding / need for invasive procedures)
    * Cause-specific work-up
      * ACS: ECG, TnI/CK
      * Septic work-up: bld + urine + sputum c/st, CXR, +/- other c/st or imaging
      * Bleeding: may need endoscopy / imaging e.g. CTA (call MO)
      * Addisonian crisis: spot cortisol + paired ACTH, RG, VBG, RFT
* **Fluid resuscitation**
  * Gelofusine or Hartmann’s solution or NS 500ml FR x 1 / Q1H x 1
  * If fluid overload, can try cautiously give 250ml (半枝) first
* **Inotropes** (as indicated, usually when unresponsive to fluid resuscitation e.g. given 1L)<br>
  *In some hospitals, it might be better if you call MO before initiating inotropes*
  * **Dopamine** 200mg in 100ml NS, usually start at 10ml/hr, titrate against BP
    * Titrate by +/- 5ml
    * Theoretically aim MAP > 65 but usually can write aim SBP > 90
  * **Adrenaline** (if dopamine is not enough): 6mg in 100ml NS, usually start at 5ml/hr
    * **Adrenaline 0.1mg (1ml 1:10000) IV bolus if SBP < 50**
    * Some hospitals use phenylephrine instead
  * Noradrenaline is usually reserved in ICU settings (require central line)
* Order to **withhold (W/H) antihypertensives & diuretics if SBP < 110** [add remarks in IPMOE]

* **Treat underlying cause\*\***
  * **Sepsis**
    * Start/step up **antibiotics** based on relevant current/past c/st result<br>
      (empirical: IV Augmentin -> Tazocin -> Meronem)
    * **Prompt source control** (e.g. drainage/debridement)
  * **Addisonian crisis**
    * H’stix Q4H, NS Q1H x 4-6 pints
    * **IV hydrocortisone** 100mg Q8H or 50mg Q6H
  * **Anaphylaxis**
    * **IM adrenaline 1:1000 0.5ml stat** +/- repeated dose
      * **AVOID sedation!!** If airway problem persist after IM adrenaline -> consult anes/ENT x awake fibreoptic
        intubation / tracheostomy / cricothyroidotomy
    * **Remove allergen**
    * IV hydrocortisone 100mg Q6-8H
    * IV Piriton 10mg Q8H
    * **Label allergy and discontinue allergen**
  * **Hypovolemia**
    * Usually fluid resuscitation is enough
    * **Rule out active bleeding!**
  * **Bleeding**
    * **T&S** +/- transfusion
    * **W/H antiplatelets** (urgent consult cardiac if on DAPT)
    * **W/H +/- reverse anticoagulation**
    * Localise and **stop bleeding** (may need to call MO)
  * **CHF**
    * W/H Lasix/TNG if SBP<100
    * W/H BB/ACEI
    * **Treat arrhythmia/ischemia/structural lesion**
  * **Massive PE / cardiac tamponade**
    * **Consult CCU x urgent echo**
    * Thrombolysis/pericardiocentesis
  * **Tension PTX**
    * Immediate (**do not wait for CXR**) **needle aspiration**<br>
      16-18G at 2nd intercostal space mid-clavicular line
    * Followed by chest drain (and pleurodesis)
