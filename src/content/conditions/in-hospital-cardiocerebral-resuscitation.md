# In-hospital cardiocerebral resuscitation

## BLS

- Check for scene safety
- Check for responsiveness
  - Tap on shoulders and shout
- Call for help
- Check for pulse and breathing for 5-10 seconds
- High quality CPR
  - Chest compressions
    - 100-120 per minute
    - 5-6 cm
    - Full chest recoil between compressions
    - Minimize interruptions to less than 10 seconds
  - Ventilation
    - 2 breaths every 30 compressions
    - Each breath over 1 second with visible chest rise
    - Squeeze 1/2 to 1/3 volume of bag-valve mask
    - Avoid hyperventilation
    - Continuous ventilation at 6 breaths per minute if advanced airway in place

## Defibrillation

- [**Biphasic defibrillation** - 150-200 J (manufacturer recommendation)](../managements/cardioversion-defibrillation)
- [**Monophasic defibrillation** - 360 J](../managements/cardioversion-defibrillation)

## Medications

- [Epinephrine](../managements/epinephrine)
  - 1 mg 1:10,000 IV every every second cycle (every 3-5 minutes)
  - Give immediately for non-shockable rhythms
- [Anti-arrhythmics](../managements/arrest-anti-arrhythmics)
  - **Amiodarone**
    - 300 mg IV bolus after 3rd shock
    - 150 mg IV bolus after 5th shock
  - **Lignocaine**
    - 100 mg IV bolus after 3rd shock
    - 50 mg IV bolus after 5th shock

## Special considerations

- Hypothermia
  - Defer defibrillation and medications until core temperature >30°C
- Opioid overdose
  - [**Empirical naloxone**](../managements/naloxone)
- Pregnancy
  - Left lateral decubitus position
  - Manual left uterine displacement
  - Consider resuscitative delivery if no ROSC after 5 minutes and fundal height above umbilicus
- Drowning
  - Start CPR with rescue breaths first

## Primary assessment

- Airway
  - Open
    - Head-tilt chin-lift
    - Jaw-thrust
  - Clear
    - Suction
    - Remove foreign body
  - Maintain
    - Advanced airway
- Breathing
  - 100% oxygen in cardiac arrest
- Circulation
  - CPR feedback device
  - Continuous waveform capnography
    - <10 mmHg = unlikely ROSC
    - Sudden increase = ROSC
    - Normal = 35-45 mmHg
  - IV/IO access
  - Fluids
  - Temperature
- Disability
  - Neurological function
  - Glucose
  - AVPU - alert, verbal, pain, unresponsive
  - Pupils
- Exposure

## Secondary assessment

### SAMPLE

- Signs and symptoms
- Allergies
- Medications
- Past medical history
- Last meal
- Events leading to arrest

### 5 Hs and 5 Ts

| Cause                  | Investigation                |
|------------------------|------------------------------|
| Hypovolemia            | Examine for bleeding, fluids |
| Hypoxia                | Pulse oximetry               |
| Hydrogen ion           | Arterial blood gas           |
| Hypo/hyperkalemia      | Arterial blood gas           |
| Hypothermia            | Tympanic temperature         |
| Tension pneumothorax   | Examine lungs and trachea    |
| Tamponade              | Echo                         |
| Toxins                 | History and tox screen       |
| Thrombosis (coronary)  | ECG and troponin             |
| Thrombosis (pulmonary) | Echo                         |

### Head-to-toe exam

#### Head

- Low tympanic temperature
  - Hypothermia
- Pinpoint pupils
  - Opioid intoxication
  - [**1.2mg IV naloxone**](../managements/naloxone)
- Unequal pupils
  - Intracranial bleed/stroke
- Blood in mouth
  - GI hemorrhage
- Foreign bodies in airway
  - Upper airway obstruction
- Cyanotic lips
  - Hypoxia

#### Neck

- Tracheal deviation
  - Tension pneumothorax
- Distended jugular veins
  - Tension pneumothorax, pulmonary embolism, cardiac tamponade
- Flat jugular veins
  - Hypovolemia

#### Chest

- Unequal chest expansion/unilateral absence of breath sounds
  - Tension pneumothorax
- Signs of trauma
  - Surgical emphysema

#### Abdomen

- Distension
  - Intra-abdominal bleeding
- Blood in per rectal
  - PR bleeding

#### Groin

- Unilateral loss of femoral pulse
  - Aortic rupture
- Femoral vein pits
  - IVDU
  - [**Empirical naloxone**](../managements/naloxone)

#### Lower limbs

- Swelling
  - DVT + PE
- Necrosis
  - Necrotizing fasciitis

## Post-resuscitation care

- Airway
  - Assess airway
  - Establish advanced airway if necessary
- Breathing
  - Target SpO2 90-98%
- Circulation
  - Target MAP >=65 mmHg
  - [IV fluids](../managements/fluids.md) or [vasopressors](../managements/vasopressors.md)
- Diagnostics
  - 12-lead ECG
  - Diagnostic imaging as indicated
- Treat underlying cause
- Ongoing critical care
- Coronary angiography as indicated
- Assess patient, if unresponsive
    - Temperature control
      - Maintain between 32-37.5°C for at least 36 hours
    - EEG
    - Prognostication
