# Dyspepsia

## Symptoms
Bloating/belching (胃氣漲), acid reflux (胃酸倒流, 喉嚨酸), heartburn (火燒心)

## Causes
* GERD (+/- Barrett)
* Gastritis/peptic ulcer (+/- H pylori)
* Gastric/pancreatic CA/HCC
* Pancreatitis
* Mesenteric ischaemia
* Functional dyspepsia

## Assessment
* **Any epigastric pain / GI bleed symptoms?**
* **Abdomen soft? Any epigastric tenderness?**
* **Previous OGD findings and tx** (if H pylori +ve, is eradication confirmed?)

## Management
* **Triact** (antacid) / **Pepcidine** (H2 antagonist) / **Pantoloc** (PPI) for acid reflux/heartburn
* **Gasteel/Gaviscon** for belching/bloating
* **Refer SUR** if persistent

*If epigastric pain / GI bleed symptoms – refer to section on abd pain / haematemesis / PR bleed!*

## Management of H Pylori infection

### Triple therapy
1. PO Pantoloc 40mg BD or Nexium 40mg BD (i.e. **double the usual dose!**) x 2 weeks
2. PO Amoxicillin 1000mg BD (if penicillin allergy: PO Flagyl 500mg TDS) x 2 weeks
3. PO Clarithromycin 500mg BD x 2 weeks
* **Withhold statin** while on clarithromycin (risk of rhabdomyolysis)
* **Avoid Nexium with Plavix** (use Pantoloc or lansoprazole instead)

### Eradication test (must arrange! Only 80% successful eradication rate)
* **GU:** need **rescope OGD with biopsy** for rapid urease test and histology (chance of malignancy)
* **DU:** can do **urea breath test / stool antigen test**
* **Timing** (to minimise chance of false negative): **>4 weeks after antibiotics** (and need to withhold PPI 2 weeks before eradication test), and must not be in acute UGIB episode
