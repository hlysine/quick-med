## Causes

| Type | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Primary** | Tension, migraine, cluster headache |
| **Secondary — vascular** | ICH, SAH, SDH, EDH, cerebral venous sinus thrombosis, carotid/vertebral artery dissection |
| **Secondary — infectious** | Meningitis, encephalitis (CNS infection) |
| **Secondary — structural** | SOL (space-occupying lesion), raised ICP |
| **Secondary — other** | Giant cell arteritis (GCA), glaucoma, trigeminal neuralgia, TMJ/odontogenic/cervicogenic |

## Assessment

- Vitals — if **fever + headache**: manage as CNS infection → blood c/st + LP
- GCS
- Recent fall/HI
- **SOCRATES**:
  - **Thunderclap onset** → SAH / artery dissection
  - Worse when erect → low ICP; worse when supine/Valsalva → raised ICP
- Associated symptoms: fever, neck pain, eye pain, visual disturbance, jaw claudication, neurological symptoms
- Full neurological exam
- Background: malignancy, immunosuppression, coagulopathy, antiplatelet/anticoagulant use

## Management

- **Paracetamol (Panadol)** prn

### Red Flags — Neuro obs, urgent CT brain, ± LP

#### Raised ICP

- Elevate HOB 30°, loosen neck accessories
- Urgent consult neurosurgery (NS)
- If surgical CN3 palsy / herniation: IV mannitol; hyperventilate to PaCO2 4–4.5 kPa

#### Low ICP

- Lie flat, IVF replacement

#### Fever / CNS Infection

1. Urgent blood c/st
2. **IV dexamethasone before/with 1st dose antibiotics**
3. Urgent CT brain + LP
4. **IV Ceftriaxone (Rocephin) 2 g Q12H**
5. **IV Vancomycin**
6. **IV Acyclovir 10 mg/kg Q8H**
7. If immunocompromised / age >55 / alcoholic: **IV Ampicillin 2 g Q4H**
8. ± IV Metronidazole (Flagyl) if Hx mastoiditis/sinusitis/otitis/abscess

#### SAH

- Urgent CT angiogram
- Aim SBP <140 mmHg
- Reverse anticoagulation
- Tranexamic acid (Transamin)
- **Nimodipine 60 mg PO Q4H**
- Urgent consult NS

#### New / Enlarged Haematoma / ICH

- Aim SBP <140 mmHg
- Plt >100, INR <1.5
- ± Urgent consult NS

#### Artery Dissection

- **Activate stroke call**
- Urgent CT angiogram
- Urgent consult NS

#### Giant Cell Arteritis (GCA)

- Systemic glucocorticoids
- Urgent consult Ophthalmology/Vascular Surgery (OVS)

### Persistent Headache

- Consult neurology
