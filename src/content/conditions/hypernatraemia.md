## Key Cut-offs

- Normal range: **135–145 mmol/L**
- Correct at rate **<6–8 mmol/L over 24 hrs**
- Usually treat when **Na >150 mmol/L**

---

## Causes

- Most often: **patient is dehydrated**
- Diabetes insipidus (cranial or nephrogenic)
- DKA / HHS (osmotic diuresis)
- Hypercalcaemia
- Drugs: **lithium** (causes DI), **Tazocin / Timentin** (high Na load)

---

## If Symptomatic

- Neuro-obs Q4H +/- CT brain
- **Consult ICU**

---

## Management

### General Measures
- Off **diuretics** and **NaCl tablets**
- Review culprit drugs (lithium, Tazocin / Timentin)

### Rehydration
- IVF Q6H or Q8H
- **Avoid normal saline**
- Use **1/2:1/2** or **D5** (prefer not D5 if poor H'stix control)
- Rate depends on fluid status and medical history (e.g. CHF)

### Monitoring
- **RFT Q8H or Q12H**; aim to reduce Na by **<6–8 mmol/day**
- Monitor **H'stix** while on D5

### Workup for Underlying Cause
| Investigation | Condition Suspected |
|---|---|
| H'stix | DKA / HHS |
| Serum Ca | Hypercalcaemia |
| Serum + urine osmolality | Diabetes insipidus |

---

## Pitfall: Cerebral Oedema

- If rate of correction **>10 mmol/day**: risk of cerebral oedema
- Check for neurological symptoms; **slow or reverse correction** if overshoot detected
