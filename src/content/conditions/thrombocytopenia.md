## General Approach

- Most of the time has a **known cause** (e.g. haematology patients)
- If sudden onset:
  - Consider **pseudo-thrombocytopenia** (platelet clumping) and **drug-induced** causes
  - Recheck CBC in citrated blood
  - **Peripheral blood smear (PBS)**
  - Recent use of: heparin, antibiotics, anti-epileptics

---

## Look Out for MAHA (DIC / TTP / HUS)

Check PBS for **schistocytes** + retic, LDH, haptoglobin, unconjugated bilirubin, clotting, RFT

> Unless life-threatening bleeding, **transfusion NOT usually indicated** in ITP, SLE, TTP, HUS, DIC

---

## Neurological Assessment

- Ask for any neurological symptoms and recent trauma/head injury (even minor — risk of ICH with low platelets)
- Chart **GCS + pupil size**
- Urgent **CT brain**
- Neuro-obs

---

## Transfusion Indications

| Platelet Count | Indication |
|---|---|
| <10 × 10⁹/L | Prophylactic transfusion |
| <20 × 10⁹/L | With **fever** |
| <50 × 10⁹/L | Active bleeding or before invasive procedures |
| <100 × 10⁹/L | Before eye or neurosurgery |
| Any count | Suspected platelet dysfunction with active bleeding or before invasive procedures |

**Common order:** 4 units or 6 units platelets, full rate

---

## Key Facts

- Each unit of platelet raises Plt count by **~7–10 × 10⁹/L**
- If pancytopenia + DIC: **think APL** — urgent PBS interpretation by haematopathologist; give **ATRA immediately**
- Transfusion of components (platelet, FFP, etc.) does **not** need T&S as long as this patient has had a T&S done in this hospital in their lifetime
