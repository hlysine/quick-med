## Key Cut-offs

- Normal range: **3.5–5.0 mmol/L**

---

## ECG Features

- Prolonged PR interval
- Flattened T wave
- Prominent U wave
- +/- Tachyarrhythmia

---

## Management

### Urgent Measures
- If **K <2.0** or **ECG changes**: **cardiac monitoring** +/- consult ICU

### Oral Replacement (Preferred Whenever Possible)
- **PO KCl syrup 2 g Q2H x 1–3 doses**
- Avoid in patients on chemotherapy or with oral / GI ulcers
- Alternative: **Slow K 600 mg tablets** (slower effect)
- If NPO: can order *NPO except Syrup KCl*

### IV Replacement (If Oral Unavailable or K <2.5)
- **Baby / concentrated K** (irritates veins — risk of thrombophlebitis):
  - IV 10 mmol K in 100 ml water over 1 hr x1
  - IV 20 mmol K in 100 ml water over 2 hrs x1
- Add KCl to IVF if setting an end date (e.g. 10 mmol K in 500 ml NS Q8H x1 day)

### Post-Replacement
- Order **post-RFT** if significant hypoK (<3.0)
- Check **Mg** and **VBG** if persistent hypoK after replacement

---

## Expected Potassium Increase

| Replacement | Dose | K Increase |
|---|---|---|
| KCl syrup | 2 g = 27 mmol | ~0.3 mmol/L |
| Slow K | 600 mg = 8 mmol | ~0.1 mmol/L |
| IV KCl | 10 mmol | ~0.13 mmol/L |

- **Limit IV KCl** to 40 mmol/hr and 80 mmol/day

---

## Renal Dosing Tips

- **Always check creatinine first**
- Renal patients not on dialysis: replace cautiously (e.g. syrup KCl 0.5–1 g x1 if K <3.0)
- **CAPD patients**: replace to normokalaemia
- **HD patients**: almost never replace

---

## Special Situations

- **ACS / arrhythmia**: keep K >4.0
- **Check carefully** whether K has been replaced already, and the timing of the latest blood draw in relation to replacements
