# Chest Drain Related Problems

## Indications for Chest Drains
* Pneumothorax
* Pleural effusion
* Empyema

## Normal Functioning Chest Drain
* Swinging
* Underwater seal
* Bubbling (if for PTX)
* May or may not need suction

## No bubbling, No swinging - what to do?

### Causes
* Fully expanded PTX / fully drained pleural effusion (drain holes appose pleura)
* Blocked drain
* Kinked drain
* Dislodgement

### Management
* Check patient's clinical status - dyspnoea? Oxygen saturation? Increased oxygen requirement?
* **Visible blockage/ kinking/ dislodgement?**
* **If blocked/ kinked:** check for **subcutaneous emphysema** (palpate chest for **crepitus**, order CXR)
    * If subcutaneous emphysema: 1) remove blockage/kinking, 2) increase suction / reinsert larger chest drain, 3) if stridor/desat, **need skin incision!!**
* **CXR to confirm tube position +/- re-expansion of PTX**
* If clinically well, lung fully re-expanded and drain in-situ on CXR
    * Remove chest drain (keep chest drain if need pleurodesis)

## Air leak (bubbling)

> **Either system air leak (open system) or patient "air leak"**

* **System air leak:** no bubbling and swing after turning off suction
    * Dislodgement: **is the outermost side hole inside the chest cavity?** (*can check at bedside and on CXR - the last side hole interrupts the radioopaque line*)
    * Try pinching the skin at exit site, if bubbling stops, then leak is at exit site; can try add suture
    * Connection of drain to chest drain box - is it secure/ tight?
* **Patient air leak:** increases during cough/ prolonged speech
    * If persistent air leak (beyond 3-5 days) for PTX: CT thorax, consult CTS (may have fistula)

## Haemothorax
* **Inspect for clots!!** Presence of clots indicates **fresh bleeding**
    * *vs blood-stained effusion in CA patients: no clots, not emergency*
* **Complications:** desat (mass effect), shock (mediastinal shift -> decreased venous return), anaemia
* **Management:**
    * Obs Q1H x4 then Q4H if stable
    * **Large bore (32-36Fr) chest drain**, chart output
    * Haemodynamic support, transfusion as needed
    * Bld x CBC, clotting; keep Plt > 50, INR < 1.5
    * **Transamin if no C/I**
    * **Urgent consult CTS** if massive haemothorax (definition: initial blood loss > 1.5L, or continuing blood loss > 200ml/hr x 4hrs)

> **DO NOT spigot the chest drain** even if large amount of bleed! Must drain the blood out, otherwise mass effect will cause desat or mediastinal shift then shock

> **Indications for pleurodesis:**
> * Traumatic PTX
> * Recurrent PTX (first contralateral or second ipsilateral)
> * Bilateral PTX
> * Secondary spontaneous PTX i.e. underlying lung disease
> * Spontaneous haemothorax
> * Persistent air leak
> * Failed lung re-expansion
> * High risk occupations: pilots, drivers
> * Pregnancy
