# Dizziness

## Causes
* **Light-headedness (頭重腳輕/頭昏腦脹) / presyncope (就快失去知覺):**
    * Hypotension
    * Hypoxia / hyperventilation with hypocapnia
    * Hypoglycaemia
    * Anaemia
    * Reflex presyncope: vasovagal/situational, carotid hypersensitivity
    * Postural hypotension: diuretics/vasodilators, hypovolaemia (e.g. haemorrhage), autonomic neuropathy
    * **Cardiac: arrhythmia, ACS, structural** (AS, HOCM, tamponade)
    * **Vascular:** SVCO, subclavian steal syndrome
    * Psychiatric: anxiety
* **Vertigo (天旋地轉):**
    * Peripheral: BPPV, Meniere disease, vestibular neuritis/labyrinthitis, Ramsay Hunt, otitis media
    * **Central: vertebrobasilar TIA/stroke, brainstem lesion, ↑ ICP, vertebral artery dissection, vestibular migraine**

> Many patients with light-headedness tell you they feel 天旋地轉 – I like to ask 有無覺得世界向住同一個方向轉/行路偏埋一邊 instead

## Assessment
* **Vitals, GCS**
* Light-headedness vs vertigo; onset and duration, triggering factors (head movement, posture)
    * *Association with head movement suggests vertigo*
* Associated symptoms: N/V, headache, visual disturbance, hearing loss, tinnitus, chest discomfort, SOB, palpitations, autonomic symptoms
    * *Ear symptoms (sensorineural hearing loss/tinnitus) suggests **peripheral** vertigo*
* **Full neuro exam: CN** (pupils, EOM, CN7, dysarthria), 4 limbs power, **cerebellar signs**, meningism
    * Nystagmus suggests vertigo, vertical/rotational nystagmus suggests *central* vertigo
    * Brainstem signs (*CN palsies/cerebellar signs*) suggest *central* vertigo

## Management
* (May or may not need) Neuro obs, H'stix monitoring, cardiac monitoring
* If postural dizziness: postural BP (supine + erect 0,1,3 minutes) x2
* If cardiac symptoms: ECG LLII
* If neurological sign suggestive of central vertigo: **urgent CT brain**
* **Stemetil** (s/e: EPSE, so may avoid if Parkinson's) or **Merislon** prn for vertigo
* **Maxolon** prn for N/V
* If experience vertigo on aminoglycoside: consider toxicity, switch abx
* Consult neuro/ENT if persistent
