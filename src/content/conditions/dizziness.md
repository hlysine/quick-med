## Causes

### Light-headedness / Presyncope

- Hypotension, hypoxia/hyperventilation, hypoglycaemia, anaemia
- Reflex presyncope: vasovagal/situational, carotid hypersensitivity
- Postural hypotension
- Cardiac: arrhythmia, ACS, structural (AS, HOCM, tamponade)
- Vascular: SVCO, subclavian steal syndrome
- Psychiatric: anxiety

### Vertigo

| Type | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Peripheral** | BPPV, Meniere's disease, vestibular neuritis/labyrinthitis, Ramsay Hunt syndrome, otitis media |
| **Central** | Vertebrobasilar TIA/stroke, brainstem lesion, raised ICP, vertebral artery dissection, vestibular migraine |

## Assessment

- Vitals, GCS
- **Characterise**: light-headedness vs vertigo
- Onset, duration, triggering factors (head movement, posture)
- Associated symptoms: N/V, headache, visual disturbance, hearing loss, tinnitus, chest discomfort, SOB, palpitations, autonomic symptoms
- **Ear symptoms** suggest peripheral vertigo
- Full neurological exam: CN, 4-limb power, cerebellar signs, meningism
- **Nystagmus**: vertical or rotational suggests **central** cause
- **Brainstem signs** suggest central vertigo

## Management

- ± Neuro obs, H'stix monitoring, cardiac monitoring
- If postural dizziness: **postural BP** (supine + erect 0, 1, 3 min) × 2
- If cardiac symptoms: **ECG LLII**
- If neurological signs suggest central vertigo: **urgent CT brain**
- **Prochlorperazine (Stemetil)** prn — avoid in Parkinson's disease
- **Betahistine (Merislon)** prn
- **Metoclopramide (Maxolon)** prn for N/V
- If vertigo on aminoglycoside: consider ototoxicity, consider switching antibiotic
- Consult neurology/ENT if persistent
