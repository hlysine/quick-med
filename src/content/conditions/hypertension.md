# Hypertension

## Definition

- SBP >140
- Nurses to inform when SBP >160

## Hypertensive Urgency/Crisis

- Most have chronic poorly controlled HTN or a transient spike
- Asymptomatic SBP <200 is usually not worrying — can give stat oral meds
- **Rule out end organ damage (EOD):**
  - ICH / hypertensive encephalopathy
  - Ischaemic stroke
  - APO / decompensated HF / ACS
  - Dissecting aneurysm

## Assessment

- How high vs baseline?
- Neurological symptoms: headache, visual blurring, slurring, weakness
- Chest discomfort
- PMH:
  - **Active ICH / bleeding / aortic dissection** → tighter BP control
  - **Ischaemic stroke / AMI / ischaemic bowel** → looser BP control
- Antihypertensives already given?

## Management

### Oral Medications

| Drug | Starting Dose | Max Daily Dose |
|---|---|---|
| Norvasc (amlodipine) | 2.5–5 mg | 20 mg |
| Minipress (prazosin) | 1 mg | 20 mg |
| Hydralazine | 25 mg | 200 mg |
| Methyldopa | 250 mg | — |
| Captopril | 6.25 mg | — |

- **NPO patients:** Norvasc is the only oral agent that can be given

### IV Medications

- **Labetalol infusion:** 200 mg in 100 ml NS, start 5–10 ml/hr; or 100 mg in 100 ml NS, start 15 ml/hr
- **Labetalol IV bolus:** 5–10 mg
- **TNG infusion:** 50 mg undiluted at 5 mg/hr

### Investigations

Only if suspecting crisis with SBP >200 **plus** neurological or chest symptoms:

- **ACS:** ECG ± cardiac enzymes
- **Aortic dissection:** bilateral arm BP + PA CXR ± CT aortogram
- **ICH:** CT brain + CBC + clotting

### Management in Specific Conditions

**Ischaemic stroke** — loose BP control
- Not for tPA: SBP ≤220
- For tPA: SBP ≤185/110 before tPA; ≤180/105 for 24 hrs after

**ICH** — strict BP control
- Target SBP ≤140
- Reverse anticoagulation
- Neuro obs Q1H
- Urgent consult Neurosurgery

**Aortic dissection**
- Target SBP 100–120, HR 60–70
- Obs Q1H
- Urgent consult Cardiothoracic Surgery

**ACS / APO**
- Avoid BB / labetalol / ACEI
- Prefer IV TNG

### Persistent / Resistant Hypertension

- If young (<40), resistant, or hypoK HTN → consult Endocrine for secondary HTN screening
- Review precipitating drugs: corticosteroids, sympathomimetics, calcineurin inhibitors, ESA
- Consider: OSA, coarctation
- Investigations: RFT, ARR, ONDST, 24 hr urine metanephrines, IGF-1, TFT, Ca/PO4, PTH
- Bilateral renal artery Doppler — **avoid ACEI/ARB before ruling out bilateral RAS**
- Essential HTN: refer GOPC ± consult MED
