# Hypertension

> **Generally refers to SBP > 140, but usually nurses will inform you when SBP > 160**

## Hypertensive Urgency/ Crisis

Most cases with high BP either have 1) chronic poorly controlled HTN or 2) only a transient BP spike which resolves when
properly rested (and reversible causes e.g. pain/constipation/AROU treated).

*So usually asymptomatic HTN with SBP < 200 is not worrying (can just stat oral medications).*

But important to rule out possible end organ damage (EOD) caused by HTN – **Head to Toe approach:**

* Intracranial haemorrhage/ hypertensive encephalopathy
* Ischaemic stroke
* Acute pulmonary oedema/ decompensated heart failure/ ACS
* Dissecting aneurysm (usually rare unless known case)

## How to determine the URGENCY?

* How **high** is the blood pressure? What is the **baseline** BP?
* Any **neuro symptom** (e.g. headache, visual blurring, slurring, weakness) or **chest discomfort**?
* Basic information about the patient – age, PMH, admission reason, DNACPR case?
  * Active ICH / bleeding / aortic dissection -> tighter BP control!
  * Ischaemic stroke / AMI / ischaemic bowel -> looser BP control!
* Is he on any antihypertensives? Were the medications given & when?

## Commonly Used Medications

### Oral Medications

* **Norvasc** (amlodipine) 2.5 or 5mg once (max daily dose 20mg)
* **Minipress** (prazosin) 1mg once (max daily dose 20mg)
* **Hydralazine** 25mg once (max daily dose 200mg)
* **Methyldopa** 250mg once
* **Captopril** 6.25mg once

### IV Medications

* **Labetalol** (max daily dose 300mg)
  * 200mg in 100ml NS [fixed concentration 2 to 1], start with 5-10ml/hr
  * 100mg in 100ml NS [fixed concentration 1 to 1], start with 15ml/hr
  * IV bolus 5-10mg once (*usually by houseman*)
* **TNG infusion** (especially useful if APO)
  * 50mg (no dilution), 5mg/hr

> **Tips:**
> * If NPO (e.g. surgery pt), you can order **NPO except Norvasc** (not even NPOEM)
> * **Can repeat** give same medications/ try another drug class if BP remains high
> * Consider **reversible causes:** optimise pain control, treat constipation / AROU!

## Further Investigations

**ONLY if you really suspect hypertensive crisis** e.g. when **SBP > 200 with neuro symptom** (e.g. headache, visual
blurring, slurring, weakness) or **chest discomfort**

* ACS: ECG +/- cardiac enzymes
* Aortic dissection: bilateral arm BP + PA CXR +/- CT aortogram
* ICH: CT brain + CBC, clotting

## Management in specific conditions

* **Ischaemic stroke**
  * Usually have fixed protocol for stroke pathways to follow!
  * Loose BP control
    * Not for tPA: **keep SBP <= 220 mmHg**
    * **For tPA:** keep <= 185/110 before tPA, **<= 180/105** for 24hrs after tPA
* **ICH**
  * Strict BP control, **aim SBP <= 140**
  * Reverse anticoagulation
  * Neuro obs Q1H, urgent consult NS
* **Aortic dissection**
  * Strict BP control, **aim SBP 100-120, HR 60-70**
  * Obs Q1H, urgent consult CTS
* **ACS / APO**
  * Avoid beta blockers (including Labetalol) or ACEI
  * Usually prefer use **IV TNG**

## Persistent high BP

* **If young (< 40) / resistant / hypoK HTN:** consult/refer endocrine for secondary HTN screening
  * Review drugs: corticosteroids, sympathomimetics, calcineurin inhibitors, ESA
  * S/S of OSA, coarctation of aorta
  * RFT (for CKD), ARR, ONDST, 24hr urine metanephrines, IGF-1, TFT, Ca/PO4, PTH
  * Bil. renal artery Doppler (avoid ACEI / ARB before ruling out bil. renal artery stenosis)
* **Essential HTN: refer GOPC** (+/- consult MED) x poor HTN control / newly diagnosed HTN
  * Refer to HK primary care HTN reference framework (summary below) for drug choice
  * Looser BP control for elderly / symptoms of postural hypotension
