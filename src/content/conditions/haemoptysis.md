## Definition

- **Massive haemoptysis** = >100ml/hr or >150ml/24hrs
- Distinguish from: UGIB (coffee ground/haematemesis), oral bleeding, epistaxis

## Causes

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Infective | TB, necrotising pneumonia, bronchiectasis |
| Malignancy | Primary/metastatic lung CA |
| Vascular | PE, vascular malformation, aneurysm, fistula |
| Autoimmune | Vasculitis (e.g. GPA, anti-GBM disease) |
| Systemic | Antiplatelet/anticoagulant use, coagulopathy |

## Complications

- Airway obstruction
- Aspiration
- Respiratory failure
- Anaemia

## Assessment

- **Vitals**
- **Haemoptysis history:** amount in past 24–48hrs, clots, onset
- **Associated symptoms:** SOB, chest discomfort, sputum, fever, constitutional symptoms, rash/joint pain
- **TOCC** for TB
- **Respiratory exam:** air entry, upper zone consolidation, coarse creps/sputum

## Management

### General

- Gentle suction prn; **position bleeding side down** (if known)
- Chart haemoptysis
- Urgent CXR
- ECG (for PE)

### Bloods and Investigations

- CBC + clotting; **transfuse to keep Plt >50, INR <1.5**
- Sputum: c/st, AFB, TB PCR, TB culture, cytology ×3

### Medications

- **W/H antiplatelets** (urgent consult cardiac if on DAPT); W/H ± reverse anticoagulation
- **PO Transamin 250mg TDS** prn/regular (C/I in active thrombosis e.g. PE/DVT/DIC/ACS/stroke)
- **IV Tazocin** if suspect infection

### If Massive Haemoptysis

- Obs Q1H
- Haemodynamic support, T&S + transfusion
- **Urgent consult Anaesthesia** x intubation with lung isolation
- **Urgent CTA** ± bronchial artery embolisation
- ± Urgent consult CTS x rigid bronchoscopy/lung resection

### Follow-up

- Consult **Respiratory** (may need CT thorax, bronchoscopy, angiogram, autoimmune markers)
