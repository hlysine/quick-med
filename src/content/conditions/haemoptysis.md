# Haemoptysis

> **Massive haemoptysis = >100ml/hr or >150ml/24hrs**

## Causes
* Infective: TB, necrotising pneumonia, bronchiectasis
* CA
* Vascular: PE, vascular malformation/aneurysm/fistula
* Autoimmune disease
* Systemic: antiplatelet/anticoagulant, coagulopathy
* Ddx: UGIB, oral bleeding, epistaxis (*clues: nasal blood, oral bleeding source, hx of vomiting*)

## Complications
* Airway obstruction
* Aspiration
* Respiratory failure
* Anaemia

> If intubated, haemoptysis will be blood in tracheal aspirate; blood found in oral cavity will be from larynx/pharynx/ GI tract; **may insert R/T and aspirate to aid localisation!**
> Urgent consult ENT for unstoppable epistaxis/ laryngeal/ pharyngeal bleeding (initially can consider stuffing Merocel in nostril for epistaxis if already intubated i.e. airway protected)

## Assessment
* Vitals
* Amount in past 24-48hrs, any clots? Onset?
* SOB, chest discomfort, sputum, fever, constitutional symptoms, rash/joint pain, TOCC for TB
* Resp exam: air entry, upper zone consolidation, coarse crep/sputum

## Management
* Gentle suction prn; position bleeding side down (if known)
* Chart haemoptysis
* Urgent CXR
* ECG (for PE)
* **Bld x CBC, clotting;** transfuse to keep Plt > 50, INR < 1.5
* **Sputum x c/st, AFB, TB PCR, TB culture, cytology x3**
* **W/H antiplatelets** (urgent consult cardiac if on DAPT), **W/H +/- reverse anticoagulation**
* **PO Transamin 250mg TDS prn/regular** (**C/I in active thrombosis** e.g. PE/DVT/DIC /ACS/stroke)
* IV Tazocin if suspect infection
* **If massive haemoptysis (>100ml/hr or >150ml/24hrs)**
    * Obs Q1H
    * Hemodynamic support, T&S and transfusion as needed
    * Urgent consult Anes x intubation with lung isolation
    * Urgent CTA +/- bronchial artery embolization
    * +/- urgent consult CTS x rigid bronchoscopy/lung resection
* Consult respi (may need CT thorax, bronchoscopy, angiogram, autoimmune markers)
