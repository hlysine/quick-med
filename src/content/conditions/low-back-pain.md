# Low Back Pain

## Causes
* Mechanical: vertebral collapse, bone metastasis, PID, spondylosis, facet joint disease
* Muscle sprain, spasm
* Infective: spondylodiscitis
* Inflammatory: spondyloarthritis
* Neuropathic: spinal stenosis, cord compression, cauda equina syndrome, radiculopathy
* Referred pain: AAA, pancreatitis, loin pain

## Assessment
* History: onset (acute/chronic, hx of **trauma**), **radiation** (Abd to back? Back to LL?), characteristics (mechanical, neuropathic, inflammatory, nocturnal), associated symptoms (fever, **weakness, numbness, sphincter disturbance**)
* Spine exam: tenderness, stepping, paraspinal muscle spasm, sensory level
* LL neuro: tone, power/reflex (by myotome), sensation (by dermatome)
* Sciatica: straight leg raise, crossed straight leg raise, hamstring and bowstring sign
* PR: tone, grip, sensation
* Abdominal exam: tenderness, **pulsatile expansile mass (AAA)**

## Management
* S/S of neurological compression: **urgent MRI C/T/LS spine** (according to level of clinical localisation) **+ whole spine sagittal screening, urgent consult NS**
* New vertebral collapse: if stable (compression #), refer P&O x thoracolumbar orthosis
* Old vertebral collapse: refer P&O x soft lumbar corset
* Non-specific: analgesics, back physiotherapy
* Neuropathic pain: Lyrica/gabapentin
* Refer ORT/rheumat

> **Review XR LS spine:** note alignment, and whether outline of endplates and pedicles are intact!
