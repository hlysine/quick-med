- **Paraphimosis**: urological emergency; prepuce trapped behind corona of glans; most common cause: forgetting to retract foreskin after foley insertion; presentation: pain + prepuce oedema (longstanding retracted prepuce without pain or oedema without constricting ring is NOT true paraphimosis)
  - **Management — manual reduction**: lots of xylocaine jelly; D50 soaked gauze + manual compression ±ice pack; main force is to push glans IN rather than pull prepuce out
  - If unable to reduce: **urgent consult urology**

- **Scrotal pain/swelling**: hernia (±IO symptoms = strangulated/incarcerated), Fournier's gangrene, testicular torsion/rupture, epididymo-orchitis/scrotal abscess, hydrocele, varicocele, testicular tumour
  - **Assessment**: fever/septic; history (sudden onset/trauma/STD/DM/previous episodes/abd pain/IO symptoms); exam (overlying skin: erythematous/blisters/crepitus = NF warning; swelling: can get above/transilluminable; mass/fluctuance/tenderness; lie of testes/high-lying = torsion; cremasteric reflex)
  - **Management**:
    - **Fournier's gangrene**: urgent consult URO + broad spectrum abx (Meronem)
    - **Epididymo-orchitis**: PO levofloxacin 500mg daily ×1-2 weeks + doxycycline if sexually active; if scrotal abscess: urgent USG; FG/A1c for undiagnosed DM
    - **Testicular torsion**: urgent consult URO (direct OT)
    - **Testicular mass/hydrocele/varicocele**: routine consult URO
    - **Incarcerated/strangulated hernia**: urgent consult SUR
    - **Simple hernia**: routine consult SUR
