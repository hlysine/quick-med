# Male Genitalia Problems (Paraphimosis / Scrotal)

## Paraphimosis
* **Urological emergency!**
* Prepuce becomes trapped behind the corona of glans penis and cannot be retracted
* **Most common cause: forgetting to retract the foreskin after Foley insertion!**
* **Presentation: pain + prepuce oedema** (*some patients may have longstanding retracted prepuce without pain; prepuce oedema without a constricting ring is not true paraphimosis!*)

### Management - manual reduction
* **LOTS of xylocaine jelly** (act as LA + lubricant)
* **D50 soaked gauze + manual compression +/- ice pack** (*works if the patient can stand the cold LOL*)
    * Soak the gauze with D50 and wrap the penis with the gauze
    * Aim is to reduce swelling of the glans by osmosis + manual compression
* After 5-10 minutes, try to manually reduce the foreskin as shown below
    * Main force is to push the glans in rather than pull the prepuce out
    * It can take a few tries and also some force... it is painful but it works
* If still unable to reduce, need to urgent consult urology! (*they will be annoyed but well you tried...*)

## Scrotal Pain/Swelling

* **Hernia (if painful/IO symptoms: strangulated/incarcerated hernia)**
* **Fournier's gangrene**
* **Testicular torsion / rupture**
* **Epididymo-orchitis / scrotal abscess**
* Hydrocele
* Varicocele
* Testicular tumour (germ cell, lymphoma, carcinoma)

| Get above swelling | Testis and epididymis | Transillumination | Tenderness | Differential diagnosis |

### Assessment
* **Vital signs - fever / septic looking?**
* **History**
    * Sudden onset/ chronic?
    * History of trauma? (testicular torsion)
    * History of STD/ urethral discharge? DM? (epididymo-orchitis, scrotal abscess)
    * Previous episodes?
    * Abd pain? IO symptoms? (strangulated hernia)
* **Physical examination** [always comment on bilateral scrotum, even if one side is normal!]
    * Overlying skin: erythematous/ blister/ crepitus/ open wound/ discharge
        * **Blister/crepitus is worrisome of Fournier's gangrene!!**
    * Swelling: can get above? Trans-illuminable?
    * Any discrete mass? Fluctuance (abscess)? Painful?
    * **Lie of testes**, whether testes are high lying (testicular torsion)
    * Cremasteric reflex (for testicular torsion)

### Management
* **Fournier's gangrene: urgent consult URO, start broad spectrum abx (e.g. Meronem) + stat**
* **Epididymo-orchitis: PO levofloxacin 500mg daily x 1-2/52**, + PO doxycycline if sexually active
    * If suspect scrotal abscess: urgent USG scrotum (may need drainage)
    * Workup for undiagnosed DM (FG, A1c)
* **Testicular torsion: urgent consult URO** (may need direct OT exploration)
    * Usually for young age group, history of trauma, sudden onset
* Testicular mass, hydrocele, varicocele: routine consult URO
* **Incarcerated/strangulated hernia: urgent consult SUR**
* Simple hernia: routine consult SUR
