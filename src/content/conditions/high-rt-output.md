# High R/T Output

> **No strict definition, but nurses may inform you if >50 ml / 4hrs**

## Causes
GOO, IO, ileus

## Assessment
* **Content of R/T output;** any vomiting too?
* **Abdomen – Pain? Tender? Succussion splash?**
* **Signs of aspiration:** cough/sputum/SOB

## Management
* **R/T to BSB** (as opposed to spigotting), **aspirate Q4H**
* **W/H R/T feeding;** maintenance IVF
* **Monitor RFT, VBG, Ca/PO4, Mg**
* **AXR** (+ CXR if suspect aspiration)
* **Add PPI; off Imodium, opioids**

*If bloody / coffee ground output – refer to section on haematemesis!*

> **How to read the chart:**
> * **Asp** = volume aspirated with syringe at that moment and discarded
> * **BE** = bedside bag emptied at that moment (so the content you see in the bag now is the output from the last BE time till now)
> * **So total output** = sum of all aspirated volumes (Asp) + sum of all emptied volumes (BE) + current volume in the bag
