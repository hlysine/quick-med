## Causes

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Upper airways | Airway obstruction (FB/vomit/blood/anaphylaxis/infection/tumour/goitre), sputum retention, aspiration |
| Lungs | PTX, pleural effusion, collapse, pneumonia/CA, asthma/COPD, ILD/bronchiectasis |
| Cardiovascular | PE, ACS/IHD, tamponade/pericardial effusion, APO/CHF |
| Chest/abdominal wall | Kyphoscoliosis, ascites |
| Neurological | Respiratory muscle fatigue |
| Increased respiratory drive | Anaemia, metabolic acidosis, intoxication, anxiety |

## Assessment

- **Vitals:** SpO2, RR, HR, BP
- **GCS**
- Signs of **airway obstruction**
- Onset/progression, characteristics (**orthopnoea/PND/SOBOE**)
- Associated symptoms: choking/aspiration, cough/sputum, haemoptysis, chest discomfort
- **Respiratory exam:** asymmetry, tracheal deviation, breath sounds
- **Calves:** asymmetric swelling (DVT/PE)
- **Fluid status**

## Management

### Investigations

- **CXR**
- ± ECG + bloods: CBC, RFT, ABG, TnI/CK, NT-proBNP, TFT
- ± Sputum/NPS
- ± Lung function tests
- ± CT thorax
- ± Cardiac stress test + echo
- ± Neck XR/CT, FL

### If Significant Respiratory Distress

- **Empirically give O2** even if no desaturation
- Prop up (sit upright)
- Sputum suction prn
- Chest physiotherapy
- **Ventolin 2 puffs Q4H prn** if wheeze

### Cause-Specific Management

- Refer to **Desaturation** section for cause-specific treatment
- **SOB due to anxiety:** breathe into paper bag, sublingual/PO Ativan, consult CP/PSY
- **Consult MED** if persistent/unexplained
