# Shortness of Breath (SOB)

## Causes
* **Upper airways**
    * Airway obstruction: foreign body (vomit/blood), anaphylaxis, infection, tumour, goitre etc.
    * Sputum retention
    * Aspiration
* **Lungs**
    * PTX, pleural effusion, collapse
    * Pneumonia, CA
    * Asthma, COPD
    * ILD, bronchiectasis
* **Cardiovascular**
    * PE
    * ACS/IHD
    * Tamponade/pericardial effusion
    * APO/CHF
* Chest/abdominal wall: kyphoscoliosis, ascites (diaphragmatic splinting)
* Neurological disease (causing respiratory fatigue)
* **↑ respiratory drive** (but no respiratory problem)
    * Anaemia
    * Metabolic acidosis
    * Intoxication
    * Anxiety

## Assessment
* Vitals (particularly SpO2), GCS
* Signs of **airway obstruction**: stridor/inability to speak, absent air flow, high resistance on ventilation
* Onset and progression, characteristics e.g. orthopnea/PND/SOBOE
* Associated symptoms: **choking** (aspiration), **cough/sputum, haemoptysis, chest discomfort**
* **Respiratory exam (note asymmetry):** trachea, chest rise, breath sounds +/- added sounds
* **Calves** (soft, non-tender?)
* **Fluid status**

## Management
* **CXR**
* +/- additional workup depending on clinical picture: ECG, blds (e.g. CBC, RFT, ABG, TnI/CK, NT-proBNP, TFT), sputum/NPS, lung function test, CT thorax, cardiac stress test, echo, neck XR/CT, FL
* If in significant respiratory distress, can empirically give e.g. 2L O2 even if no desat
* **Supportive treatment:** prop up, sputum suction prn, chest PT, Ventolin 2 puffs Q4H prn if wheeze
* **Cause-specific treatment:** refer to section on desat
* SOB due to anxiety: breathe into paper bag, sublingual/PO Ativan, consult CP/PSY
* Consult MED if persistent/unexplained
