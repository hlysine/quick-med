## For major transfusion reaction:
* **Report AIRS** (confirm with MO)
    * *Not a must if you commit fever is due to febrile non-haemolytic reaction*
* Stop transfusion + **DISCONNECT the transfusion set** from patient
* Check blood unit for blood type and signs of contamination
* Return blood unit + transfusion set to blood bank / microbiology lab for testing
* Repeat T&S, c/st (if fever), CBC d/c, L/RFT, clotting, fibrinogen, D dimer, haptoglobin, LDH, retic, DAT
* Urine x haemoglobin
* If SOB/chest discomfort: + CXR, ECG, +/- ABG
* Treat underlying cause (see above)

> **Tip:** for **EACH** unit transfused, you can find in the progress notes (*mandatory for nurses to chart*) the patient's vitals:
> 1. Before start of transfusion
> 2. 15 minutes after start of transfusion
> 3. At the end of the transfusion
