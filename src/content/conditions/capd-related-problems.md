# CAPD Related Problems

## Cloudy Effluent

> **TREAT as CAPD PERITONITIS!**

### Diagnostic Criteria
* Clinical features (e.g. fever, abdominal pain, cloudy effluent)
* **WCC >= 100 cells/mm3 and percentage of neutrophils > 50%**
* Positive CAPD dialysate fluid culture

### Assessment
* Save CAPD effluent for inspection
* Septic work-up
* Send CAPD fluid for gram stain, culture, microscopy, cell count

### Management
* **Rapid flushing of PDF fluid 2L x3, + IP heparin 500-1000 units/ L**
* **Increase CAPD regimen to 4 bags per day** (*may consult MO before administrating this, or wait for case MO to decide*) **+ IP heparin 500-1000 units/L**
* If no septicaemia / fever: **IP cefazolin 1g daily + IP ceftazidime 1g daily + stat**
* If septicaemia / fever: **IV cefazolin + IV cefepime** (or gentamicin if anuric) + PO nystatin
* Chart I/O, BW and monitor RFT
* Repeat PD fluid x WCC, gram smear, culture
* **Check clinical response**

> However, there can still be other causes of abdominal pain/ turbid CAPD fluids!
> e.g. causes of acute abdomen (e.g. perforated viscus, appendicitis, etc)
> **If limited clinical response within 1-2 days of starting antibiotics, consider other causes and work up accordingly (e.g. erect CXR/ AXR for free gas/ dilated bowels), CT scan for causes of acute abdomen**
> *(We have seen cases of CAPD presenting with abdominal pain, treated initially as CAPD peritonitis but ended up having incarcerated inguinal hernia / peritonitis secondary to perforated appendicitis!)*

## Exit Site Infection

### Clinical Features
* Purulent discharge
* Erythema +/- indurance/ fluctuance around catheter insertion site

### Management
* Take wound swab for microscopy and culture
* Antibiotics according to wound swab results (topical gentamicin cream, oral Abx e.g. cloxacillin 500mg QID 2/52)
* Consult renal

## Low Tenckhoff output

### Causes
* Blockage: by fibrin clots
* Kinking: external, or internal (e.g. faecal-loaded bowel)
* Low osmotic pull: due to hyperglycaemia

### Management
* H'stix monitoring -> Actrapid if high
* **KUB** (check for kinking) -> if constipation: laxatives, mobilise patient (change posture)
* If blockage: consult renal team for flushing / IP heparin / IP urokinase / Tenckhoff revision
