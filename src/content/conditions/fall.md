## Causes

- **Visual/balance/neurological**: decreased visual acuity, vestibular dysfunction, peripheral neuropathy, Parkinson's, CVA, cognitive impairment
- **Environmental**: wet floors, poor lighting, loose rugs, inappropriate footwear, unfamiliar surroundings
- **Medications**: sedatives, antihypertensives, diuretics, antipsychotics, polypharmacy

## Complications

| System | Injury |
|---|---|
| **Head** | ICH, EDH, SDH, SAH, cerebral contusion (coup and contrecoup), skull fracture |
| **Neck** | C-spine fracture, facet joint dislocation, central cord syndrome |
| **MSK** | Fracture, dislocation, muscle/joint sprain |
| **Chest/abdominopelvic** | Visceral injuries (rarer, higher-energy mechanism) |

## Assessment

- **Vitals**, GCS
- **History of fall**:
  - Witness present?
  - Cause: doing what / tripped / slipped / weakness / visual disturbance / dizziness / LOC
  - Mechanism and energy: height of fall, impacted body parts
  - Current symptoms, especially neurological and neck/back
- **Full neurological exam**: CN (pupils, EOM, CN7, dysarthria), 4-limb power and sensation; PR if neck/back injury suspected
- **Scalp**: wound, haematoma, tenderness
- **Musculoskeletal**: assess body parts per complaints

## Management

- **Fall precautions**: bed rails up, call bell within reach, non-slip footwear, clear environment
- **Neuro obs** Q1H × 4, then Q4H if stable
- **Bloods**: CBC + clotting
- **Trauma series XR**: lateral C-spine, CXR, AP pelvis; + symptomatic sites
- **CT brain** if any of:
  - HI / scalp injury
  - Unwitnessed fall
  - Unreliable history
  - Neurological symptoms
- **If neck injury / spine symptoms**:
  - Philadelphia neck collar, log roll precautions
  - Urgent consult ORT
- **If recurrent falls**: consult GERI
