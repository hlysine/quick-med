## Complications
* **HI:** ICH, haematoma (EDH, SDH, SAH), brain concussion (coup and countercoup), skull #
* **Neck injury:** C spine fracture, facet joint dislocation, central cord syndrome
* **Other MSK injuries:** fracture, dislocation, muscle/joint sprain
* (Rarer) chest and abdominopelvic visceral organ injuries

## Assessment
* **Vitals, GCS**
* **History of fall:** any witness?
    * **Cause:** Doing what? Tripped/slipped? Weakness? Visual disturbance? Dizziness/**LOC**?
    * **Mechanism and energy of impact:** height of fall, impacted body parts
    * **Current symptoms:** especially neurological symptoms, neck/back pain
* **Full neuro exam:** CN (pupils, EOM, CN7, dysarthria), 4 limbs power/sensation, PR if neck/back injury
* **Scalp** wound/haematoma/tenderness (suggests possible HI)
* Assess body parts according to complaints

## Management
* **Fall precautions**
* **Neuro obs** Q1H x4 then Q4H if stable
* Bld x CBC, clotting
* **Trauma series XR:** lateral C spine, CXR, AP pelvis, (+ symptomatic sites)
* **CT brain** if HI / scalp injury / unwitnessed fall / unreliable history / neurological symptoms
* **If neck injury/spine symptoms:** Philadelphia neck collar, log roll, urgent consult ORT
    * (If unstable C spine #: may need Halo device or SOMI brace later)
* If recurrent falls: consult GERI

> **Documentation tip:** write **"found sitting/lying on floor"** rather than "fall", to avoid bringing trouble to nurses!

> Some departments have fall protocols for you to follow!
