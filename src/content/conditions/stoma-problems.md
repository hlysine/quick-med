# Stoma Problems

## Stoma Bleeding
* **Most important is to rule out GI bleeding** (i.e. as you would with PR bleeding)
    * Any melaena/ fresh PRB coming from stoma i.e. GIT
    * Treat as you would any GIB
* Most common cause is **peristomal bleeding**, which is due to granulation / friction/ skin wounds
    * Can stop by direct pressure
    * If still not yet stop, consider apply **adrenaline gauze**

## High Stoma Output

> **Defined as stoma output > 1L/day**

* Requires aggressive fluid and electrolyte replacement to **correct dehydration and sodium loss**
    * **Especially remember to replace magnesium**
    * Ideally 400-800ml/day

### Management
* **Strict I/O chart** (can opt for daily body weight)
* **Rehydration** with aim to achieve adequate urine output 0.5ml/kg/day
* **Monitor RFT, Ca/PO4, Mg, VBG**
* **Anti-motility agents** (avoid if high R/T output i.e. still in ileus)
    * **PO Imodium 2mg TDS**
    * Codeine (opioid agonists and act on mu-receptors of bowel in both circulator and longitudinal muscles to slow peristalsis) (less preferred as sedative and addictive)
* **Anti-secretory drugs**
    * PPI
    * Somatostatin analogues (e.g. octreotide)

## Stoma Prolapse
* Check for signs of ischaemia and gangrene (i.e. whether mucosa is healthy), positive output (no IO)
* Try to manually reduce if possible (+/- IM tramadol before procedure)
    * Can try to cover the swollen mucosa with D50 gauze to reduce swelling (c.f. paraphimosis)
