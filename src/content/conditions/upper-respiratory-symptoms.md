# Upper Respiratory Symptoms

> **Cough, sore throat, rhinorrhoea, nasal congestion**

## Causes
* **Cough**
    * Lower tract disease: pneumonia, asthma, COPD, ILD, bronchiectasis, TB, CA lung
    * URTI (including influenza, nCoV, common cold), post-viral, allergic
    * GERD, aspiration
    * Heart failure, ACEI
* Nasal symptoms: URTI, allergic rhinitis, nasal polyp etc.

## Assessment
* Symptoms and onset; if sputum+, ask about colour
* Red flags: SOB, haemoptysis

## Management
* **If SOB: consider lower tract/cardiac disease**
* NPS x nCoV, flu, +/- resp virus panel
* Sputum x c/st if any
* **Cough:**
    * Productive: mucolytic (Fluimucil, Bisolvon), expectorant (MES, guaifenesin)
    * Dry: cough suppressant (Pholcodine, codeine, dextromethorphan *Phensedyl*)
* Sore throat: throat lozenge (Dequadin), Panadol
* Rhinorrhoea / nasal congestion: decongestant (phenylephrine, pseudoephedrine)
* Allergy: antihistamine (Piriton, Cetirizine, diphenhydramine, promethazine), steroid nasal spray

## Dosage for commonly used drugs (for KWH BBs!)
* Fluimucil A PO 200mg TDS
* Actifed PO 1 tab TDS
* Bisolvon PO 8mg TDS
* Dequadin lozenge PO 250mcg TDS
* Piriton PO 4mg TDS
* Chlorhexidine MW LA 10ml BD
* Bonjela LA (no dosage)

> For miscellaneous drugs, click the **"BNF"** icon on IPMOE – it lists drugs on the formulary by category!

> For patients with oral mucositis, can prescribe chlorhexidine mouthwash / Difflam / thymol gargle. If causes oral ulcer: Bonjela oral gel
