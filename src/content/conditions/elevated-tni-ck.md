## ACS Diagnosis

**2 out of 3** criteria:
1. Chest pain
2. Elevated cardiac enzymes
3. Serial ECG ST/T changes

- DM/elderly/female may have **silent MI or atypical presentation**
- Also ask for: SOB, nausea/vomiting, diaphoresis
- **Serial trending** with TnI AND CK ± LDH required if moderate–high clinical suspicion (they peak and drop at different times)

---

## Causes of Elevated Troponin (TnI)

- ACS, stable angina
- Chronic heart failure, myocarditis, cardiomyopathy
- Arrhythmia (especially tachycardia)
- PE, respiratory failure
- Sepsis
- Renal failure

> hsTnI up to 300+ commonly caused by the above; **not worrying for significant infarct** if not doubling in serial tests and peak <400

---

## Causes of Elevated CK

- ACS
- **Rhabdomyolysis** (muscle pain, AKI, myoglobinuria — treatment: rehydration with IVF)
- Myositis
- Renal failure
- Recent exercise

## ECG Interpretation for ACS

### Unstable Angina / NSTEMI (Subendocardial Ischaemia)
- Horizontal/downsloping ST depression in lateral leads (I, II, V4–6)
- Upsloping ST depression is non-specific
- ST depression/TWI do **NOT** indicate ischaemic territory
- Localised ST depression is usually reciprocal of a STEMI elsewhere
- **Diffuse ST depression + STE in aVR** = left main/LAD occlusion → primary PCI

### STEMI Criteria (2 contiguous leads, J point elevation)

| Patient | V2–V3 | Other leads |
|---|---|---|
| Men <40 | ≥2.5 mm | ≥1 mm |
| Men ≥40 | ≥2.0 mm | ≥1 mm |
| Women | ≥1.5 mm | ≥1 mm |

- Look for associated features: acute morphology change, hyperacute/inverted T waves, pathological Q waves, reciprocal changes
- For LVH/LBBB: STE >1/6 of deep S in V1–3, or present in V4–6

### Territory Relationships

| Territory | Leads | Look also for |
|---|---|---|
| Anterior STEMI | V1–4 | Reciprocal ST depression in inferior leads |
| Lateral STEMI | V5–6, aVL | Reciprocal inferior leads |
| Inferior STEMI | II, III, aVF | Posterior STEMI; RV MI; bradycardia/heart block |

**Posterior STEMI:** horizontal ST depression V1–3, tall broad R waves V2–3, upright T (as reciprocal changes); use posterior leads V7–9 if needed

**RV MI:** hypotension, V4R; give fluids; **C/I nitrate and morphine**

> Type A aortic dissection can mimic inferior STEMI

### STEMI Equivalents (Occlusion MI — Benefit from Primary PCI)
- NSTEMI with unstable BP/pulse or persistent/recurrent angina
- New LBBB with unstable BP/pulse or APO or Sgarbossa criteria
- New RBBB + LAFB (proximal LAD)
- de Winter ST/T complex
- STE in aVR + diffuse ST depression (left main/proximal LAD)
- **Wellens' type A/B** (biphasic/inverted T in V2–3 = proximal LAD — caution: occurs during pain-free reperfusion)

## Management: Unstable Angina / Non-occlusive MI

- Bed rest, cardiac monitoring, SpO2 monitoring (keep O2 >92%), BP/P Q4H
- Serial TnI Q4–6H until peak
- **Bloods:** CBC d/c + clotting + RFT; FG + FL + HbA1c
- **Sublingual TNG** 500 mcg Q5–15 min × 1–3 prn
- Persistent ischaemia/HF/HTN: **IV isosorbide dinitrate** 2–10 mg/hr (keep SBP >100; **C/I** recent sildenafil or RV MI)

### DAPT + LMWH (for MO)
- **Aspirin** 325 mg chewable stat, then 80 mg daily + pantoloc
- **Clopidogrel** 300 mg stat, then 75 mg daily
- **Enoxaparin** 1 mg/kg SC Q12H + stat (Q24H if CrCl <30)

### Additional Medications
- **Atorvastatin** 80 mg daily + stat (plaque stabilisation regardless of LDL)
- **Metoprolol** 25 mg BD + stat (C/I in RV MI) — start after ruling out acute HF
- **Losartan** 25 mg daily + stat, increase to 50 mg as tolerated — start after ruling out acute HF

- Consult AMI nurse × risk stratification

## Management: STEMI / Equivalents

Same as above **PLUS:**

- Active **primary PCI** call
- Revascularisation within 12 hrs (or <24 hrs with ongoing ischaemia):
  - **Primary PCI within 90 min**, or
  - **Thrombolysis (TNK)**
- Post-PCI monitoring:
  - Symptoms
  - CK peak 4–8 hrs post-reperfusion (vs 12 hrs without reperfusion)
  - ECG Q8H × 3 (>50% STE resolution at 60–90 min; if not: **rescue PCI**)
- Avoid **NSAID/steroid** early (aneurysm/myocardial rupture risk)
- Monitor radial artery wound
