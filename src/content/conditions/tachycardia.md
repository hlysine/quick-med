# Tachycardia

> **Typically refers to when HR >100**

## How to determine the URGENCY?
* What is the **blood pressure**?
* How **high** is the HR? Is it **new** tachycardia?
* Is the patient **alert**? Any **chest discomfort**?

> **Verbal order:** BPx1, ECG LLII, cardiac monitor

> **Unstable tachycardia** = shock attributed to tachyarrhythmia (when HR >150) / altered consciousness / angina / acute decompensated heart failure -> **warrants electrical cardioversion**  
> *(in most hospitals, it might be better if you call MO before initiating electrical cardioversion)*

## Sinus / Atrial tachycardia
* **Find and treat underlying cause**
    * ECG (consider CTPA if R heart strain: R axis deviation, RBBB, TWI inferior leads, S1Q3T3)
    * CBC d/c, L/RFT, CaPO4, Mg, TFT
* **Fluid challenge** (e.g. NS or Hartmann 500ml FR/Q1H) as tolerated

## SVT
* **Vagal maneuvres:** carotid sinus massage (C/I if carotid bruit) or Valsalva (if can cooperate)
* **ATP (C/I in asthma, COPD, ADHF, ACS, heart block)**
    * ATP 10mg bolus (*by houseman: crash trolley standby -> rapid injection of ATP -> flush with at least 5ml NS -> elevate the limb to accelerate drug delivery to the heart*)
    * Print rhythm strip from cardiac monitor before & after ATP; may record asystole (usually transient) -> bradycardia -> sinus rhythm -> SVT may recur
    * If unsuccessful cardioversion, repeat 10-20mg boluses once/twice in 1-2min intervals
* Still unsuccessful but **stable: verapamil** (2.5-)5mg IV slow injection over 3min under cardiac monitor
    * Can try digoxin/BB/CCB; acc to VERY BASIC manual: CCB more likely to terminate arrhythmia, BB just slow rate and more likely hypotension
* Still unsuccessful + **unstable:** 50J **synchronised** cardioversion, increase by 50-100J increments
* Consult MED x long-term Mx (ablation; BB/CCB/class I/III antiarrhythmic)

## Fast AF / atrial flutter
* **Unstable: synchronized** cardioversion 120-200J for AF, 50-100J for atrial flutter

> Usually only tachycardia > 150bpm causes significant BP drop; think twice before cardioverting – **is the shock cardiogenic?** Or is it hypovolemia/sepsis causing concomitant shock and fast AF?

* **Rate 100-130, stable:**
    * Fluid challenge
    * Oral BB/CCB e.g. **metoprolol 2.5mg stat**
        * *BB C/I in asthma, COPD, acute heart failure, ACS, AV block, WPW (pre-excited AF)*
* **Rate >130, stable:** IV antiarrhythmic
    * Common choices:
    * **Diltiazem** 0.25mg/kg (standard loading dose 10mg) slow injection over 3-5min under cardiac monitor (*by houseman*)
        * +/- maintenance infusion 50mg in 50ml NS 5-10ml/hr (W/H if SBP<100 or HR<80)
        * *CCB C/I in acute heart failure, AV block, WPW (pre-excited AF)*
    * **Amiodarone** 150mg in 100ml D5 Q30min (loading) +/- 10-50mg/hr (maintenance: standard dilution 600mg in 500ml D5, either Q24H or change rate to desired ml/hr)
        * Best if on anticoagulation already (small stroke risk from rhythm control)
        * *C/I in acute heart failure, AV block, known thyrotoxicosis* or young female with no TFT in recent 1 month (amiodarone can trigger thyroid storm!)
        * Avoid in atrial flutter (does not slow AV node -> exacerbates rapid conduction)
    * If CHF (LVEF<40%) or BP borderline: **digoxin** 250mcg IV Q8H x3 (loading) +/- 125mcg IV Q24H after (maintenance)
        * Slow onset around 1hr
        * *C/I in AV block, VT/VF; avoid if renal failure or hyperK* (exacerbated by digoxin) or hypoK/hypoMg/hyperCa (increased digoxin sensitivity -> predispose to toxicity)
    * Avoid co-prescribing amiodarone and digoxin (increased amiodarone toxicity: long QT)
    * Pre-excited AF: IV procainamide (C/I in AV block, Torsade, SLE, MG); avoid AVN blocking agents e.g. ATP, BB, CCB, digoxin, amiodarone
* **Blood workup:** CBC d/c, CRP, L/RFT, Ca/PO4, Mg, TFT; aim K>4
* **New AF/flutter:** consult MED x long-term Mx (rate/rhythm control + anticoagulation +/- ablation)

## Regular wide complex tachycardia: SVT with conduction aberrancy (block/pre-excitation) or monomorphic VT
* **Unstable:** 100J **synchronised** cardioversion
* **Stable: class I/III antiarrhythmics** e.g. amiodarone / procainamide / lignocaine
    * *Avoid AVN blocking agents in VT: ATP, BB, CCB, digoxin*
* **Blood workup:** CBC d/c, CRP, L/RFT, Ca/PO4, Mg, TnI/CK Q4H x2
* **Post-termination 12-lead ECG** x underlying cause: ischemia, HCM, long QT, WPW antidromic AVRT
* Consult MED x cardiac workup +/- intervention e.g. ICD

## Polymorphic VT
* **UNsynchronised cardioversion** 150-360J no matter stable or not (since it degenerates to VF)
* **Blood workup:** CBC d/c, CRP, L/RFT, Ca/PO4, Mg, TnI/CK Q4H x2
* **Post-termination 12-lead ECG** x underlying cause: ischemia, long QT
    * **Long QT: MgSO4 1-2g in 10ml D5 IV bolus, correct K and Ca, discontinue offending drugs** (class I/III antiarrhythmics, quinolones/macrolides, antipsychotics, antidepressants), consult CCU for isoproterenol
    * Ischemia: off catecholamines, anti-anginal drugs (nitrates, BB), revascularisation if ACS
* Urgent consult MED/CCU
