# Tachycardia

## Definition

- HR >100

## Assessment

- BP, HR level, alertness, chest discomfort
- **Unstable tachycardia** = shock attributed to tachyarrhythmia (HR >150) / altered consciousness / angina / ADHF → **electrical cardioversion**

## Management

### Sinus / Atrial Tachycardia

- Find and treat underlying cause
- Investigations: ECG, consider CTPA if R heart strain; CBC d/c, L/RFT, Ca/PO4, Mg, TFT
- Fluid challenge: NS / Hartmann 500 ml FR/Q1H

### SVT

- Vagal manoeuvres: carotid sinus massage (C/I if carotid bruit) or Valsalva
- **ATP 10 mg IV bolus**
  - C/I in asthma, COPD, ADHF, ACS, heart block
  - Crash trolley standby; rapid injection then flush ≥5 ml NS; elevate limb
  - Print rhythm strip before and after
  - If unsuccessful: repeat 10–20 mg bolus once or twice at 1–2 min intervals
- Still unsuccessful + **stable:** verapamil 2.5–5 mg IV slow over 3 min
- Still unsuccessful + **unstable:** 50 J synchronised cardioversion, increasing by 50–100 J
- Consult MED for long-term management

### Fast AF / Atrial Flutter

**Unstable:**
- AF: synchronised cardioversion 120–200 J
- Atrial flutter: synchronised cardioversion 50–100 J

**Rate 100–130, stable:**
- Fluid challenge
- Oral BB or CCB — e.g. metoprolol 2.5 mg stat
  - BB C/I in asthma, COPD, acute HF, ACS, AV block, WPW

**Rate >130, stable — IV antiarrhythmic:**

| Agent | Dose | Maintenance | Contraindications |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Diltiazem** | 0.25 mg/kg (standard 10 mg) slow over 3–5 min | 50 mg in 50 ml NS at 5–10 ml/hr; W/H if SBP <100 or HR <80 | Acute HF, AV block, WPW |
| **Amiodarone** | 150 mg in 100 ml D5 over 30 min | 600 mg in 500 ml D5 | Acute HF, AV block, known thyrotoxicosis, young female without recent TFT; **avoid in atrial flutter** |
| **Digoxin** (CHF with LVEF <40% or borderline BP) | 250 mcg IV Q8H × 3 (loading) | 125 mcg IV Q24H | AV block, VT/VF; avoid in renal failure / hyperK / hypoK / hypoMg / hyperCa; avoid co-prescribing with amiodarone |
| **Procainamide** (pre-excited AF) | IV — see MED | — | AV block, Torsade, SLE, MG; **avoid AVN-blocking agents** |

**Blood workup:** CBC d/c, CRP, L/RFT, Ca/PO4, Mg, TFT; **aim K >4**

- New AF/flutter → consult MED

### Regular Wide Complex Tachycardia (SVT with aberrancy or monomorphic VT)

- **Unstable:** 100 J synchronised cardioversion
- **Stable:** class I/III antiarrhythmics (amiodarone / procainamide / lignocaine)
  - **Avoid AVN-blocking agents in VT** (ATP, BB, CCB, digoxin)
- Blood: CBC d/c, CRP, L/RFT, Ca/PO4, Mg, TnI/CK Q4H × 2
- Post-termination 12-lead ECG; consult MED

### Polymorphic VT

- **Unsynchronised cardioversion 150–360 J** regardless of stability
- Blood workup as above
- Post-termination ECG

**Long QT:**
- MgSO4 1–2 g in 10 ml D5 IV bolus
- Correct K / Ca
- Discontinue offending drugs: class I/III antiarrhythmics, quinolones / macrolides, antipsychotics, antidepressants
- Consult CCU for isoproterenol

**Ischaemia:**
- Off catecholamines
- Anti-anginals
- Revascularisation if ACS

- Urgent consult MED / CCU
