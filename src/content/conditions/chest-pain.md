# Chest Pain

## Causes
* Cardiac: **ACS**, stable angina, myocarditis, pericarditis, Takotsubo (stress) cardiomyopathy
* Vascular: **aortic dissection**, aortic aneurysm, **PE**
* Respiratory: **PTX**, pneumonia, pneumonitis, bronchitis, pleuritis
* GI: **oesophageal tear/perforation**, oesophagitis, **GERD**, gastritis, PUD, **PPU**, pancreatitis, gallstone, biliary colic, cholecystitis, cholangitis
* MSK: costochondritis, rib fracture, flail chest, autoimmune disease
* Neurological: zoster
* Psychiatric: anxiety

> Beware of atypical presentation of ACS in diabetics, elderly and women!

## Assessment
* Vitals, respiratory distress
* **SOCRATES of chest pain:** is it chest or epigastric/RUQ pain? Is it new or different in characteristic?
    * **Anginal:** dull, left-sided/central, radiate to left shoulder/arm, +/- SOBOE, palpitations, N/V
    * **Tearing** (aortic dissection, oesophageal perforation): sharp, radiate to back
    * **Pleuritic:** sharp, exacerbated with respiratory movements/coughing, +/- SOB, cough
    * **Dyspepsia/heartburn/GERD:** acid regurgitation, belching, N/V, related to mealtime
    * **Mechanical** (costochondritis, rib fracture): chest wall tenderness, +/- crepitus, flail chest
    * **Neuropathic** (zoster): dermatomal tingling and sensory loss
* Radio-radial / radio-femoral delay (if suspicion of dissection: listen for AR murmur)
* Calves: soft, non-tender? Asymmetric calf swelling? (If yes: order bilateral calf circumference x1)

> **Clues for oesophageal perforation:**
> * Hx of **vomiting/retching** then chest pain/UGIB
> * **Surgical emphysema**/pleural effusion on CXR
>
> -> NPO, IVF, avoid R/T, IV broad spectrum abx, urgent consult SUR

## Management
* **Angina: need to r/o ACS!**
    * Cardiac monitor
    * ECG stat + Q4H x 1-2 more
    * Urgent serial TnI/CK Q4H x 2-3 sets
    * Sublingual TNG prn, +/- Elantan, Isordil
    * If stable angina (known, no exacerbation): consider start aspirin, refer MED x IHD
    * **If ACS: Refer to section on elevated TnI/TnT/CK for management of ACS!**
* **Tearing/radiating to back: need to r/o aortic dissection!**
    * Bilateral arm BP x1 (>20mmHg difference is significant)
    * Urgent PA CXR (for widened mediastinum/aortic knob)
    * Urgent CT aortogram
* Pleuritic/mechanical: Panadol, +/- CXR, XR ribs, +/- ECG (for PE, pericarditis)
    * If SOB: refer to section on SOB
* Dyspepsia/heartburn/GERD: refer to sections on dyspepsia/epigastric pain
* Neuropathic: look for other dermatome involvement (disseminated zoster needs airborne precaution), vesicular fluid/swab x VZV, acyclovir/ganciclovir, Lyrica/gabapentin, consult MED
