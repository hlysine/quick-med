## Causes

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Cardiac | ACS, stable angina, myocarditis, pericarditis, Takotsubo cardiomyopathy |
| Vascular | Aortic dissection, aortic aneurysm, PE |
| Respiratory | PTX, pneumonia, pneumonitis, bronchitis, pleuritis |
| GI | Oesophageal tear/perforation, oesophagitis, GERD, gastritis, PUD, PPU, pancreatitis, gallstone, biliary colic, cholecystitis, cholangitis |
| MSK | Costochondritis, rib fracture, flail chest, autoimmune |
| Neurological | Zoster |
| Psychiatric | Anxiety |

## Assessment

- **Vitals**, respiratory distress
- **SOCRATES** of chest pain — pain character guides diagnosis:

| Character | Likely Cause | Features |
|---|---|---|
| Dull, left-sided/central, radiates to left shoulder/arm | Anginal (ACS/angina) | ± SOBOE, palpitations, N/V |
| Tearing, radiates to back | Aortic dissection, oesophageal perforation | Sharp, sudden onset |
| Sharp, worse with breathing/coughing | Pleuritic | ± SOB, cough |
| Acid regurgitation, related to meals | Dyspepsia/GERD | Belching, N/V |
| Chest wall tenderness, ± crepitus | Mechanical (costochondritis, rib fracture) | Flail chest if multiple |
| Dermatomal tingling and sensory loss | Neuropathic (zoster) | Look for rash |

- **Radio-radial / radio-femoral delay** (if dissection suspected — listen for AR murmur)
- **Calves:** soft, non-tender? Asymmetric calf swelling?

> **Beware atypical ACS presentation in diabetics, elderly, and women**

## Management

### Anginal (r/o ACS)

- Cardiac monitor
- **ECG stat + Q4H ×1–2**
- **Urgent serial TnI/CK Q4H ×2–3 sets**
- Sublingual TNG prn ± Elantan/Isordil
- Stable angina (known, no exacerbation): consider starting aspirin, refer MED x IHD
- If ACS: **refer to Elevated TnI/TnT/CK section**

### Tearing/Radiating to Back (r/o Aortic Dissection)

- **Bilateral arm BP ×1**
- Urgent PA CXR (widened mediastinum/aortic knob)
- **Urgent CT aortogram**

### Pleuritic/Mechanical

- Panadol ± CXR + XR ribs + ECG (for PE/pericarditis)
- If SOB: refer to SOB section

### Dyspepsia/Heartburn/GERD

- Refer to **Dyspepsia section**

### Neuropathic (Zoster)

- Look for other dermatome involvement (**disseminated zoster needs airborne precaution**)
- Vesicular fluid/swab x VZV
- Acyclovir/ganciclovir
- Lyrica/gabapentin
- Consult MED

### Oesophageal Perforation (Clues)

- Hx of vomiting/retching then chest pain/UGIB
- Surgical emphysema/pleural effusion on CXR
- **Management:** NPO + IVF + avoid R/T + IV broad spectrum antibiotics + urgent consult SUR
