# Elevated D-dimer

> **Important ddx: PE, DIC**

## Causes of Elevated D-dimer
* **Thromboembolism**
    * Arterial: ACS, CVA, acute limb ischaemia
    * Venous: DVT, PE
    * DIC
* Thrombolytic therapy
* **Inflammation:** sepsis (very common in COVID!), surgery/trauma
* **Cancer**
* Liver disease (reduced clearance of fibrin degradation products)
* Nephrotic syndrome
* Pregnancy

> D-dimer is non-specific and only used to exclude PE in low-risk patients, so **do NOT order it** unless you are 1) planning to use it to book a CTPA, 2) suspecting DIC

## Approach
* Assess for any clinical bleeding/thromboembolism
* **If any S/S of PE -> consider urgent CTPA**
    * Risk factors (Virchow's triad): immobilisation, pregnant, OCP/estrogen, CA, nephrotic syndrome, thrombophilia/APLS, past DVT/PE
    * Unexplained desat/sinus tach; pleuritic chest pain, SOB, hemoptysis, calf tenderness/swelling
    * ECG sinus tachycardia, R heart strain (R axis deviation, counterclockwise rotation, RVH, RBBB, TWI R sided and inferior leads, S1Q3T3 (non-sensitive))
* If bleeding symptom: check CBC, clotting, fibrinogen (look for DIC)
