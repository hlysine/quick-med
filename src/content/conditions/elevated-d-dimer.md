## Important Differentials

- **PE**
- **DIC**

---

## Causes

- **Thromboembolism:**
  - Arterial: ACS, CVA, acute limb ischaemia
  - Venous: DVT, PE
  - DIC
- Thrombolytic therapy
- Inflammation: sepsis, very common in COVID, surgery/trauma
- Cancer
- Liver disease (reduced clearance)
- Nephrotic syndrome
- Pregnancy

---

## Approach

### Assess for Clinical Bleeding / Thromboembolism

**If any signs/symptoms of PE:**
- Consider urgent **CTPA**
- Risk factors (Virchow's triad): immobilisation, pregnancy, OCP/estrogen, malignancy, nephrotic syndrome, thrombophilia/APLS, past DVT/PE
- Symptoms: unexplained desaturation, sinus tachycardia, pleuritic chest pain, SOB, haemoptysis, calf tenderness/swelling
- ECG findings: sinus tachycardia, right heart strain (R axis deviation, counterclockwise rotation, RVH, RBBB, TWI in right-sided and inferior leads, **S1Q3T3**)

**If bleeding symptoms:**
- Check **CBC + clotting + fibrinogen** (look for DIC)

---

## Key Principle

> D-dimer is **non-specific** and only used to **EXCLUDE PE in low-risk patients**

**Do NOT order** unless:
1. Planning to use result to book a CTPA, or
2. Suspecting DIC
