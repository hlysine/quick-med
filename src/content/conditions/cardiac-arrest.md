# Cardiac Arrest

> **No pulse, no breathing**

## Your role as a houseman
Attend arrest call **immediately**. Usually, houseman's role is to **set venous access and take arrest bloods**, as most competent nurses will deliver ACLS according to algorithm, and your MO (+/- medical +/- anes/ICU) will also be called in to 1) discuss DNACPR with relatives, 2) supervise ACLS, 3) intubate, 4) look for underlying causes. But **be prepared to lead the ACLS** if you happen to be with an inexperienced team!

If it is a DNACPR case, you will receive a phone call to **certify the patient** when the cardiac rhythm is flat. This is NOT urgent, and some relatives prefer to witness their relatives' "last moment" before the official certification time. But as courtesy to the bereaving family, consider completing the procedure within 1-2hrs of the relatives' arrival, and if you are comfortable, speak to them briefly 「我係實習醫生，啱啱同XXX 做左檢查，佢已經無呼吸，無心跳，即係已經無生命既跡象。我地紀錄死亡時間係 xx:xx。希望你地可以節哀順變」. Most relatives appreciate that (you are the last doctor to care for the patient)!

## ACLS algorithm
* Early recognition: **check pulse and breathing for all unresponsive patients!** Don't miss an arrest!
* No pulse, no breathing:
    1. Activate arrest call
    2. Start continuous chest compressions +/- defibrillations (circulation first! C -> A -> B)
    3. **Don full PPE before starting ventilations** (no need if already intubated)
* **Arrest bloods:** CBC d/c, clotting, L/RFT, Ca/PO4, ABG, lactate, NH3, RG, TnI/CK
    * +/- T&S, blood c/st if indicated

> **Tips for taking arrest blood:**
> * Take from **femoral artery** (at the mid-inguinal point) using 20ml (or 10ml) syringe
> * If cannot feel pulse: blind puncture by landmark and perform sunray motion while aspirating
> * Memorise the bottle colours so you can take the blood first then go back to issue the tests:
>   1 blue (Na citrate), 3-4 green (Li heparin), 2 purple (K EDTA), 2 grey (Na fluoride) bottles
> * Save the arrest blood template in your CMS account!

## Adult Cardiac Arrest Algorithm

* **1. Start CPR**
  * Give oxygen
  * Attach monitor/defibrillator
* **Rhythm shockable?**
  * **Yes (VF/pVT):**
    * **2.** Shock
    * **3.** CPR 2 min
      * IV/IO access
    * Rhythm shockable?
      * **Yes:**
        * **4.** Shock
        * **5.** CPR 2 min
          * Epinephrine every 3-5 min
          * Consider advanced airway, capnography
        * Rhythm shockable?
          * **Yes:**
            * **6.** Shock
            * **7.** CPR 2 min
              * Amiodarone or lidocaine
              * Treat reversible causes
  * **No (Asystole/PEA):**
    * **8.** Epinephrine ASAP
    * **9.** CPR 2 min
      * IV/IO access
      * Epinephrine every 3-5 min
      * Consider advanced airway, capnography
    * Rhythm shockable?
      * **Yes:** Go to VF/pVT loop (steps 5 or 7)
      * **No:**
        * **10.** CPR 2 min
          * Treat reversible causes
        * Rhythm shockable?
          * **Yes:** Go to VF/pVT loop
          * **No:**
            * **11.** If no signs of return of spontaneous circulation (ROSC), go to 10 or 11; if ROSC, go to Post-Cardiac Arrest Care. Consider appropriateness of continued resuscitation.

**CPR Quality:**
* Push hard (at least 5 cm) and fast (100-120/min) and allow complete chest recoil.
* Minimize interruptions in compressions.
* Avoid excessive ventilation.
* Change compressor every 2 minutes, or sooner if fatigued.
* If no advanced airway, 30:2 compression-ventilation ratio.
* Quantitative waveform capnography: If PETCO2 is low or decreasing, reassess CPR quality.

**Shock Energy for Defibrillation:**
* **Biphasic:** Manufacturer recommendation (initial dose of 120-200 J); if unknown, use maximum available. Second and subsequent doses should be equivalent, and higher doses may be considered.
* **Monophasic:** 360 J

**Drug Therapy:**
* **Epinephrine IV/IO dose:** 1 mg every 3-5 minutes
* **Amiodarone IV/IO dose:** First dose: 300 mg bolus. Second dose: 150 mg. OR
  **Lidocaine IV/IO dose:** First dose: 1-1.5 mg/kg. Second dose: 0.5-0.75 mg/kg.

**Advanced Airway:**
* Endotracheal intubation or supraglottic advanced airway.
* Waveform capnography or capnometry to confirm and monitor ET tube placement.
* Once advanced airway in place, give 1 breath every 6 seconds (10 breaths/min) with continuous chest compressions.

**Return of Spontaneous Circulation (ROSC):**
* Pulse and blood pressure
* Abrupt sustained increase in PETCO2 (typically >= 40 mm Hg)
* Spontaneous arterial pressure waves with intra-arterial monitoring

**Reversible Causes:**
* Hypovolemia
* Hypoxia
* Hydrogen ion (acidosis)
* Hypo-/hyperkalemia
* Hypothermia
* Tension pneumothorax
* Tamponade, cardiac
* Toxins
* Thrombosis, pulmonary
* Thrombosis, coronary

## Post-ROSC (return of spontaneous circulation) management
* Check vitals
* Cardiac monitoring
* ECG, CXR
* Continue to look for and treat underlying cause
