# Cardiac Arrest

> **No pulse, no breathing**

## Your role as a houseman

Attend arrest call **immediately**. Usually, houseman's role is to **set venous access and take arrest bloods**, as most
competent nurses will deliver ACLS according to algorithm, and your MO (+/- medical +/- anes/ICU) will also be called in
to 1) discuss DNACPR with relatives, 2) supervise ACLS, 3) intubate, 4) look for underlying causes. But **be prepared to
lead the ACLS** if you happen to be with an inexperienced team!

If it is a DNACPR case, you will receive a phone call to **certify the patient** when the cardiac rhythm is flat. This
is NOT urgent, and some relatives prefer to witness their relatives' "last moment" before the official certification
time. But as courtesy to the bereaving family, consider completing the procedure within 1-2hrs of the relatives'
arrival, and if you are comfortable, speak to them briefly 「我係實習醫生，啱啱同XXX 做左檢查，佢已經無呼吸，無心跳，即係已經無生命既跡象。我地紀錄死亡時間係
xx:xx。希望你地可以節哀順變」. Most relatives appreciate that (you are the last doctor to care for the patient)!

## ACLS algorithm

* Early recognition: **check pulse and breathing for all unresponsive patients!** Don't miss an arrest!
* No pulse, no breathing:
  1. Activate arrest call
  2. Start continuous chest compressions +/- defibrillations (circulation first! C -> A -> B)
  3. **Don full PPE before starting ventilations** (no need if already intubated)
* **Arrest bloods:** CBC d/c, clotting, L/RFT, Ca/PO4, ABG, lactate, NH3, RG, TnI/CK
  * +/- T&S, blood c/st if indicated

> **Tips for taking arrest blood:**
> * Take from **femoral artery** (at the mid-inguinal point) using 20ml (or 10ml) syringe
> * If cannot feel pulse: blind puncture by landmark and perform sunray motion while aspirating
> * Memorise the bottle colours so you can take the blood first then go back to issue the tests:
    > 1 blue (Na citrate), 3-4 green (Li heparin), 2 purple (K EDTA), 2 grey (Na fluoride) bottles
> * Save the arrest blood template in your CMS account!



## Post-ROSC (return of spontaneous circulation) management

* Check vitals
* Cardiac monitoring
* ECG, CXR
* Continue to look for and treat underlying cause
