# Diarrhoea

## Causes
* **Watery/loose stool:** GE, IBS, malabsorption, thyrotoxicosis, spurious diarrhoea (chronic constipation)
    * *Ddx: faecal incontinence (usually chronic; if acute, r/o cord compression!)*
* **Bloody diarrhoea:** dysentery (EHEC, Campylobacter, Shigella, Amoeba)
    * *Rule out other causes of PRB (see next section)!*

## Assessment
* GI bleed symptoms, abd pain, vomiting
* +/- thyrotoxic symptoms
* Hydration status
* Abdomen
* Drugs e.g. colchicine, metformin

> **How to read stool chart:**
> * **BLS** = brown loose stool
> * **GSS** = green soft stool
> * **YWS** = yellow watery stool
> * **PRB** = per rectal bleed
> * **"Melaena"** may be true melaena, or may be iron-stained or dark green stool!

## Management
* Chart I/O
* **Monitor electrolytes** (RFT, Ca/PO4, Mg, VBG)
* If persistent: stool x c/st, GE viruses, (+ CD toxin if admission >96hrs / recent antibiotic / IBD)
* **PO Imodium prn**
* **Withhold laxatives** e.g. lactulose/ Dulcolax/ senna (be careful if lactulose given for liver patients – need to maintain BO to prevent HE!)
* **Off offending drugs** e.g. colchicine, recently started metformin
* **If fever/bloody/mucoid stool:** antibiotic e.g. levofloxacin

## Management of C diff Infection
* **Contact precautions – and wash hands using soap (to kill its spores), not alcohol rub!**
* **Non-fulminant**
    * **PO vancomycin 125mg QID x 10 days** or
    * PO fidaxomicin 200mg BD x 10 days
* **Fulminant** (shock, ileus, toxic megacolon):
    * PO vanco 500mg QID + **IV Flagyl** 500mg Q8H, consult SUR
