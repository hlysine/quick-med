## Causes

| Type | Causes |
|---|---|
| Watery / loose stool | GE, IBS, malabsorption, thyrotoxicosis, spurious diarrhoea |
| Bloody diarrhoea (dysentery) | EHEC, Campylobacter, Shigella, Amoeba |
| Faecal incontinence | If acute: rule out **cord compression** |

## Assessment

- GI bleed symptoms, abdominal pain, vomiting
- ± Thyrotoxic symptoms
- Hydration status
- Abdominal exam
- Drugs: colchicine, metformin

## Management

- Chart I/O
- Monitor electrolytes: RFT, Ca/PO4, Mg, VBG
- If persistent: stool x c/st + GE viruses
  - **+ C. diff toxin** if admission >96hrs / recent antibiotics / IBD
- PO Imodium prn
- Withhold laxatives (keep lactulose for liver patients to prevent HE)
- Off offending drugs
- If fever / bloody / mucoid stool: **antibiotics** (levofloxacin)

## C. difficile Infection

> **Contact precautions + wash hands with SOAP** (kills spores — alcohol does NOT)

### Non-Fulminant

- **PO vancomycin 125mg QID ×10 days**
- OR **PO fidaxomicin 200mg BD ×10 days**

### Fulminant (Shock, Ileus, Toxic Megacolon)

- **PO vancomycin 500mg QID + IV Flagyl 500mg Q8H**
- Consult SUR
