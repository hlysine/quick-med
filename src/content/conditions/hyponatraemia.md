## Key Cut-offs

- Normal range: **135–145 mmol/L**
- Correct at rate **<6–8 mmol/L over 24 hrs**
- Usually treat when **Na <125** or **symptomatic**

---

## Causes (by Volume Status)

| Volume Status | Causes |
|---|---|
| **Hypovolaemic** | GI loss (vomiting / diarrhoea), non-sensible skin loss, renal loss (diuretics / mineralocorticoid deficiency) |
| **Euvolaemic** | SIADH (diagnosis of exclusion), hypocortisolism, hypothyroidism, excess IVF, primary polydipsia, beer potomania |
| **Hypervolaemic** | CHF, cirrhosis, nephrotic syndrome, severe renal failure |
| **Pseudohyponatraemia** | Hyperglycaemia (dilutional), hyperglobulinaemia (e.g. multiple myeloma) |

---

## Assessment

- Rule out **symptomatic hyponatraemia** (neurological symptoms)
- Assess **fluid status**
- Exclude **drip arm** as cause of low Na

---

## Management

### If Symptomatic
- Neuro-obs Q4H +/- CT brain
- **Consult ICU**

### Investigations
- Blood: **osmolality + TFT + AM cortisol** (especially if euvolaemic)
- Paired urine: **osmolality + sodium**
- **RFT Q12H** to monitor trend — aim Na rise **<6–8 mmol/day**

### Interpretation of Urine Results
- In **true hypoNa**: urine osmo <100 and urine Na <20 expected
- If urine osmo >100 or urine Na >20: kidney problem or ADH excess (SIADH etc.)

### Replacement by Volume Status

| Volume Status | Treatment |
|---|---|
| **Dry (Hypovolaemic)** | NS IVF (rate based on degree of dryness); use 1/2:1/2 if diabetic |
| **Euvolaemic (SIADH)** | NaCl PO tablets (900 mg per tablet) + fluid restriction <1–1.2 L/day; start 900 mg BD/TDS x 3/7, step up to 1800–2700 mg |
| **Hypervolaemic** | Treat underlying cause; fluid restriction + low-salt diet; IV Lasix 40 mg Q8H (20 mg Q8H or Q12H if borderline BP); Aldactone if cirrhosis |

---

## Pitfall: Osmotic Demyelination Syndrome

- If rate of correction **>10 mmol/day**: risk of osmotic demyelination syndrome
- Check for neurological symptoms; **slow or reverse correction** if overshoot detected
