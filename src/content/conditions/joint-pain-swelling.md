## Causes

- Osteoarthritis
- **Septic arthritis**
- Rheumatological (including gout and pseudogout)
- Trauma
- Haemarthrosis (trauma / coagulopathy)
- Limb swelling near joints — **malignancy**: osteosarcoma, lymphoma, soft tissue sarcoma

## Assessment

- **Clinical history**: onset (acute vs chronic), joints involved (small vs large, mono vs polyarticular), trauma
- **Risk factors for septic arthritis**: prosthesis, immunosuppression/DM, recent surgery, IVDU
- **Physical exam**: fever, swelling, crepitations, warmth, range of movement
- **XR bilateral joints**

## Management

### Osteoarthritis

- Analgesics, physiotherapy, weight loss
- Refer orthopaedics if debilitating

### Septic Arthritis

- WCC/CRP elevated, fever, monoarticular, angry joint
- **NPOEM** until knee tap aspirate Gram smear negative
- **Urgent consult orthopaedics** for arthrocentesis ± arthroscopic washout
- IV antibiotics: empirical, then directed by Gram stain / culture

### Gouty Arthritis

- History of gout / hyperuricaemia, metabolic syndrome, low-grade fever, inability to move joint
- **If 1st episode / fever / no known gout history**: urgent consult orthopaedics
- **Treatment**:
  - Low purine diet +
  - **Colchicine** 0.5 mg PO TDS (withhold if diarrhoea) × 3/7  
    **OR**
  - **Voltaren SR** 100 mg daily + **Pantoloc** 40 mg daily × 3/7
  - **If renal impairment**: corticosteroids instead
- Check **serum urate** after acute episode + **HLA-B\*5801** before starting allopurinol
- Refer / consult medicine if recurrent / persistent / tophi
