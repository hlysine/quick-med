# Joint Pain / Swelling

## Causes
* Osteoarthritis
* **Septic arthritis**
* Rheumatological causes (**including gout** and pseudogout)
* **Trauma**
* Haemarthrosis (trauma, coagulopathy)
* Limb swelling near joints (*not at joints*): Malignancy (osteosarcoma/ lymphoma/ soft tissue sarcoma)

## Assessment
* Clinical history – **onset** (acute vs chronic), involvement of joints (small vs big; mono vs poly), **trauma**
    * *Risk factors for septic arthritis:* prosthesis, immunosuppression (DM), recent joint surgery, IVDU
* Physical examination – any **fever**, any swelling, crepitations, warmth, range of movement
* **X-ray** bilateral joints

## Management
* Depends on underlying cause
* Osteoarthritis
    * Analgesics
    * Physiotherapy
    * Encourage weight loss
    * Refer ORT (not really need IP consult) if debilitating and indicated for joint replacement
* **Septic arthritis [WCC/ CRP increase, fever, monoarticular arthritis, angry looking joint]**
    * **NPOEM** (until knee tap aspirate Gram smear negative)
    * **Urgent consult ORT** for arthrocentesis +/- arthroscopic washout
    * **IV antibiotics** (empirical then step up according to Gram stain/ culture results)
* **Gouty arthritis [Hx of gout/ hyperuricaemia, metabolic syndrome, low grade fever, unable to move]**
    * **If suspicion of septic arthritis** (e.g. 1st episode mono-arthritis, fever, no known gout history): **urgent consult ORT**
    * Can treat empirically with low purine diet +
        * **Colchicine 0.5mg PO TDS (withhold if diarrhoea)** x 3/7 OR
        * **Voltaren SR 100mg PO daily with Pantoloc 40mg PO daily cover** x 3/7
        * *If renal impairment: prescribe corticosteroids*
    * Check clinical response; monitor renal function
    * Check urate **after** acute episode; + blood x HLA-B\*5801
    * Refer/consult MED if recurrent/persistent/ tophi
