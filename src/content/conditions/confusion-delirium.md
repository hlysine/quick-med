# Confusion / Delirium

## Causes
* **Dehydration** / hypotension / poor perfusion (HF, arrhythmia, SVCO) / hypertensive encephalopathy
* Hypoxia / **hypercapnia**
* **Hypoglycaemia** / hyperglycaemic crisis
* **AROU / constipation / pain**
* **Major stress:** sepsis, ACS/PE/dissection, acute abdomen, haemorrhage
* **Metabolic:** hyper/hypoNa, hyper/hypoCa, hyper/hypoMg, acidosis, uraemia, ↑ NH3, ↓ thiamine, ↓ folate/B12, myxoedema coma, Addisonian crisis, drug intoxication/withdrawal
* **Neurological: HI, CVA (ischaemic/haemorrhagic), seizure (convulsive/non-convulsive), CNS infection**
* Psychiatric: dementia, psychosis

## Assessment
* Vitals (*if fever + confusion, manage as CNS infection, need bld c/st & LP – refer to section on ↓ GCS*)
* **GCS, H'stix stat**
* I/O chart
* **Full neuro exam:** CN (pupils, EOM, CN7, dysarthria), 4 limbs power, cerebellar signs, **meningism**
* General exam: septic looking, resp distress, abdomen, calves
* Drug/alcohol history

> Sometimes nurses may just ask you to order restraint prn, but make sure to ask about the reason and if it is confusion, assess the patient!

> Physical restraint exacerbates delirium, does not prevent fall, and increases risks of bedsore, DVT, and aspiration! So it is a last resort to deal with disruptive acts e.g. pulling out drips/tubes/drains.

## Management (*choose relevant items based on clinical suspicion)
* Environmental cues: hearing/visual aids, side room/next to window, clock, visiting by familiar people
* Chart I/O, maintain hydration
* PO/IV thiamine
* PO/IV Haldol 0.5mg as directed prn (maximum 5mg/day) for agitation/psychotic symptoms
* **Off offending drugs** e.g. benzodiazepines (except when alcohol withdrawal), anticholinergics, opioids
* **CT brain** (must order if no obvious cause)
* Bld x CBC d/c, clotting, L/RFT, Ca/PO4, Mg, NH3, VBG, lactate, TnI/CK, TFT, B12, folate, spot cortisol
    * + ABG (instead of VBG) if CO2 retention suspected
    * + bld c/st if sepsis/CNS infection suspected
    * + serum toxicology if drug intoxication/withdrawal suspected
* MSU/CSU x m'stix, c/st, (+ toxicology if drug intoxication/withdrawal suspected)
* Sputum c/st if any
* PVRU x1
* CXR, KUB
* ECG
* LP, EEG
* Consult occupational therapist x cognitive assessment
* Consult MED/geri/PSY for delirium/dementia

> **Conscious sedation:**
> * Make sure airway equipment, O2, BVM, suction, IVF available
> * Monitor BP and SpO2, **ABORT if shock/desat!**
> * IV Dormicum 0.5-1mg Q2min (**maximum 5mg**) (onset 1-3min, duration 30min)
