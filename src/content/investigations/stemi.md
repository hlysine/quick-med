# STEMI

## Presentation

* Dull chest discomfort, tightness, pressure, squeezing, crushing
* Radiation to shoulder, upper arms, lower jaw, neck
* Shortness of breath, chest tightness without pain
* Fatigue, out of energy
* Palpitation, lightheadedness
* Pre-syncope, syncope
    * Especially raised vagal tone in inferior MI

## Investigation

* Coronary angiogram
    * 50% obstruction is the threshold for ischemia
    * Indicates intervention - stenting
* Intravascular ultrasound (IVUS)

## ECG findings in ischemia/infarction

* ST segment elevation
* ST segment depression
* Q waves
* T wave inversion

![ECG findings in myocardial ischemia](/assets/mi-ecg-findings-overview.png)

> **Differential diagnosis of ST elevation**
> * Acute STEMI
>     * Localizing convex ST elevation
>     * Reciprocal ST depression
> * Global ischemia
>     * ST elevation in aVR
>     * Diffuse ST depression
> * Acute pericarditis
>     * Diffuse concave ST elevation
>     * PR depression
> * LVH with strain pattern
>     * V1-3 concave ST elevation
>     * LVH features
> * Early repolarization / high take-off
>     * Concave ST elevation without reciprocal ST depression
>     * J point elevated
> * Left bundle branch block

## ST Elevation Myocardial Infarction (STEMI)

![ACS spectrum](/assets/stemi-acs-spectrum.png)

![STEMI evolution](/assets/stemi-evolution-stages.png)

### Presentation

* Intense pain >20 minutes
* Diaphoresis, nausea, rarely vomiting
* Differential diagnosis
    * Aortic dissection
        * Sudden onset sharp ripping pain
        * History of hypertension
    * Pericarditis
        * Positional/respiratory variation in pain
        * Worse when lying down
    * Other pathologies
        * Very localized pain
        * Fleeting pain - lasts a few seconds

### Hyperacute T wave

* Tall and broad T wave during acute onset of STEMI

### ST Changes

![ST morphology](/assets/stemi-st-morphology.png)

* ST elevation >2mm in V2-V3, >1mm in all other leads
    * Not a strict requirement
    * Correlate with presentation
* ST elevation in 2 leads of same area
* ST elevation concaving downward

### Reciprocal ST depression

* Anterior elevation = inferior depression
* Inferior elevation = lateral depression
* Lateral elevation = no reciprocal changes

### Abnormal Q wave

* Q duration > 30ms and >25-30% of R
* Not visible in NSTEMI

### Inferior STEMI

![Inferior STEMI](/assets/inferior-stemi-ecg.png)

* **ST elevation:** inferior leads (II, III, aVF)
* **Reciprocal ST depression:** I, aVL
* **Abnormal Q wave:** Inferior leads
* Right coronary artery occlusion
    * Often right ventricular/posterior infarction
        * Tall R wave, ST depression, upright T wave...

### Anterior STEMI

![Anterior STEMI](/assets/anterior-stemi-ecg.png)

* **ST elevation:** Septal (V1 V2) / Anterior (V3 V4) / Anterolateral (V5 V6)
* **Reciprocal ST depression:** II, III, aVF
* **Abnormal Q wave:** anterior leads
* Left anterior descending artery occlusion
    * Associated with higher mortality and complications

### de Winter T Waves

![de Winter T waves](/assets/de-winter-t-waves-ecg.png)

* No ST elevation but equivalent to anterior STEMI
* Depressed ST take-off
* Hyperacute T waves

### Posterior STEMI

![Posterior leads V7-V9](/assets/posterior-stemi-leads.png)

![Posterior STEMI ECG](/assets/posterior-stemi-ecg.png)

* **ST elevation:** Posterior leads (V7-V9 = V1-V3 flipped)
    * **Manifest as ST depression in V1-V3**
* **Reciprocal ST depression:** V1-V3
* **Abnormal Q wave:** Posterior leads (V7-V9 = V1-V3 flipped)
    * **Manifest as tall R wave in V1-V3**
* Tall and broad R in V1-V3
* **Upright T in V1-V3**
* R/S>1 in V2
* Commonly co-exist with inferior/lateral infarctions

> ST depression in V2-V3 may be NSTEMI or posterior STEMI. Requires ST elevation in posterior leads to confirm.
>
> PCI only applicable for STEMI.

### Right ventricular STEMI

![Right ventricular STEMI](/assets/right-ventricular-stemi-ecg.png)

* Prove with right-sided leads
* **ST elevation: V4R**
    * Occasionally a hint of ST elevation in V1 (V2R in mirrored placement)
* **Reciprocal ST depression:** V2 (V1R in mirrored placement)

### Global ischemia - STEMI-equivalent

![Global ischemia ECG](/assets/global-ischemia-stemi-equivalent-ecg.jpeg)

* **ST elevation**: aVR
* **Diffuse ST depression:** I, II, aVL, V4-6
* Treat as STEMI (50% chance)
