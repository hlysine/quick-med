# Anaemia / Hb Drop

## Important cut-offs:
1. **Hb < 7.0 g/dL** -> transfuse
2. **Hb drop by >= 1.0 g/dL within 1-2 days** -> assess for bleeding

> Blood loss charted in OT record may grossly underestimated!

## Approach to Hb drop
* Check history: Any major OT done? Any charted **bleeding episodes**?
* **Abd exam + PR exam for GI bleeding**
* **Check drain output and wound dressing** (for oozing blood) in post-op patients
* Repeat CBC + clotting mane; if continued Hb drop, transfuse to keep **Hb>7, Plt>50, INR<1.5**
* **W/H antiplatelets** (urgent consult cardiac if on DAPT), **W/H +/- reverse anticoagulation**

> If unexplained anaemia with no previous workup and no obvious bleeding source, **do anaemic workup** (e.g. iron profile, B12/folate, retic, L/RFT, TFT, FOBT x3) and consider refer MED/GOPC

## Common Transfusion Triggers
* **Hb < 7.0**
* Ongoing blood loss
* Symptomatic anaemia

> Each unit of red cell raises Hb by ~ 1g/dL

## Typical Transfusion Order
* **Transfuse 1 unit red cell, Q4H per unit** (some departments prefer Q3H)
    * Consider more if severe anaemia/ ongoing blood loss
    * Consider faster rate (e.g. Q1-2H) if unstable (e.g. decreased BP/ tachycardia) due to ongoing blood loss
* **Post-transfusion CBC, RFT** (*why RFT? Because transfusion can cause/exacerbate hyperK*)
    * If Hb doesn't rise after transfusion, **reassess for ongoing bleeding!**
* **Post-transfusion Lasix** (to prevent TACO; no need if clinically dehydrated or BP borderline)
    * IV 20mg or 40mg once (post-transfusion), omit if SBP<110
    * Consider give 1 dose of Lasix per pint if transfuse more than 1U
    * If already in APO before transfusion: consider pre-transfusion IV Lasix 40-60mg
    * **For CAPD patients: extra 4.25% PDF Q2H** instead of Lasix
    * **For HD patients:** if not too severe, transfuse during next HD session (no need Lasix)

> **Common pitfall: HyperK with anaemia**
> * Correct hyperK first!
> * If not severe hyperK: can transfuse simultaneously with K lowering drugs (Resonium A/C +/- DI drip)

> Transfusion is also **C/I in hyperleukocytosis** (e.g. WCC is 3-digit) - must consult MO first!

> **Anaemia due to CKD:** aim Hb 10-11.5 with ESA (and ensure not iron deficient); Hb >13 increases CV risk!
> **Avoid transfusion if possible in transplant candidates!**

## Indications for Special Blood Products

### Leucodepleted red cell
* Recurrent febrile non-haemolytic transfusion reaction (>= 2 consecutive)
* All thalassaemia major patients requiring regular transfusion
* Haematological disease
* Paediatrics oncological patients
* Prevention of transmission of viral infections (e.g. CMV)
    * HIV patients who are CMV seronegative
    * Alternative to CMV sero-negative blood components
* Intrauterine transfusion

### CMV negative blood products
* Elective transfusion during pregnancy (EXCEPT during labour, delivery, miscarriages or TOP)
* CMV antibody negative recipients of allogenic stem cell grafts
* Intrauterine transfusion
* Neonates with birth weight < 1.5kg

*However, should not withhold lifesaving transfusion if CMV negative bloods not available. Leucodepleted blood components are alternatives.*

### Irradiated cellular blood products (to prevent transfusion graft versus host disease)
* Severe congenital cellular immunodeficiency
* Allogenic haemopoietic stem cell transplant patients
* Autologous haematopoietic stem cell transplantation (from >= 7 days prior to bone marrow/ stem cell/ lymphocyte harvest until 3 months post transplant)
* CAR-T therapy
* Patient treated with purine analogs (e.g. fludarabine, cladribine, clofarabine, bendamustrine) until 12 months after completion of therapy
* Anti-T cell treatment (e.g. anti-thymocyte globulin, alemtuzumab, other T cells monoclonals)
    * For aplastic anaemia or leukaemia
    * From initiation of treatment until 12 months after completion of therapy
* Intrauterine transfusion
* Neonates who have previously received intrauterine transfusion undergoing top-up transfusion until 6 months of age
