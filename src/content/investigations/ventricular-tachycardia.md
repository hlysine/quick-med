# Ventricular Tachycardia (VT)

* Sustained if longer than 30s or requiring intervention
* May be polymorphic if complexes are from multiple foci/exits

## Torsades de pointes (TdP)

![Torsades de pointes ECG](/assets/torsades-de-pointes-ecg.png)

* QRS complex twists around isoelectric line due to long QT
* Caused by drugs or congenital

## Key features

* Broad and weird QRS complexes
* Absence of RBBB/LBBB features
* Extreme axis deviation - QRS positive in aVR, negative in I, aVF
* AV dissociation with V rate faster than A

    ![AV dissociation](/assets/vt-av-dissociation.png)

    * Indicated in 2 pairs of constant PP interval

* Capture beats - ventricles captured by dissociated atrial beat to produce normal QRS complex

    ![Capture beat](/assets/vt-capture-beat.png)

* Fusion complex - fusion of sinus and ventricular beat

    ![Fusion complex](/assets/vt-fusion-complex.png)

* Precordial leads all positive QRS or all negative

    ![Precordial concordance 1](/assets/vt-precordial-concordance-1.png)

    ![Precordial concordance 2](/assets/vt-precordial-concordance-2.png)

* RSR' complex with taller left ear is **specific to VT**

    ![RSR' rabbit ear sign](/assets/vt-rsr-rabbit-ear.png)

    * Taller right ear in RBBB

* Brugada sign - time from onset of Q to peak of S >100ms in any of V1-V6

    ![Brugada sign](/assets/vt-brugada-sign.png)

* Josephson sign - notching near nadir of S wave

    ![Josephson sign](/assets/vt-josephson-sign.png)
