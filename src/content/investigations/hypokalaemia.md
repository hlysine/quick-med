# Hypokalaemia

> Replacing K is the thing you do the most throughout houseman year...

> **Normal range: 3.5-5.0 mmol/L**

## ECG Features

![Hypokalaemia ECG](/assets/hypokalaemia-ecg.png)

* Prolonged PR interval
* Flattened T wave
* Prominent U wave
* +/- Tachyarrhythmia
* ST depression
* T wave inversion
* **Prominent U waves** - U wave taller than T wave
* Long QU interval

> **Correct K quickly!** If ACS/arrhythmia: **keep K > 4.0**

> Check carefully whether K has been replaced **ALREADY**, and the timing of the latest blood in relation to K replacements!

## Management
* If **K < 2.0 or ECG changes: cardiac monitoring +/- consult ICU**
* **Oral replacement whenever possible - PO KCl syrup 2g Q2H x 1-3 doses**
    * **Avoid syrup K in patients on chemotherapy/ with oral or GI ulcers**
    * Alternative: slow K 600mg tablets (its effect is slow - as the name suggests)
* **Consider IV replacement if oral route unavailable or when K < 2.5**
    * Baby K (or some places called conc K) - **irritates veins and cause thrombophlebitis!**
        * IV 10mmol K in 100ml water over 1hr x 1
        * IV 20mmol K in 100ml water over 2hrs x 1
    * Add KCl to IVF: not preferred unless set an **end date** to it (e.g. 10mmol K in 500ml NS Q8H x 1 day) or else easy to overshoot if no one remembers to off
* **Order post-RFT if significant hypoK** (e.g. <3.0)
* **Check Mg and VBG if persistent hypoK** even after replacement

### Expected increase in K after replacement
* Syrup KCl 2g = 27 mmol = around 0.3 mmol/L
* Slow K 600mg = 8 mmol = 0.1 mmol/L
* IV KCl 10 mmol = 0.13 mmol/L

> **Limit IV KCl to 40mmol/hr and 80mmol/day**

> **IV conc K has less K** than 1 dose of syrup KCl (it is just rapid), so if K < 2.5 remember to **give syrup KCl 2g x3 after rapid IV conc K!**

> If NPO (especially in surgery patients), you can order **NPO except Syrup KCl** (not even NPOEM)

### Tips on replacing K
* **Always check creatinine** - renal function which determines if the body can excrete K
* **Renal patients not on dialysis:** replace cautiously (e.g. syrup KCl 0.5-1g x1 if K < 3.0)
* **Patients on CAPD:** replace and **aim normokalaemia** (small RCT in Thailand shows benefit of reducing risk of CAPD peritonitis)
* **Patients on HD: almost NEVER replace** (can repeat RFT, if still K < 2.5 or ECG changes then give syrup KCl 0.5-1g x1)
