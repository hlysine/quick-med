# Acute Kidney Injury (AKI)

> **Definition: increased in serum Cr to >1.5x baseline within 1 week, or U/O < 0.5 ml/kg/hr for 6 hrs**

| Stage | Plasma creatinine INCREASE | (or) Urine output |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | Up to 2x increase PCr over a week<br>Or rise in PCr >= 26.5 umol/L (>= 0.3 mg/dL) | Oliguric (< 0.5 mL/kg/hr) for 6-12 hours |
| **2** | Up to 3x increase PCr over a week | Oliguric for >= 12 hours |
| **3** | More than 3x increase PCr over a week<br>Or rise in PCr >= 353.6 umol/L (>= 4.0 mg/dL) | Oliguric (< 0.3 mL/kg/hr) for 24 hours or anuria for >= 12 hours |

## Causes
* Pre-renal: **dehydration, shock**, cardiorenal/hepatorenal syndrome, bil RAS (+ACEI/ARB/NSAID)
* Renal (GN, AIN, ATN)
    * UTI
    * **Nephrotoxic drugs:** NSAID, aminoglycoside, acyclovir, tenofovir, radiocontrast
    * Tumour lysis, multiple myeloma, rhabdomyolysis etc.
* Post-renal: **obstructive stone / tumour / stricture**

## Complications: AEIOU
* **Acidosis**
* **Electrolyte abnormalities** (hyperK, hyperPO4, hypoCa)
* **Intoxication / drug toxicities**
* **Oedema / fluid overload**
* **Uraemic pericarditis/encephalopathy** (can cause severe pericardial effusion, seizures)

## Assessment
* **Vitals, fluid status**
* **Urinary symptoms:** loin pain, dysuria, haematuria, turbid urine, LUTS
* **Review drugs:** nephrotoxic drugs, anticholinergics/opioids (AROU), renally-excreted drugs
* **Cr baseline and trend, urea and electrolytes**

## Management
* **Chart U/O** (if severe AKI, consider foley to BSB for better U/O monitoring)
* Initial workup:
    * Bld x CBC d/c, RFT
    * MSU/CSU x m'stix, c/st
    * PVRU x1
    * **If suspect obstruction: KUB + urgent USG urinary system / NCCT A+P (stone protocol)**
* **If dry** (clinically, or increased urea:Cr ratio): **IV fluid replacement + maintenance**, encourage fluid intake
* **If fluid overload:** fluid restriction <1.2L/day, IV Lasix e.g. 40mg Q12H
    * If already on IV Lasix: increased dosage for renal failure (*uremic toxin displaces Lasix from albumin and decreases Lasix delivery to tubules*), increased frequency for heart failure, increased dosage + frequency (and add IV albumin 20g/day) for nephrotic syndrome
* If septic / UTI symptoms / increased WCC: empirical antibiotics (according to past urine c/st)
* **Off all nephrotoxic drugs if possible: NSAID, aminoglycoside, acyclovir, tenofovir**
* **Adjust/off all renally-excreted medications based on latest CrCl (see below)**
* **Treat complications (AEIOU);** consult renal/ICU x RRT if refractory to medical treatment
* If no improvement in Cr
    * Monitor CBC d/c, L/RFT, Ca/PO4, VBG
    * Consider USG / NCCT / consult URO x obstructive cause
    * Consider consult MED x RRT plan and workup for intrinsic renal disease (Bld x ANCA, ANA, anti-dsDNA, anti-gbm, C3/4, HBsAg, anti-HCV, SPE, Ig pattern, FLC; UPE; *but leave these for MO to order since interpretation may be equivocal!*)

> **If eGFR < 45** not on dialysis but need contrast CT:
> * Plain CT or USG if doesn't lower diagnostic yield; **proceed with contrast CT** if clinically indicated
> * **Volume expansion with NS** (alternatives: IV NaHCO3 or IV acetylcysteine but no evidence)

> **If eGFR < 30** not on dialysis but need contrast MRI: **should proceed with group II gadolinium-based contrast** which has very low risk of nephrogenic systemic fibrosis

## Drugs that require dosage adjustment in renal failure

### 1. Antihypertensives/cardiac medications
* **RAAS antagonists (ACE-Is, ARBs, aldosterone antagonists, direct renin inhibitors)**
    * Avoid in people with suspected functional renal artery stenosis
    * Start at lower dose in people with eGFR < 45 ml/min/1.73 m2
    * Assess eGFR and measure serum potassium within 1 week of starting or following any dose escalation
    * Temporarily suspend during intercurrent illness, planned IV radiocontrast administration, bowel preparation prior to colonoscopy, or prior to major surgery
    * Do not routinely discontinue in people with eGFR < 30 ml/min/1.73 m2 as they remain nephroprotective
* **Beta-blockers**
    * Reduce dose by 50% in people with eGFR < 30 ml/min/1.73 m2
* **Digoxin**
    * Reduce dose based on plasma concentrations

### 2. Analgesics
* **NSAIDs**
    * Avoid in people with eGFR < 30 ml/min/1.73 m2
    * Prolonged therapy is not recommended in people with eGFR < 60 ml/min/1.73 m2
    * Should not be used in people taking lithium
    * Avoid in people taking RAAS blocking agents
* **Opioids**
    * Reduce dose when eGFR < 60 ml/min/1.73 m2
    * Use with caution in people with eGFR < 15 ml/min/1.73 m2

### 3. Antimicrobials
* **Penicillin**
    * Risk of crystalluria when eGFR < 15 ml/min/1.73 m2 with high doses
    * Neurotoxicity with benzylpenicillin when eGFR < 15 ml/min/1.73 m2 with high doses (maximum 6 g/day)
* **Aminoglycosides**
    * Reduce dose and/or increase dosage interval when eGFR < 60 ml/min/1.73 m2
    * Monitor serum levels (trough and peak)
    * Avoid concomitant ototoxic agents such as furosemide
* **Macrolides**
    * Reduce dose by 50% when eGFR < 30 ml/min/1.73 m2
* **Fluoroquinolones**
    * Reduce dose by 50% when eGFR < 15 ml/min/1.73 m2
* **Tetracyclines**
    * Reduce dose when eGFR < 45 ml/min/1.73 m2; can exacerbate uremia
* **Antifungals**
    * Avoid amphotericin unless no alternative when eGFR < 60 ml/min/1.73 m2
    * Reduce maintenance dose of fluconazole by 50% when eGFR < 45 ml/min/1.73 m2
    * Reduce dose of flucytosine when eGFR < 60 ml/min/1.73 m2

### 4. Hypoglycemics
* **Sulfonylureas**
    * Avoid agents that are mainly renally excreted (e.g., glyburide/ glibenclamide)
    * Other agents that are mainly metabolized in the liver may need reduced dose when eGFR < 30 ml/min/1.73 m2 (e.g., gliclazide, gliquidone)
* **Insulin**
    * Partly renally excreted and may need reduced dose when eGFR < 30 ml/min/1.73 m2
* **Metformin**
    * Suggest avoid when eGFR < 30 ml/min/1.73 m2, but consider risk-benefit if eGFR is stable
    * Review use when eGFR < 45 ml/min/1.73 m2
    * Probably safe when eGFR >= 45 ml/min/1.73 m2
    * Suspend in people who become acutely unwell

### 5. Lipid-lowering
* **Statins**
    * No increase in toxicity for simvastatin dosed at 20 mg per day or simvastatin 20 mg /ezetimibe 10 mg combinations per day in people with eGFR < 30 ml/min/1.73 m2 or on dialysis
    * Other trials of statins in people with eGFR < 15 ml/min/1.73 m2 or on dialysis also showed no excess toxicity
* **Fenofibrate**
    * Increases SCr by approximately 0.13 mg/dl (12 umol/l)

### 6. Chemotherapeutic
* **Cisplatin**
    * Reduce dose when eGFR < 60 ml/min/1.73 m2
    * Avoid when eGFR < 30 ml/min/1.73 m2
* **Melphalan**
    * Reduce dose when eGFR < 60 ml/min/1.73 m2
* **Methotrexate**
    * Reduce dose when eGFR < 60 ml/min/1.73 m2
    * Avoid if possible when eGFR < 15 ml/min/1.73 m2

### 7. Anticoagulants
* **Low-molecular-weight heparins**
    * Halve the dose when eGFR < 30 ml/min/1.73 m2
    * Consider switch to conventional heparin or alternatively monitor plasma anti-factor Xa in those at high risk for bleeding
* **Warfarin**
    * Increased risk of bleeding when eGFR < 30 ml/min/1.73 m2
    * Use lower doses and monitor closely when eGFR < 30 ml/min/1.73 m2

### 8. Miscellaneous
* **Lithium**
    * Nephrotoxic and may cause renal tubular dysfunction with prolonged use even at therapeutic levels
    * Monitor eGFR, electrolytes, and lithium levels 6 monthly or more frequently if the dose changes or the patient is acutely unwell
    * Avoid using concomitant NSAIDs
    * Maintain hydration during intercurrent illness
    * Risk-benefit of drug in specific situation must be weighed
