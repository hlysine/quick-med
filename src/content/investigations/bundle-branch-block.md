# Bundle Branch Block

## Right bundle branch block

![RBBB ECG](/assets/rbbb-ecg.png)

![RBBB V1/V6 morphology](/assets/rbbb-v1-v6-morphology.png)

* QRS duration >120ms
* rsR' and inverted T wave in V1
* Deep and wide S in lateral leads (I, aVL, V6)
* Right ventricle activation is delayed because of the block, manifests as delayed R' in V1 and delayed deep S in V6
* Slowly progressive degenerative disease
* Not a risk indicator for cardiac disease unless already co-existing

## Left bundle branch block

![LBBB ECG](/assets/lbbb-ecg.png)

* QRS duration >120ms
* No Q wave in lateral leads (I, aVL, V6)
    * No initial activation of septum from left bundle
    * Existence of Q wave rules out LBBB
* Monophasic QRS in lateral leads (I, aVL, V6)
    * With notching if LBB completely non-functional
* QS or rS in V1 and V2
* **Comes with ST elevation and downsloping ST**
    * **Does not indicate MI**
* Slowly progressive degenerative disease
* Associated with development of CVD

## Left anterior fascicular block

![LAFB ECG](/assets/lafb-ecg.png)

![LAFB R wave peak time in aVL](/assets/lafb-r-peak-time.png)

* QRS duration longer than normal but <120ms
* QRS axis -45 to -90 (LAD)
* R takes >45ms to peak in aVL
* Benign consequence of aging unless associated with AF/heart failure

## Left posterior fascicular block

![LPFB ECG](/assets/lpfb-ecg.png)

* QRS duration longer than normal but <120ms
* QRS axis 90 to 180 (RAD)
* rS in lateral leads (I, aVL)
* qR in inferior leads (II, III, aVF)
* Often co-exists with RBBB
